# Glossary Q-T

| Term | Definition | Related Article |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| R-value | Thermal resistance of a material or assembly, measured in m2K/W. The inverse of U-value for a single layer. Higher R-value means better insulation. R = thickness / thermal conductivity (lambda). | u-values |
| Rafter | An inclined structural member forming the slope of a pitched roof, spanning from the ridge to the eave. In modular construction, rafters are typically prefabricated as part of roof cassettes. | timber-frame |
| RE2020 (FR) | Reglementation Environnementale 2020 -- French building regulation replacing RT2012. Introduces carbon limits alongside energy performance, pushing towards bio-sourced materials like timber. | energy-performance |
| Reinforced concrete | Concrete with embedded steel reinforcement bars (rebar) to resist tensile forces. Used for foundations, slabs, and retaining walls in modular construction. | foundations |
| Renewable energy | Energy from naturally replenishing sources: solar (PV/thermal), wind, geothermal, biomass, and heat pumps (aerothermal). EU and national codes mandate minimum renewable shares in new buildings. | renewable-energy |
| Ridge | The highest horizontal line of a pitched roof where two sloping surfaces meet. Ridge height is often regulated by local zoning plans (PLU, PUG, bestemmingsplan). | |
| Screw pile (Helicoidal pile) | A steel foundation pile with helical plates, screwed into the ground mechanically. Minimal soil disturbance, no concrete needed, and fully removable -- ideal for modular and temporary buildings. | foundations |
| Seismic zone | Geographic area classified by expected earthquake ground acceleration. Romania uses ag values from 0.10g to 0.40g (P100); the Netherlands has negligible seismicity except Groningen. | seismic-p100 |
| SCIA (IT) | Segnalazione Certificata di Inizio Attivita -- Italian certified notification for starting certain building works (minor renovations) without a full building permit. | |
| SIP panel (Structural Insulated Panel) | A sandwich panel of rigid insulation (EPS or PUR) bonded between two structural boards (OSB or cement). High thermal performance and rapid assembly; used for walls, roofs, and floors. | sip-panels |
| Slab foundation | A flat concrete pad poured directly on prepared ground, acting as both foundation and ground floor. Suitable for stable soils; common in single-storey modular homes. | foundations |
| Snow load | The design load imposed by accumulated snow on a roof, defined per Eurocode 1 (EN 1991-1-3) based on geographic zone and altitude. Romania ranges from 1.0 to 3.5 kN/m2. | eurocodes-overview |
| Solar gain | Heat gained through glazed surfaces from solar radiation. Desirable in winter (passive heating) but must be controlled in summer to prevent overheating (g-value of glazing, shading). | solar-orientation |
| Strip foundation | A continuous concrete footing under load-bearing walls, typically 40-80 cm wide and 60-120 cm deep. The most common foundation type for low-rise residential buildings in Europe. | foundations |
| Structural engineer (Inginer de structuri / Constructeur) | Professional responsible for designing the load-bearing structure and ensuring compliance with Eurocodes and national structural codes (P100, NEN, NTC). | eurocodes-overview |
| Sustainability | In construction: minimizing environmental impact through material selection, energy efficiency, water conservation, waste reduction, and design for disassembly (circular economy). | circular-economy |
| Thermal bridge | A localised area in the building envelope with significantly higher heat transfer than the surrounding elements. Common at junctions (wall-floor, wall-roof, window frames). Causes energy loss and condensation risk. | u-values |
| Thermal mass | The ability of a material to absorb, store, and release heat. High thermal mass (concrete, brick) stabilizes indoor temperatures; timber-frame homes rely instead on insulation and mechanical systems. | passive-house |
| Timber frame | A structural system using engineered or sawn timber members (studs, beams, joists) to form walls, floors, and roofs. The primary construction method for ModulCA modules. | timber-frame |
| U-value | Thermal transmittance of a building element, measured in W/m2K. Lower values indicate better insulation. Typical wall targets: 0.15-0.20 W/m2K (Passive House) to 0.28 W/m2K (standard code). | u-values |
