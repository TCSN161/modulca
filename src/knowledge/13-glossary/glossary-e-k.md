# Glossary E-K

| Term | Definition | Related Article |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| Eave | The lower edge of a roof that overhangs the wall face. Eave details are critical for weather protection and ventilation of the roof structure. | wall-buildup |
| EPBD (EU) | Energy Performance of Buildings Directive -- EU legislation requiring member states to set minimum energy performance requirements and mandate energy certificates. Recast in 2024. | eu-energy-performance-epbd |
| EPB (BE) | Energieprestatie en Binnenklimaat -- Belgian energy performance and indoor climate regulation. Sets requirements for insulation, airtightness, ventilation, and overheating. | energy-performance |
| Eurocode | Suite of European structural design standards (EN 1990-1999) covering actions on structures, concrete, steel, timber, masonry, geotechnical, and seismic design. | eurocodes-overview |
| Fire resistance (REI rating) | European classification of building elements: R = load-bearing capacity, E = integrity (no flame passage), I = insulation (temperature limit). Expressed in minutes, e.g., REI 60. | fire-safety |
| Flexwonen (NL) | Dutch concept of flexible/temporary housing, typically modular units placed on temporary sites for 10-15 years. Subject to lighter permit requirements under the Omgevingswet. | modular-prefab-regulations-nl |
| Foundation | The structural base that transfers building loads to the ground. Types include strip, slab, screw pile, and deep foundations, chosen based on soil conditions and building weight. | foundations |
| GEG (DE) | Gebaudeenergiegesetz -- Germany's Building Energy Act (2024), merging previous EnEV and EEWarmeG into a single law governing energy efficiency and heating systems. | energy-performance |
| Geotechnical survey | Site investigation to determine soil type, bearing capacity, groundwater level, and foundation requirements. Mandatory in seismic zones and for buildings over two storeys in Romania. | foundations |
| Green roof | A roof partially or fully covered with vegetation over a waterproof membrane. Provides insulation, stormwater retention, and biodiversity benefits. Extensive types have 5-15 cm substrate. | landscaping |
| Greywater | Wastewater from sinks, showers, and washing machines (excluding toilet water). Can be recycled for irrigation or toilet flushing to reduce potable water consumption. | water-management |
| Hardiness zone | Climate classification (USDA or EU equivalent) indicating minimum winter temperatures. Relevant for plant selection in landscape design and outdoor living areas. | landscaping |
| HVAC | Heating, Ventilation, and Air Conditioning -- the mechanical systems controlling indoor temperature, humidity, and air quality. Sizing depends on building envelope performance. | hvac-sizing |
| Insulation (types) | Materials that reduce heat transfer: mineral wool (stone/glass), EPS, XPS, PIR/PUR, wood fibre, cellulose, and aerogel. Selection depends on thermal conductivity (lambda), fire class, and moisture behaviour. | insulation-materials |
| Kadaster (NL) | Dutch land registry and mapping authority. Maintains records of property boundaries, ownership, and mortgage registrations. Must be consulted before any building application. | kadaster-land-registry |
| Kwaliteitsborger (NL) | Independent quality assurance inspector required under the Dutch Wkb (Wet kwaliteitsborging) for certain building categories. Replaces municipal building inspection for new-build homes. | building-permit-process-nl |
