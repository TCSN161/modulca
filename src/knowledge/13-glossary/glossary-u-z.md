# Glossary U-Z

| Term | Definition | Related Article |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| Underfloor heating (Incalzire in pardoseala / Vloerverwarming) | A radiant heating system with pipes or cables embedded in the floor screed. Low-temperature operation (30-40 C) makes it ideal for heat pump systems and well-insulated modular homes. | hvac-sizing |
| Vapor barrier (Bariera de vapori / Dampscherm) | A membrane with very low water vapor permeability (Sd > 100 m) installed on the warm side of insulation to prevent moisture from migrating into the wall assembly and causing condensation. | wall-buildup |
| Ventilation System C | Mechanical exhaust ventilation with natural air supply through adjustable trickle vents in windows. Common in Belgian and Dutch residential buildings; simpler but less energy-efficient than System D. | hvac-sizing |
| Ventilation System D | Balanced mechanical ventilation with supply and exhaust fans plus heat recovery (HRV/ERV). Recovers 75-95% of heat from exhaust air; required for Passive House and most NZEB designs. | hvac-sizing |
| Vernacular architecture | Traditional building styles that evolved from local materials, climate, and cultural practices. Studying vernacular forms informs contextually appropriate modular design. | |
| VHR (Ventilatie met Warmteterugwinning) | Dutch term for ventilation with heat recovery (System D). The heat exchanger transfers warmth from stale exhaust air to fresh incoming air, reducing heating demand significantly. | hvac-sizing |
| Vinyl (PVC) flooring | Synthetic flooring material available as LVT (Luxury Vinyl Tile) or LVP (plank). Waterproof and low-maintenance; common in modular bathrooms and kitchens. Consider VOC emissions when specifying. | |
| Waterschap (NL) | Dutch regional water authority (waterboard) responsible for water level management, flood protection, and wastewater treatment. May impose requirements on drainage and foundation design near waterways. | water-board-regulations |
| Wind load | The force exerted on a building by wind pressure and suction, calculated per Eurocode 1 (EN 1991-1-4) based on geographic wind zone, terrain category, and building height. Critical for lightweight modular structures. | eurocodes-overview |
| XPS insulation (Extruded Polystyrene) | Closed-cell rigid foam insulation with low water absorption. Lambda typically 0.033-0.036 W/mK. Used below grade (foundations, inverted roofs) where moisture resistance is essential. | insulation-materials |
| Zero-energy building (ZEB) | A building that produces as much renewable energy as it consumes annually. Goes beyond NZEB by achieving net-zero energy balance, typically through extensive PV and heat pump systems. | nzeb-standards |
| Zoning (Urbanism / Planologie) | The regulatory framework dividing land into zones with permitted uses, building parameters (height, density, setbacks), and conditions. Governed by PUG/PUZ in Romania, bestemmingsplan/omgevingsplan in NL. | urban-planning-pug-puz |
