# Kellert — Biophilic Design: The Theory, Science, and Practice

## Overview

**Biophilic design** is the practice of connecting building occupants to nature through direct and indirect natural elements. *Biophilic Design* (2008) by **Stephen R. Kellert**, Judith Heerwagen, and Martin Mador is the foundational text, backed by decades of environmental psychology research.

The core finding: humans are **biologically wired** to respond positively to nature. Buildings that incorporate natural elements measurably improve health, productivity, and happiness.

## The 14 Patterns of Biophilic Design

Terrapin Bright Green (building on Kellert's work) codified **14 patterns** that architects can apply:

### Nature in the Space (Direct Experience)
| # | Pattern | Residential Application |
|---|---------|----------------------|
| 1 | **Visual Connection** | Large windows facing gardens |
| 2 | **Non-Visual Connection** | Sound of water, birdsong, fresh air |
| 3 | **Non-Rhythmic Stimuli** | Clouds, leaves, firelight — unpredictable |
| 4 | **Thermal Variability** | Warm sunlit spot vs. cool shaded nook |
| 5 | **Presence of Water** | Fountain, pond, rain chain |
| 6 | **Dynamic Light** | Dappled sunlight through trees |
| 7 | **Nature Connection** | Garden, green roof, indoor plants |

### Natural Analogues (Indirect Reference)
| # | Pattern | Residential Application |
|---|---------|----------------------|
| 8 | **Biomorphic Forms** | Organic curves, branching patterns |
| 9 | **Material Connection** | Wood, stone, natural fibers |
| 10 | **Complexity & Order** | Fractal patterns (wood grain, leaves) |

### Nature of the Space (Spatial Experience)
| # | Pattern | Residential Application |
|---|---------|----------------------|
| 11 | **Prospect** | Views from elevated positions |
| 12 | **Refuge** | Cozy nooks, window seats, alcoves |
| 13 | **Mystery** | Partially obscured views that invite exploration |
| 14 | **Risk/Peril** | Balconies, mezzanines, glass floors |

## Measurable Benefits

Research cited by Kellert shows:

| Metric | Improvement with Biophilic Design |
|--------|----------------------------------|
| Stress reduction | 15–25% lower cortisol |
| Productivity | 6–12% increase |
| Healing time (hospitals) | 8.5% faster recovery |
| Property value | 4–8% premium |
| Absenteeism | 10–15% reduction |
| Sleep quality | Improved with natural light cycles |

## ModulCA's Biophilic Integration

ModulCA embeds biophilic principles at every scale:

1. **Module level**: Large windows (Pattern 1), natural wood CLT exposed (Pattern 9)
2. **Layout level**: Courtyard configurations (Pattern 7), indoor-outdoor flow
3. **Material level**: Timber structure, natural finishes, stone accents
4. **Landscape level**: Garden design, sustainable drainage, native planting
5. **Quiz integration**: Biophilic score determines window-to-wall ratio and material recommendations

The Architectural Profile Quiz includes a **biophilic affinity score** (1–5) that directly influences design recommendations — more biophilic profiles get larger windows, exposed timber, and courtyard layouts.
