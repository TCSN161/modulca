# Ryan Smith — Prefab Architecture

## Overview

*Prefab Architecture: A Guide to Modular Design and Construction* (2010) by **Ryan E. Smith** is the most comprehensive academic treatment of prefabricated and modular construction. It covers the full spectrum from panelized systems to volumetric modules — exactly what ModulCA builds.

## Key Topics

### The Five Types of Prefabrication

Smith classifies prefab into a hierarchy of increasing factory completion:

| Type | Factory % | Description | ModulCA Relevance |
|------|-----------|-------------|------------------|
| **1. Components** | 10–20% | Trusses, beams, precast elements | Foundation elements |
| **2. Panelized** | 20–40% | Wall/floor/roof panels, assembled on-site | SIP option |
| **3. Modular** | 40–70% | 3D volumetric units, stacked/joined | ★ **Core ModulCA model** |
| **4. Sectional** | 70–85% | Large sections transported whole | Large module variants |
| **5. Complete** | 85–95% | Fully finished, placed on foundation | Premium finish level |

ModulCA operates at **Type 3–4**: 3×3m volumetric modules manufactured off-site with ~60–80% completion, transported, and assembled on the client's land.

### Design for Manufacturing & Assembly (DfMA)

Smith emphasizes designing for the factory, not just the building:
- **Standardized dimensions** → ModulCA's 3×3m grid
- **Transport constraints** → Module max width 3.5m (road legal EU)
- **Lifting points** → Crane-ready connection details
- **Tolerance management** → Factory precision ±2mm vs. site ±10mm
- **Connection design** → How modules join structurally

### Quality vs. Traditional Construction

| Metric | Traditional | Modular (per Smith) | ModulCA Target |
|--------|------------|-------------------|----------------|
| Construction waste | 15–25% | 2–5% | <5% |
| Schedule | 12–18 months | 4–8 months | 3–6 months |
| Defects at handover | 5–10 per unit | 1–2 per unit | <2 |
| Weather delays | 20–30% of schedule | 5% (factory only) | <5% |
| Cost variance | ±15–25% | ±5–10% | ±5% |

## Why It Matters for ModulCA

This book validates the entire modular construction model:
1. **Quality**: Factory conditions produce better buildings than site conditions
2. **Speed**: Parallel manufacturing + site prep cuts total time 40–60%
3. **Sustainability**: Less waste, optimized material use, recyclable at end-of-life
4. **Cost predictability**: Fixed factory costs vs. variable site costs
5. **Scalability**: Once you design a module, you can produce thousands

ModulCA's pricing model (€800–1,200/m²) and timeline estimates (3–6 months) directly reference the benchmarks Smith established.
