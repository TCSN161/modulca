# Interior Space Planning

## Overview
Space planning is the art of organizing interior spaces for optimal function, comfort, and flow. In modular homes, space planning determines which rooms go in which modules, how modules connect, and how the home feels to live in. Good space planning makes a compact home feel spacious.

## Planning Principles

| Principle | Description | Application |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Zoning | Group related functions together | Kitchen + dining adjacent, bedrooms clustered |
| Circulation | Clear paths through the home | No dead-ends, logical flow |
| Public → Private gradient | Social spaces at entry, private at back | Living near entrance, bedrooms furthest |
| Daylight access | Habitable rooms get windows | Bathrooms and storage can be interior |
| Acoustic separation | Noisy rooms away from quiet ones | Kitchen away from bedrooms |
| Views | Best views for most-used rooms | Living room gets the garden view |

## Room Adjacency Matrix

| Room | Must Be Near | Should Be Near | Keep Away From |
|------|-------------|---------------|----------------|
| Kitchen | Dining | Living, utility | Bedrooms |
| Dining | Kitchen | Living, terrace | — |
| Living | Dining, terrace | Entrance | — |
| Master bedroom | Master bath | — | Kitchen, entrance |
| Children's bedrooms | Children's bath | Play area | Kitchen |
| Home office | — | Entrance (separate access) | Living (noise) |
| Bathroom | Bedrooms | — | Kitchen (plumbing ok) |
| Utility/laundry | Kitchen | Bathroom | Living |
| Entrance | Living | Home office | Bedrooms |

## Open Plan vs. Closed Rooms

| Layout | Pros | Cons | Best For |
|--------|------|------|----------|
| Open plan | Spacious feel, social, light | Noise, cooking smells, clutter visible | Living + dining + kitchen |
| Semi-open | Best of both, flexible | Needs careful design | Kitchen visible but separable |
| Closed rooms | Privacy, acoustic, contained | Can feel small | Bedrooms, office, bathroom |

## ModulCA Space Planning

| Home Size | Modules | Suggested Layout |
|-----------|---------|------------------|
| Studio (1 person) | 3 modules | Kitchen-living (2) + bathroom (1) |
| 1-bedroom (couple) | 4-5 modules | Living-kitchen (2) + bedroom (1) + bathroom (1) + entrance/utility (1) |
| 2-bedroom (family) | 6-7 modules | Living-kitchen (2-3) + 2 bedrooms (2) + bathroom (1-2) + utility (1) |
| 3-bedroom (large family) | 8-10 modules | Living-kitchen (3) + 3 bedrooms (3) + 2 bathrooms (2) + utility (1-2) |

## ModulCA Application
- **Module = room**: Each module is naturally one room — simplifies space planning
- **Open connections**: Remove shared wall between modules for open-plan
- **L-shape trick**: L configuration naturally separates public (living) from private (bedroom wing)
- **Entrance module**: Dedicated 1-module entrance with coat storage and shoe area
- **Service core**: Stack bathroom + kitchen modules for efficient plumbing runs
