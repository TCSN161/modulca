# Color Theory for Interiors

## Overview
Color profoundly affects how spaces feel — warmer or cooler, larger or smaller, calming or energizing. Understanding color basics helps create interiors that support the intended mood and function of each room.

## Color Psychology in Rooms

| Color Family | Psychological Effect | Best Room Application |
|-------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| White / off-white | Spacious, clean, calm | Any room, small spaces especially |
| Warm grey | Sophisticated, grounding | Living room, bedroom |
| Cool grey | Modern, professional | Home office, bathroom |
| Beige / cream | Warm, inviting, traditional | Living room, bedroom |
| Sage green | Calming, nature-connected | Bedroom, bathroom, office |
| Dusty blue | Serene, restful | Bedroom, bathroom |
| Warm terracotta | Earthy, cozy, Mediterranean | Kitchen, dining, accent wall |
| Deep navy | Dramatic, cocooning | Accent wall, study |
| Black | Bold, grounding (small doses) | Accents, frames, fixtures |
| Natural wood tones | Warm, biophilic, timeless | Floors, ceilings, furniture |

## Room-by-Room Palette Guide

| Room | Base (walls 60%) | Secondary (30%) | Accent (10%) |
|------|-----------------|-----------------|--------------|
| Living room | Warm white or light grey | Wood + fabric (sofa, rug) | One bold color (cushions, art) |
| Kitchen | White or light color | Wood cabinetry | Metal fixtures, tile backsplash |
| Master bedroom | Soft neutral or muted color | White bedding + wood | Textile accent (headboard, throw) |
| Bathroom | White or light tile | Wood vanity or shelving | Black or brass fixtures |
| Home office | Cool light color (focus) | Wood desk + shelving | Green plant accents |
| Children's room | Warm neutral base | Colorful textiles + art | Changeable accents (grows with child) |

## 60-30-10 Rule

| Proportion | Role | Typical Element |
|-----------|------|-----------------|
| 60% | Dominant / base | Walls, large surfaces |
| 30% | Secondary | Furniture, rugs, curtains |
| 10% | Accent | Cushions, art, decorative objects |

## Color and Space Perception

| Effect Desired | Color Strategy |
|---------------|----------------|
| Room feels larger | Light walls, white ceiling, similar tones throughout |
| Room feels cozier | Darker walls, warm tones, color on ceiling |
| Ceiling feels higher | White ceiling, lighter than walls |
| Ceiling feels lower | Darker ceiling than walls |
| Room feels longer | Dark color on short end walls |
| Room feels warmer | Warm undertones (yellow, red, orange base) |
| Room feels cooler | Cool undertones (blue, green, grey base) |

## ModulCA Application
- **White module interiors**: Factory-painted white base — clients personalize with paint and furnishings
- **Wood as the "second color"**: Exposed CLT ceiling or accent wall provides the 30% secondary color naturally
- **Per-module palette**: Each module can have its own accent — bedroom calm, kitchen energizing
- **Natural material palette**: Wood + white + one natural accent (stone, terracotta) — timeless ModulCA palette
