# Traditional Romanian Profile

## Style Summary

The Traditional Romanian home reinterprets vernacular architecture through the lens of modular construction. A pitched roof with generous eaves anchors the home in the local landscape. Warm wood dominates exteriors and interiors, with carved or patterned details referencing regional motifs without literal reproduction. The covered porch (prispа) serves as a transitional space between inside and outside, central to Romanian domestic life. Rooms are more defined than in open-plan approaches, creating intimacy and warmth.

## Recommended Materials

- **Structure:** CLT panels with timber frame roof, traditional proportions
- **Exterior cladding:** Natural timber boards (horizontal or vertical), lime-washed render for contrast
- **Windows:** Timber frames, medium format, with functional shutters in painted wood
- **Interior walls:** Lime plaster in warm white, timber wainscoting, ceramic stove niche
- **Flooring:** Wide oak or fir planks, hand-scraped finish, ceramic tile in kitchen and bath
- **Roof:** Pitched 35-45 degrees, clay tile or metal sheet in traditional dark tones

## Suggested Module Layout

A typical Traditional Romanian home uses **5-7 modules** (each 3x3m = 9m2):

| Module | Function | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| 1 | Covered porch (prispa) | Entry transition, seating, seasonal use |
| 2-3 | Living room + kitchen | Connected but defined, hearth as anchor |
| 4 | Master bedroom | Warm textiles, garden-facing window |
| 5 | Second bedroom | Family or guest use |
| 6 | Bathroom + utility | Practical layout, ceramic details |
| 7 (optional) | Pantry + storage (camara) | Traditional larder, root cellar concept |

## Room Priorities

1. Covered porch as welcoming threshold
2. Kitchen as the warm core of the home
3. Living room with heating focal point (soba/fireplace)
4. Defined bedrooms with privacy
5. Generous storage and pantry space

## Sustainability Level

**Moderate.** Vernacular building wisdom inherently responds to climate: thick walls for thermal mass, generous eaves for sun control, natural ventilation through window placement. Modern upgrades: 200mm+ wood fiber insulation, triple-glazed windows behind traditional shutters, optional solar thermal for hot water. Clay tile roofs last 50+ years with minimal maintenance.

## Budget Indicator

**Mid-Low range: 1000-1400 EUR/m2.** Standard timber materials and traditional forms are cost-effective. Savings from familiar construction techniques and locally available materials. Carved details and shutters add modest cost. Total for a 5-module home (45m2 usable): approximately 45,000-63,000 EUR structure and finishes.
