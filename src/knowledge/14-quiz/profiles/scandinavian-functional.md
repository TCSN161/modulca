# Scandinavian Functional Profile

## Style Summary

The Scandinavian Functional home prioritizes light, simplicity, and everyday comfort. White and light wood surfaces maximize natural light in the interior. Every piece of furniture and every detail earns its place through utility. The concept of hygge -- cozy warmth and well-being -- permeates the design. Rooms are modest in size but impeccably proportioned, with thoughtful storage keeping surfaces clear.

## Recommended Materials

- **Structure:** CLT panels or timber frame with prefabricated wall cassettes
- **Exterior cladding:** Painted timber boards (white, pale gray, or muted blue), smooth render
- **Windows:** Wood frames painted white, triple-glazed, generous but well-proportioned
- **Interior walls:** White-painted plaster, birch or pine plywood accent walls
- **Flooring:** Light ash or birch engineered planks, white-oiled finish
- **Roof:** Gently pitched gable, metal standing seam in matte dark gray

## Suggested Module Layout

A typical Scandinavian Functional home uses **4-6 modules** (each 3x3m = 9m2):

| Module | Function | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Open living + kitchen | White kitchen, dining table as centerpiece |
| 3 | Master bedroom | Built-in wardrobe wall, bedside reading nooks |
| 4 | Bathroom + laundry | Efficient layout, washer/dryer integrated |
| 5 | Entry hallway + storage | Coat hooks, shoe bench, seasonal storage |
| 6 (optional) | Children's room or study | Compact, multifunctional with loft bed |

## Room Priorities

1. Kitchen/dining as the social center
2. Entry with proper coat and shoe storage (climate-driven need)
3. Master bedroom - serene, uncluttered, good blackout capability
4. Compact but complete bathroom
5. Flexible room for study or children

## Sustainability Level

**Moderate to High.** Scandinavian design inherently favors efficiency and durability. Well-insulated envelope is essential for the concept. Recommended: 250mm+ insulation, MVHR with heat recovery above 85%, LED lighting throughout. Simple material palette reduces waste. Preference for FSC-certified Nordic timber and low-VOC finishes.

## Budget Indicator

**Mid range: 1200-1600 EUR/m2.** The restrained palette and standard geometries keep costs contained. Quality is in the details and proportions rather than expensive materials. Total for a 4-module home (36m2 usable): approximately 43,000-58,000 EUR structure and finishes.
