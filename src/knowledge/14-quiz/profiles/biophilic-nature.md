# Biophilic Nature Profile

## Style Summary

The Biophilic Nature home is designed around maximum connection to the living world. Planted walls, water features, and abundant daylight dissolve the barrier between architecture and ecosystem. Large terraces and garden rooms extend living space into nature. Every module incorporates at least one direct biophilic element -- a planted wall, a skylight, a view to greenery, or natural ventilation. The home does not just sit in nature; it participates in it.

## Recommended Materials

- **Structure:** CLT panels for carbon storage, living roof-capable structural capacity
- **Exterior cladding:** Untreated timber that weathers naturally, planted facade sections, stone base
- **Windows:** Floor-to-ceiling timber-aluminum frames, operable for natural ventilation, insect screens
- **Interior walls:** Clay plaster, cork panels, living plant wall systems with integrated irrigation
- **Flooring:** Rammed earth tiles, natural stone, reclaimed timber planks
- **Roof:** Living green roof (extensive or intensive), integrated rainwater collection

## Suggested Module Layout

A typical Biophilic Nature home uses **6-9 modules** (each 3x3m = 9m2):

| Module | Function | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Living room | Double-height planted wall, skylight, garden view |
| 3 | Kitchen + dining | Herb garden window, composting station access |
| 4 | Master bedroom | Canopy of greenery outside, operable roof window |
| 5 | Bathroom | Natural stone, rain shower, courtyard planting |
| 6-7 | Covered terrace + garden room | Retractable glazing, planted pergola |
| 8 | Second bedroom or studio | Green roof visible from interior |
| 9 (optional) | Greenhouse or conservatory | Food production, tropical plants, winter garden |

## Room Priorities

1. Garden room / covered terrace (largest outdoor-indoor zone)
2. Living space with vertical garden and maximum daylight
3. Kitchen connected to edible garden
4. Bedroom as a nature immersion retreat
5. Bathroom with natural materials and courtyard

## Sustainability Level

**Very High.** This is the most sustainable profile by design intent. Full specification: intensive green roof (150mm+ substrate), rainwater harvesting (5000L+ tank), solar PV array, MVHR with earth tube pre-conditioning, composting system, biodiversity corridor in landscape. Target: near-zero operational carbon. CLT structure provides carbon sequestration. Natural materials throughout minimize embodied carbon.

## Budget Indicator

**High range: 1800-2400 EUR/m2.** Green systems (living walls, green roof, rainwater harvesting, greenhouse) add significant cost above standard construction. However, reduced operational costs (energy, water) and increased property value provide strong long-term return. Total for a 7-module home (63m2 usable): approximately 113,000-151,000 EUR structure and finishes.
