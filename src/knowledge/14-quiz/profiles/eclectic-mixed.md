# Eclectic Mixed Profile

## Style Summary

The Eclectic Mixed home rejects a single style label in favor of personal expression. It borrows freely from multiple traditions -- a modern shell with traditional accents, industrial details alongside organic textures, bold colors next to natural tones. The unifying thread is the inhabitant's personality rather than any design rulebook. This profile works best for people with strong personal taste who want their home to feel uniquely theirs, not drawn from a catalog.

## Recommended Materials

- **Structure:** CLT panels or timber frame (flexible base for varied finishes)
- **Exterior cladding:** Mix of 2-3 materials -- timber + render + metal accents, or stone + colored plaster
- **Windows:** Varied formats -- some large, some small and positioned for effect, mixed frame materials
- **Interior walls:** Feature walls in contrasting materials (tile mosaic, patterned wallpaper, painted murals, reclaimed wood)
- **Flooring:** Mixed by zone -- terrazzo in entry, timber in living areas, patterned tile in kitchen/bath
- **Roof:** Form follows function -- pitched, flat, or combination based on module arrangement

## Suggested Module Layout

A typical Eclectic Mixed home uses **5-8 modules** (each 3x3m = 9m2):

| Module | Function | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Living + dining | Gallery wall, mixed furniture, statement lighting |
| 3 | Kitchen | Bold tile backsplash, open shelving, color accents |
| 4 | Master bedroom | Textile-rich, layered, personal sanctuary |
| 5 | Bathroom | Patterned tile, vintage-style fixtures, brass details |
| 6 | Creative room / studio | Art, music, crafts, personal hobby space |
| 7 (optional) | Entry + display | Gallery corridor, collected objects, travel souvenirs |
| 8 (optional) | Guest room or library | Maximalist bookshelves, daybed, reading light |

## Room Priorities

1. Living space as personal gallery and expression
2. Kitchen with bold character and color
3. Creative/hobby space for personal pursuits
4. Bedroom as a layered, intimate retreat
5. Bathroom as a design moment (patterned tiles, statement mirror)

## Sustainability Level

**Variable.** Depends on specific material choices. The eclectic approach can incorporate reclaimed and vintage materials naturally, which is inherently sustainable. Recommendations: prioritize reclaimed wood and vintage fixtures where possible, use low-VOC paints for bold colors, ensure the structural shell meets high insulation standards (200mm+) regardless of decorative choices. The variety of materials may increase construction waste -- plan carefully and reuse offcuts.

## Budget Indicator

**Variable range: 1200-2000+ EUR/m2.** The eclectic approach allows maximum flexibility. Mixing high-end statement pieces with budget-friendly backgrounds keeps costs controllable. Reclaimed and vintage materials can reduce costs while adding character. The risk is scope creep -- each unique detail adds design and labor time. Total for a 6-module home (54m2 usable): approximately 65,000-108,000 EUR structure and finishes depending on selections.
