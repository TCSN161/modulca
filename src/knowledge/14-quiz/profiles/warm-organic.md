# Warm Organic Profile

## Style Summary

The Warm Organic home embraces natural forms, earth tones, and a deep connection to the landscape. Curved edges soften modular geometry where possible. Timber is the dominant material both inside and out, complemented by clay plaster, stone, and linen textiles. The atmosphere is calm, grounded, and nurturing. Rooms feel like natural extensions of the terrain.

## Recommended Materials

- **Structure:** CLT panels with exposed timber ceilings
- **Exterior cladding:** Larch or thermowood vertical boards, natural oil finish
- **Windows:** Timber frames, triple-glazed, medium to large format
- **Interior walls:** Clay plaster in warm earth tones, exposed timber beams
- **Flooring:** Solid oak or ash planks, oiled finish, natural stone in wet areas
- **Roof:** Pitched or butterfly form, standing seam copper or wooden shingles

## Suggested Module Layout

A typical Warm Organic home uses **5-7 modules** (each 3x3m = 9m2):

| Module | Function | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| 1-2 | Living room + reading nook | Window seat, fireplace or wood stove |
| 3 | Kitchen + dining | Solid wood island, open shelving, herb window |
| 4 | Master bedroom | Timber ceiling, natural textiles, garden view |
| 5 | Bathroom | Natural stone, freestanding tub option |
| 6 | Covered terrace | Timber pergola, transition to garden |
| 7 (optional) | Second bedroom or studio | Flexible for family growth |

## Room Priorities

1. Kitchen/dining as the heart of the home
2. Living space with natural focal point (fireplace, large window)
3. Covered outdoor transition space
4. Master bedroom as a retreat
5. Generous bathroom with natural materials

## Sustainability Level

**High.** Natural materials with low embodied carbon are the foundation. CLT structure stores carbon. Clay plaster regulates humidity naturally. Recommended: wood fiber insulation (240mm+), natural ventilation strategy supplemented by MVHR. Green roof highly compatible with the aesthetic. Rainwater harvesting aligns with garden-forward lifestyle.

## Budget Indicator

**Mid-High range: 1400-1900 EUR/m2.** Natural materials like solid wood, stone, and clay plaster carry a premium over synthetic alternatives, but longevity and low maintenance offset initial costs. Total for a 5-module home (45m2 usable): approximately 63,000-85,000 EUR structure and finishes.
