# Industrial Loft Profile

## Style Summary

The Industrial Loft home celebrates structure and raw materiality. Exposed steel connections, concrete surfaces, and oversized openings create a dramatic, warehouse-inspired atmosphere. The plan is predominantly open, with living zones defined by furniture placement and level changes rather than walls. High ceilings (where possible through double-height modules) amplify the sense of space and freedom.

## Recommended Materials

- **Structure:** Steel frame with CLT floor panels, exposed connections
- **Exterior cladding:** Corten steel panels, dark fiber cement, standing seam metal
- **Windows:** Steel-frame industrial glazing, large format, dark frames
- **Interior walls:** Fair-faced concrete blocks, raw plaster, exposed brick veneer
- **Flooring:** Polished concrete with underfloor heating, reclaimed timber planks in bedrooms
- **Roof:** Flat or shallow mono-pitch, metal deck with EPDM membrane

## Suggested Module Layout

A typical Industrial Loft home uses **5-8 modules** (each 3x3m = 9m2):

| Module | Function | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| 1-3 | Open-plan living/dining/kitchen | Single volume, island kitchen, track lighting |
| 4 | Master bedroom | Glass partition or curtain separation |
| 5 | Bathroom | Concrete basin, walk-in rain shower, black fixtures |
| 6 | Entry + utility | Steel shelving, exposed pipes as design feature |
| 7 (optional) | Workshop or studio | Concrete floor, high-load electrical |
| 8 (optional) | Mezzanine/loft level | Sleeping or library platform over living area |

## Room Priorities

1. Large open living volume (double-height if budget allows)
2. Kitchen integrated into living space with bold island
3. Workshop or creative studio space
4. Bathroom as a design statement
5. Bedroom - private but connected to main volume

## Sustainability Level

**Moderate.** Steel and concrete have higher embodied carbon than timber-only approaches, but durability is exceptional. Offset with: high-performance insulation behind exposed surfaces, solar panels on flat roof, energy-efficient HVAC. Recommended: thermal mass of concrete for passive temperature regulation, cross-ventilation through large openings.

## Budget Indicator

**Mid range: 1300-1700 EUR/m2.** Steel framing and industrial glazing can match or slightly exceed timber costs, but minimal interior finishing (exposed surfaces) provides savings. Corten and raw concrete age well with zero maintenance. Total for a 6-module home (54m2 usable): approximately 70,000-92,000 EUR structure and finishes.
