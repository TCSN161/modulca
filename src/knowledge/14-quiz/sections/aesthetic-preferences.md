# Section 3: Aesthetic Preferences

Your visual and material preferences determine which architectural profile best matches your taste. These answers directly influence material selection, color schemes, and spatial character.

## Questions

### Q1: Which overall architectural style resonates with you most?
- **Type:** single-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Modern - clean geometry, flat roofs, large glass surfaces
  - B) Traditional - pitched roofs, warm materials, familiar forms
  - C) Organic - flowing shapes, natural integration, earth-inspired
  - D) Industrial - exposed structure, raw materials, loft-like spaces
  - E) Mixed - a bit of everything, eclectic and personal
- **Scoring:** style_profile: A=modern-minimalist, B=traditional-romanian, C=warm-organic, D=industrial-loft, E=eclectic-mixed
- **Affects:** primary profile assignment, roof form, facade treatment

### Q2: Which building material do you find most appealing?
- **Type:** single-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Wood - warm, natural, versatile
  - B) Concrete - bold, sculptural, modern
  - C) Glass - transparent, light-filled, connected
  - D) Stone - solid, timeless, grounded
  - E) Mixed - a combination depending on context
- **Scoring:** material_preference: A=wood, B=concrete, C=glass, D=stone, E=mixed
- **Affects:** cladding recommendations, structural expression, cost estimation

### Q3: Which color palette feels most like home?
- **Type:** single-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Warm - beiges, terracottas, honey tones, soft browns
  - B) Cool - grays, blues, whites, silver accents
  - C) Neutral - blacks, whites, grays with natural wood accents
  - D) Bold - strong accent colors, contrasting combinations
- **Scoring:** palette: A=warm, B=cool, C=neutral, D=bold
- **Affects:** interior finish recommendations, facade color, material pairing

### Q4: On a scale from minimalist to maximalist, where do you fall?
- **Type:** slider (1-5)
- **Options:**
  - 1) Pure minimalist - only essentials, nothing extra
  - 2) Lean - simple with a few curated pieces
  - 3) Balanced - comfortable middle ground
  - 4) Layered - rich textures, collections, personality
  - 5) Maximalist - abundant, decorative, expressive
- **Scoring:** density_score: value 1-5 directly
- **Affects:** built-in furniture ratio, wall treatment, storage approach, room proportions

### Q5: How important is a strong indoor-outdoor connection?
- **Type:** single-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Not important - clear boundary between inside and outside
  - B) Somewhat - nice views and a good terrace are enough
  - C) Important - large sliding doors, covered outdoor living
  - D) Essential - blurred boundaries, garden rooms, biophilic design
- **Scoring:** indoor_outdoor: A=1, B=2, C=4, D=5
- **Affects:** glazing ratio, terrace module design, biophilic profile weighting

### Q6: Do you prefer a home that looks contemporary or one that references historical architecture?
- **Type:** single-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Fully contemporary - forward-looking, no historical references
  - B) Mostly contemporary with subtle nods to tradition
  - C) A balanced blend of old and new
  - D) Strongly traditional - rooted in local architectural heritage
- **Scoring:** temporal_preference: A=contemporary, B=contemporary-lean, C=blended, D=traditional
- **Affects:** roof pitch, window proportions, decorative element inclusion, profile matching

### Q7: Which texture combination appeals to you most?
- **Type:** single-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Smooth and polished - glass, lacquer, marble
  - B) Matte and natural - linen, matte wood, plaster
  - C) Raw and textured - exposed brick, rough concrete, weathered metal
  - D) Soft and layered - fabrics, rugs, upholstery, woven materials
- **Scoring:** texture_profile: A=polished, B=natural, C=raw, D=soft
- **Affects:** interior material specification, surface finish recommendations, maintenance level
