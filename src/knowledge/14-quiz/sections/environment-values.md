# Section 4: Environment & Values

Your sustainability priorities and connection to nature influence material choices, energy systems, and landscape design within your modular home.

## Questions

### Q1: How important is energy efficiency to you?
- **Type:** scale (1-5)
- **Options:**
  - 1) Not a priority - standard construction is fine
  - 2) Slightly important - basic insulation and efficient appliances
  - 3) Moderately important - above-average performance, reasonable investment
  - 4) Very important - high-performance envelope, heat recovery ventilation
  - 5) Critical - passive house standard, near-zero energy consumption
- **Scoring:** energy_priority: value 1-5 directly
- **Affects:** insulation specification, HVAC system selection, window U-value, airtightness target

### Q2: How do you feel about natural and reclaimed materials?
- **Type:** single-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Indifferent - performance and cost matter more than origin
  - B) Somewhat interested - I like the idea if it does not add much cost
  - C) Prefer natural - wood, stone, lime plaster over synthetic options
  - D) Strongly committed - reclaimed, certified, low-embodied-carbon materials only
- **Scoring:** natural_material_weight: A=1, B=2, C=4, D=5
- **Affects:** material specification, CLT vs steel frame weighting, finish selection, cost impact

### Q3: Are you interested in solar panels, green roofs, or other active green systems?
- **Type:** multi-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Solar photovoltaic panels (electricity generation)
  - B) Solar thermal (hot water)
  - C) Green roof or living roof
  - D) Rainwater harvesting system
  - E) None of these - keep it simple
- **Scoring:** green_systems: count of selections (0-4), E=0
- **Affects:** roof structural requirements, module layout for south orientation, plumbing complexity, budget allocation

### Q4: What type of garden or outdoor landscape do you envision?
- **Type:** single-choice
- **Options:**
  - A) Edible garden - vegetables, herbs, fruit trees
  - B) Ornamental garden - flowers, decorative plants, curated landscape
  - C) Wild or naturalistic - native plants, meadow, low-maintenance biodiversity
  - D) Minimal - paved terrace, gravel, very low maintenance
- **Scoring:** garden_type: A=edible, B=ornamental, C=wild, D=minimal
- **Affects:** landscape module design, water infrastructure, terrace vs garden ratio, maintenance level

### Q5: How would you rate your desire for connection to the natural environment?
- **Type:** scale (1-5)
- **Options:**
  - 1) Low - I am mostly an indoor person
  - 2) Mild - I enjoy a window with a view
  - 3) Moderate - natural light and ventilation matter to me
  - 4) High - I want to feel nature from every room
  - 5) Essential - nature is the core of my living experience
- **Scoring:** nature_connection: value 1-5 directly
- **Affects:** biophilic profile weighting, glazing area, courtyard module, green wall inclusion, site orientation priority
