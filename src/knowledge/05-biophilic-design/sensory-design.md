# Multi-Sensory Design

## Overview
Architecture is experienced through all senses, not just sight. Juhani Pallasmaa's "The Eyes of the Skin" argues that modern architecture is too ocular-centric. Biophilic multi-sensory design engages touch (haptic), sound (acoustic), smell (olfactory), and even thermal sensation to create richer, more nourishing spaces.

## Sensory Design Strategies

### Touch (Haptic)
| Element | Texture | Effect |
|---------|---------|--------|
| Wood surfaces (sanded) | Warm, grained | Comfort, connection to nature |
| Stone (honed) | Cool, smooth | Grounding, permanence |
| Rough plaster (lime/clay) | Varied, organic | Artisan quality, warmth |
| Linen/cotton textiles | Soft, woven | Comfort, natural luxury |
| Cork flooring | Warm, resilient | Gentle underfoot, quiet |
| Exposed brick | Rough, varied | Character, history |

### Sound (Acoustic)
| Element | Sound Quality | Design Application |
|---------|--------------|-------------------|
| Water feature | Broadband, calming | Living room, courtyard |
| Wind in trees | Rustling, unpredictable | Garden audible from inside |
| Bird song | Natural, varied | Garden habitat planting |
| Fire crackling | Warm, intermittent | Wood stove or bioethanol fire |
| Rain on roof | Rhythmic, calming | Skylight or metal roof section |
| Silence | Absence of noise | Acoustic insulation between modules |

### Smell (Olfactory)
| Source | Scent | Application |
|--------|-------|-------------|
| Cedar/pine wood | Woody, fresh | Exposed CLT ceiling, cedar cladding |
| Fresh herbs | Green, aromatic | Kitchen herb garden/windowsill |
| Beeswax finish | Honey, warm | Natural wood floor treatment |
| Rain on earth (petrichor) | Earthy, fresh | Open windows during rain, garden nearby |
| Lavender/rosemary | Calming/stimulating | Garden planting near entrance |
| Linseed oil | Nutty, traditional | Natural wood finish |

### Thermal Variation
| Strategy | Sensation | Application |
|----------|-----------|-------------|
| Radiant floor heating | Warm feet | Bathroom, living room zones |
| Cool stone surfaces | Cool touch points | Kitchen counter, entryway |
| Sun patches moving through room | Warm spots shift with time | Large windows, open plan |
| Operable windows | Fresh air, temperature change | Natural ventilation |
| Fireplace radiant heat | Directional warmth | Wood stove or fireplace module |

## Room-by-Room Sensory Design

| Room | Primary Senses | Key Elements |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| Entrance | Touch, smell | Textured door handle, herb planting, timber threshold |
| Living room | All five | Fire, wood, textiles, views, rain sound on skylight |
| Kitchen | Smell, touch, sound | Herbs, wood/stone surfaces, cooking sounds |
| Bedroom | Touch, sound, temperature | Linen, wood, silence (acoustic isolation), cool air |
| Bathroom | Touch, temperature, smell | Stone, warm floor, cedar, steam |
| Garden | All five | Full nature immersion |

## ModulCA Application
- **CLT scent**: Exposed CLT ceiling retains subtle wood scent for months
- **Acoustic modules**: Module walls with 45dB+ isolation — bedroom silence guaranteed
- **Textured module walls**: Factory-apply lime plaster or timber cladding to module interior
- **Thermal zoning**: Different floor finishes per module — warm cork in living, cool stone in entry
- **Rain experience**: One skylight module lets occupants hear and see rain
- **Herb entrance**: Planting bed along entrance module path — brush herbs when arriving
