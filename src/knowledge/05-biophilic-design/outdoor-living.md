# Indoor-Outdoor Connection

## Overview
The transition between indoor and outdoor space is one of the most powerful biophilic design opportunities. A well-designed threshold — terrace, veranda, courtyard — extends living space, connects occupants to weather and seasons, and satisfies the biophilic need for both prospect (open view) and refuge (protected shelter).

## Threshold Design Types

| Type | Character | Best For | Min. Size |
|------|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Covered terrace | Sheltered, open on sides | All-weather outdoor living | 3×4m (12m²) |
| Open terrace/deck | Exposed, sun access | Sunbathing, dining | 3×3m (9m²) |
| Veranda/porch | Covered, one side open | Entrance transition | 1.5×3m |
| Courtyard | Enclosed by building | Privacy, microclimate | 4×4m (16m²) |
| Loggia | Covered, recessed into building | Wind-protected seating | 2×3m (6m²) |
| Pergola | Semi-covered, climbing plants | Dappled light, nature | 3×3m |
| Balcony | Cantilevered, upper floor | View, fresh air | 1.5×3m |

## Design Principles (A Pattern Language)

| Pattern | Principle | Application |
|---------|-----------|-------------|
| #140 Private terrace | Every dwelling needs a private outdoor room | At least one terrace per home |
| #163 Outdoor room | Make it feel like a room without roof | Walls on 2-3 sides, clear edges |
| #166 Gallery surround | Covered transition between in and out | Overhang at entrance and terrace |
| #167 Six-foot balcony | Min 1.80m depth for usability | Anything less is storage-only |
| #174 Trellised walk | Semi-covered path with plants | Connect modules via pergola |

## Microclimate Design

| Factor | Strategy | Notes |
|--------|----------|-------|
| Wind protection | L or U shape building forms | Create sheltered south-facing pocket |
| Sun access | South/southwest orientation | For terrace and courtyard |
| Rain shelter | 1.5m+ overhang | Protects terrace from rain |
| Privacy | Hedges, fences, planting | Especially in Dutch plots (small, close neighbors) |
| Heat island | Permeable paving, planting | Avoid full concrete terrace |
| Evening use | Wind shelter + thermal mass wall | Radiates stored heat in evening |

## ModulCA Application
- **Terrace module**: One 3×3m module as open or covered terrace — same grid, seamless connection
- **L-shape courtyard**: 6-module L creates a sheltered outdoor room on the south side
- **U-shape courtyard**: 8-module U fully encloses a private garden courtyard
- **Level threshold**: Module floor on screw piles is naturally 0.5m above ground — add terrace deck at same level for zero-threshold connection
- **Pergola extension**: Lightweight pergola structure attaches to module grid at 3m intervals
- **Sliding doors**: Module wall replaced by full-width sliding glass — 2.40m clear opening
- **Seasonal adaptation**: Add/remove shade sail or glass enclosure seasonally

## Climate Considerations

| Climate | Priority | Strategy |
|---------|----------|----------|
| Romania (continental) | Winter sun, summer shade | South terrace + deciduous pergola |
| Netherlands (maritime) | Wind protection, rain shelter | Deep overhang, wind-screen, covered terrace |
| Both | Evening warmth | Thermal mass wall on west side of terrace |
