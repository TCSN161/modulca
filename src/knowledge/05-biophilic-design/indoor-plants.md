# Indoor Plants & Green Walls

## Overview
Indoor plants are the most accessible biophilic design strategy. They improve air quality, reduce stress, increase productivity, and add visual complexity and life to interior spaces. From a single pot plant to full living walls, every level of greenery contributes to occupant wellbeing.

## Air-Purifying Plants (NASA Clean Air Study)

| Plant | Removes | Light Need | Maintenance |
|-------|---------|------------|-------------|
| Snake plant (Sansevieria) | Formaldehyde, benzene | Low | Very easy |
| Pothos (Epipremnum) | Formaldehyde, xylene | Low-medium | Easy |
| Peace lily (Spathiphyllum) | Ammonia, benzene, formaldehyde | Low-medium | Medium |
| Spider plant (Chlorophytum) | Formaldehyde, xylene | Medium | Easy |
| Rubber plant (Ficus elastica) | Formaldehyde | Medium | Easy |
| Boston fern (Nephrolepis) | Formaldehyde, xylene | Medium | Medium (humidity) |
| Areca palm (Dypsis) | Formaldehyde, xylene, toluene | Medium-high | Medium |

## Plant Placement Guidelines

| Location | Recommended Plants | Notes |
|----------|--------------------|-------|
| Window sill (south) | Succulents, herbs, cacti | Full sun tolerance |
| Window sill (north) | Ferns, pothos, peace lily | Low light tolerant |
| Living room floor | Fiddle leaf fig, monstera, palm | Statement plants |
| Kitchen | Herbs (basil, mint, rosemary) | Functional + aromatic |
| Bathroom | Ferns, orchids, tillandsia | Humidity-loving |
| Bedroom | Snake plant, pothos | Low maintenance, nighttime O₂ |
| Home office | Spider plant, ZZ plant | Air purifying, low distraction |

## Quantity Guidelines

| Space Size | Minimum Plants | Optimal | Notes |
|------------|---------------|---------|-------|
| Small room (7-10m²) | 2-3 | 4-6 | 1 module |
| Medium room (14-20m²) | 4-6 | 8-12 | 2-3 modules |
| Large room (20-30m²) | 6-8 | 12-15 | Open plan area |
| Per person (air quality) | 1 per 10m² | 1 per 6m² | NASA recommendation |

## ModulCA Application
- **Window module design**: Deep sills (200mm) serve as plant shelves
- **Green wall module**: Dedicate one interior module wall to a living wall system
- **Kitchen herb module**: Built-in herb planter in kitchen module window
- **Bathroom humidity**: Module ventilation design can support humidity-loving plants
- **Structural consideration**: Green walls add 30-50 kg/m² — CLT walls handle this easily
- **Irrigation routing**: Pre-route water supply for automated green wall irrigation in factory
