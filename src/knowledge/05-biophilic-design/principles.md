# Principles of Biophilic Design

## Overview
Biophilic design is the practice of connecting building occupants more closely to nature. The term comes from E.O. Wilson's "biophilia hypothesis" — the innate human tendency to seek connections with nature and other forms of life. In architecture, this translates into design strategies that improve health, wellbeing, and productivity.

## The 14 Patterns of Biophilic Design (Terrapin Bright Green)

### Nature in the Space (Direct Experience)
| Pattern | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| 1. Visual connection with nature | View of natural elements | Windows to garden |
| 2. Non-visual connection | Sound, touch, smell of nature | Water sounds, wood texture |
| 3. Non-rhythmic sensory stimuli | Unpredictable natural movement | Leaves rustling, firelight |
| 4. Thermal & airflow variability | Changes in temperature, airflow | Operable windows, natural ventilation |
| 5. Presence of water | Seeing, hearing, touching water | Rain chains, indoor fountain |
| 6. Dynamic & diffuse light | Varying light through the day | Skylights, light wells |
| 7. Connection with natural systems | Awareness of seasons, ecology | Deciduous trees, rain gardens |

### Natural Analogues (Indirect References)
| Pattern | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| 8. Biomorphic forms & patterns | Organic shapes in design | Curved walls, leaf patterns |
| 9. Material connection with nature | Natural materials, textures | Wood, stone, wool |
| 10. Complexity & order | Rich but organized patterns | Fractals, varied but harmonious |

### Nature of the Space (Spatial Conditions)
| Pattern | Description | Example |
|---------|-------------|---------|
| 11. Prospect | Long-distance views | Open plan, elevated position |
| 12. Refuge | Protected, enclosed space | Reading nook, alcove |
| 13. Mystery | Partially obscured views | Curved paths, layered spaces |
| 14. Risk/Peril | Safe thrill | Cantilever, glass floor |

## Health Benefits (Evidence-Based)

| Benefit | Evidence | Impact |
|---------|----------|--------|
| Stress reduction | Cortisol levels drop 15-25% | Views of nature, plants |
| Productivity increase | 8-15% improvement | Daylight, natural ventilation |
| Faster healing | 8.5% fewer hospital days | Nature views from patient rooms |
| Creativity boost | 15% increase in creative output | Natural materials, complexity |
| Better sleep | Improved circadian rhythm | Dynamic daylight exposure |
| Reduced absenteeism | 10-15% reduction | Indoor plants, natural light |

## ModulCA Application
- **Window placement**: Each module wall can have large windows facing garden/nature
- **Natural materials**: CLT structure is already biophilic — exposed wood ceilings and walls
- **Courtyard layouts**: U-shape and courtyard configurations create private nature spaces
- **Green roof modules**: Flat roof modules support green roofs (sedum or intensive)
- **Indoor-outdoor flow**: Module threshold design allows seamless terrace connection
- **Rain chains**: Replace downpipes with visible rain chains — water as design element
