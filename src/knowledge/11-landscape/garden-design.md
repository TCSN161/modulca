# Garden Design Principles

## Overview
Effective garden design divides outdoor space into functional zones tailored to household size, climate, and lifestyle. This article covers zone planning, path and terrace sizing, planting distances, and pergola dimensions relevant to modular home sites.

## Garden Zones

Residential gardens are typically organized into four primary zones:

| Zone | Purpose | Min Area (2-person) | Min Area (4-person) | Notes |
|------|---------|---------------------|---------------------|-------|
| Ornamental | Visual appeal, flowers, shrubs | 8 m² | 15 m² | Front or side garden |
| Edible | Vegetables, herbs, fruit | 10 m² | 20 m² | 10 m² feeds ~1 person basic salads |
| Play | Children's activities | -- | 15 m² | Soft surface, visible from kitchen |
| Relaxation | Seating, dining, lounging | 6 m² | 12 m² | Adjacent to house, sheltered |

### Minimum Garden Sizes by Household

| Household | Min Usable Garden | Recommended | Lot Coverage Notes |
|-----------|-------------------|-------------|-------------------|
| 1-2 persons | 30 m² | 50-80 m² | Sufficient for terrace + small beds |
| 3-4 persons | 60 m² | 100-150 m² | Allows play zone + edible garden |
| 5+ persons | 100 m² | 150-250 m² | Full four-zone layout achievable |

## Path Dimensions

| Path Type | Width | Surface Options |
|-----------|-------|-----------------|
| Stepping stones | 0.40 m | Natural stone, concrete pavers |
| Single walking path | 0.60 m | Gravel, pavers, brick |
| Comfortable double path | 1.20 m | Pavers, natural stone |
| Wheelchair accessible | 1.50 m | Smooth pavers, resin-bound gravel |
| Service/maintenance path | 0.90 m | Gravel or concrete |

Path gradients should not exceed 1:20 (5%) for accessibility. Cross-slopes for drainage: 1-2%.

## Terrace and Patio Sizing

| Configuration | Minimum Size | Comfortable Size | Notes |
|---------------|-------------|------------------|-------|
| 2-person dining | 2.0 x 2.5 m (5 m²) | 2.5 x 3.0 m | Bistro table + chairs |
| 4-person dining | 2.0 x 3.0 m (6 m²) | 3.0 x 4.0 m (12 m²) | Standard dining table |
| 6-person dining | 3.0 x 4.0 m (12 m²) | 3.5 x 5.0 m | Rectangular table |
| Lounge area | 3.0 x 3.0 m (9 m²) | 4.0 x 4.0 m | Sofa set + coffee table |
| Combined dining + lounge | 4.0 x 6.0 m (24 m²) | 5.0 x 7.0 m | Separate seating clusters |

Orientation: south or south-west facing preferred for maximum afternoon sun in Romania.

## Pergola Dimensions

| Parameter | Minimum | Standard | Notes |
|-----------|---------|----------|-------|
| Clear height | 2.20 m | 2.40-2.60 m | Allows climbing plants |
| Width (walkway) | 1.50 m | 2.00-2.50 m | Freestanding garden pergola |
| Width (terrace cover) | 3.00 m | 3.50-4.00 m | Covers dining area |
| Depth (projection) | 2.50 m | 3.00-4.00 m | Shade coverage |
| Post section (timber) | 100 x 100 mm | 120 x 120 mm | Laminated timber or hardwood |
| Post spacing | 2.50 m max | 3.00 m with beams | Structural dependent |

## Planting Distances from Structures

| Plant Type | Min Distance from Foundation | Min from Boundary |
|------------|------------------------------|-------------------|
| Groundcover / perennials | 0.30 m | 0.00 m (check local) |
| Small shrubs (<1.5 m) | 0.50 m | 0.50 m |
| Large shrubs (1.5-3.0 m) | 1.00 m | 1.00 m |
| Small trees (<6 m mature) | 2.00 m | 2.00 m |
| Medium trees (6-12 m) | 3.00 m | 3.00 m |
| Large trees (>12 m) | 5.00 m+ | 4.00 m |

## ModulCA Application

The 3x3 m module grid naturally supports garden zone planning:

- **Terrace extension**: A single 3x3 m module footprint (9 m²) matches a comfortable 4-person dining terrace. Two modules side-by-side (3x6 m = 18 m²) create a combined dining and lounge area.
- **L-shape and U-shape layouts** frame courtyard gardens, creating sheltered microclimates ideal for Mediterranean-style planting even in Romanian climate zone 6a-7b.
- **Pergola integration**: Pergola posts can align with the 3 m module grid, simplifying structural connections. A 3x3 m pergola covers one full module bay.
- **Path planning**: The 3 m grid allows a 1.20 m path with 0.90 m planting beds on each side, fitting exactly within one module width.
- **Screw pile foundations** leave soil permeable between piles, allowing garden beds to extend close to the building without disrupting drainage.
