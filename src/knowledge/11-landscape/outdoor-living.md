# Outdoor Living Spaces

## Overview
Outdoor living extends usable floor area beyond the building envelope. This article covers covered terraces, decks, pergolas, outdoor kitchens, pool setbacks, and shade structures with sizing standards relevant to modular construction.

## Covered Terraces

Covered terraces provide weather-protected outdoor space. Key dimensions:

| Parameter | Minimum | Comfortable | Notes |
|-----------|---------|-------------|-------|
| Depth (projection from wall) | 2.00 m | 3.00-4.00 m | <2 m offers little rain protection |
| Width | 3.00 m | 4.00-6.00 m | Accommodate dining table |
| Clear height | 2.20 m | 2.40-2.70 m | Match interior ceiling or slightly lower |
| Per-person dining area | 1.5 m² | 2.0 m² | Including chair push-back space |
| Dining table clearance | 0.80 m around table | 1.00 m | Passage behind seated person |

Orientation priority: South > South-West > West > East > North (for Central/Eastern Europe).

## Deck Construction

### Material Comparison

| Property | Softwood Timber | Hardwood Timber | Wood-Plastic Composite | Natural Stone |
|----------|----------------|-----------------|----------------------|---------------|
| Cost (EUR/m²) | 40-70 | 80-150 | 60-120 | 100-250 |
| Lifespan (years) | 10-15 | 20-30 | 25-30 | 50+ |
| Maintenance | Annual treatment | Annual oiling | Low, occasional wash | Very low |
| Slip resistance | Good when treated | Good to moderate | Good, textured | Variable |
| Heat absorption | Low | Low-moderate | High (gets hot in sun) | High |
| Sustainability | FSC-certified available | Slow growth, verify source | Recyclable, mixed | Quarried, high transport |
| Install over screw piles | Excellent | Excellent | Excellent | Requires subframe |

### Deck Structural Notes
- Joist spacing: 400-500 mm for timber boards, 300-400 mm for composite
- Board thickness: minimum 21 mm (timber), 22-25 mm (composite)
- Ventilation gap below deck: minimum 150 mm above ground
- Fall away from building: minimum 1:80 (1.25%)

## Outdoor Kitchens

Minimum clearances and sizing for outdoor cooking areas:

| Element | Dimension | Notes |
|---------|-----------|-------|
| BBQ/grill distance from building | 3.00 m minimum | Fire safety, reduce smoke ingress |
| BBQ distance from boundary | 1.50 m minimum | Check local fire codes |
| Counter depth | 0.60 m | Standard worktop |
| Counter height | 0.85-0.90 m | Ergonomic standing work height |
| Min counter run | 1.50 m | Prep + grill + serving |
| Comfortable counter run | 2.40-3.60 m | Full outdoor kitchen |
| Overhead clearance (range hood) | 0.70-0.80 m above grill | If covered area |
| Gas line requirements | Licensed installer only | Check Romanian ISCIR regulations |

## ModulCA Application

ModulCA's modular system creates natural outdoor living opportunities:

- **Terrace modules**: The 3x3 m module footprint serves as a deck or covered terrace unit. A single module bay (9 m²) comfortably seats 4-6 for dining. Two bays (18 m²) create a generous outdoor living room.
- **Roof overhang integration**: Module roof structures can extend 1.5-2.0 m beyond the wall to create covered terraces without separate posts, using the existing structural frame.
- **Screw pile decking**: The same screw pile foundation system used for modules supports raised timber decks, maintaining a consistent floor level from interior to exterior (critical for accessibility and aesthetics).
- **Outdoor kitchen placement**: Position outdoor kitchen modules at least one module bay (3 m) from the main living modules to meet fire safety clearances.
- **Step-down terracing**: On sloped Romanian sites, screw piles at different heights allow level decks that step down the terrain, with each terrace at a 3 m grid increment.
- **Modular pergola**: A 3x3 m or 3x6 m pergola frame aligns with the structural grid and can be delivered as a kit alongside housing modules.
