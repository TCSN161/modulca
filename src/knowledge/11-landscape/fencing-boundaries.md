# Fencing & Boundary Regulations

## Overview
Fencing defines property boundaries, provides privacy and security, and can serve as wind or noise screening. Regulations vary between EU countries and even between municipalities. This article covers common height rules for Romania and the Netherlands, fence type comparisons, and noise barrier basics relevant to modular home sites.

## Height Regulations

### Romania (Romanian Civil Code 2011, Local Urbanism Regulations)

| Location | Maximum Height | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Front boundary (street-facing) | 1.00-1.20 m solid + up to 2.00 m transparent | Transparent = open metalwork, glass, mesh above solid base |
| Side boundary | 1.80-2.00 m | Solid permitted; check local RLU |
| Rear boundary | 1.80-2.00 m | Solid permitted |
| Corner plots (visibility triangle) | 0.90 m max within 6 m of intersection | Traffic safety requirement |
| Industrial/commercial | Up to 2.50 m | With Certificat de Urbanism approval |

Romanian municipalities issue specific rules via Regulamentul Local de Urbanism (RLU). Always verify with the local Certificat de Urbanism.

### Netherlands (Burenrecht, Bouwbesluit 2012, APV)

| Location | Maximum Height | Notes |
|----------|---------------|-------|
| Front boundary (voortuin) | 1.00 m | Most gemeenten; some allow 1.20 m |
| Side boundary | 2.00 m | Standard; no permit needed up to 2.00 m |
| Rear boundary | 2.00 m | Standard backyard fence |
| Above 2.00 m | Requires omgevingsvergunning | Rarely granted in residential areas |
| Shared boundary (mandelige scheidsmuur) | Costs shared 50/50 between neighbors | Dutch Civil Code Book 5, Art. 49 |

### Distance from Property Line

| Country | Fence Position | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Romania | On the boundary line (shared) or 0.00 m inside own property | Shared fences: costs split by agreement |
| Netherlands | On the boundary line (mandelig) or own property | Neighbor can demand a 2.00 m fence at shared cost |

## Noise Barriers

For sites near roads or railways:

| Barrier Type | Noise Reduction | Min Height | Notes |
|-------------|-----------------|------------|-------|
| Solid timber fence (25 mm+) | 10-15 dB | 1.80 m | Must be gap-free; overlapping boards |
| Mass-loaded vinyl on fence | 15-20 dB | 1.80 m | Added to existing fence |
| Earth berm | 10-20 dB | 1.50-3.00 m | Effective but space-intensive |
| Acoustic masonry wall | 20-30 dB | 2.00-3.00 m | Best performance, highest cost |
| Dense evergreen hedge (3 m+) | 3-6 dB | 3.00 m+ | Psychological benefit exceeds measured reduction |

Rule of thumb: a solid barrier must break line-of-sight between noise source and receiver to be effective. Every additional metre of height above line-of-sight adds roughly 1.5 dB of reduction.

## ModulCA Application

Fencing and boundaries integrate with the ModulCA system in several ways:

- **Module placement setbacks**: Romanian regulations typically require 1.0 m minimum from side boundaries for structures. Position the outermost module at least 1.0 m from the property line, leaving space for a fence or hedge between.
- **Integrated privacy screens**: The 3 m module grid allows slatted timber screens to be mounted between structural posts at module corners, creating integrated privacy barriers that are part of the building rather than separate fences.
- **Screw pile fence posts**: The same screw pile technology used for foundations can anchor fence posts, providing stable support without concrete footings and maintaining ground permeability.
- **Prefab fence panels**: 3.0 m wide fence panels align with the module grid and can be delivered alongside housing modules as a complete site package.
- **Living boundaries**: Plant hedges at least 0.50 m from the boundary line. Allow 2-3 years for privacy hedges to reach effective height; install temporary screening during this period.
- **Noise mitigation**: For sites near busy roads, position bedrooms on the far side of the module layout, using living spaces and solid fence barriers as acoustic buffers.
