# Sustainable Drainage Systems (SUDS)

## Overview
Sustainable Drainage Systems (SUDS) manage rainwater close to where it falls, mimicking natural drainage to reduce flood risk, improve water quality, and recharge groundwater. For modular homes on screw pile foundations, SUDS integration is particularly advantageous because the elevated structure preserves natural soil permeability beneath the building.

## EU Regulatory Context

The EU Water Framework Directive (2000/60/EC) requires member states to achieve "good ecological status" for all water bodies. This drives national regulations that increasingly mandate on-site stormwater management for new developments.

### Rain Gardens

Rain gardens are planted depressions that collect and filter runoff. Sizing guidelines:

| Parameter | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Area | 5-10% of contributing impervious area | Minimum practical size 2 m² |
| Depth | 100-200 mm ponding depth | Max 300 mm for safety |
| Soil mix | 60% sand, 20% topsoil, 20% compost | Free-draining growing medium |
| Drain time | Empty within 24-48 hours | Prevents mosquito breeding |
| Setback from building | 3.00 m minimum | Protect foundations from saturation |
| Overflow | Connected to drain or second basin | For extreme rainfall events |

### Soakaway Design

Rule of thumb sizing for residential soakaways:

| Roof Area | Soakaway Volume Required | Typical Dimensions |
|-----------|--------------------------|-------------------|
| 20 m² | 1.0 m³ | 1.0 x 1.0 x 1.0 m |
| 40 m² | 2.0 m³ | 1.0 x 1.0 x 2.0 m |
| 60 m² | 3.0 m³ | 1.5 x 1.0 x 2.0 m |
| 80 m² | 4.0 m³ | 2.0 x 1.0 x 2.0 m |
| 100 m² | 5.0 m³ | 2.5 x 1.0 x 2.0 m |

**Formula**: Volume = Roof Area x Rainfall Intensity x Storm Duration / 1000, with safety factor. The 1 m³ per 20 m² rule assumes a 50 mm/hr design storm for 1 hour with 1:1 safety factor.

Soakaway placement: minimum 5 m from any building foundation, minimum 2.5 m from boundary.

## Sizing Calculation Example

For a typical ModulCA 4-module home (4 x 9 m² = 36 m² roof footprint):

1. **Roof area**: 36 m² (assuming flat/low-pitch roof draining to one side)
2. **Design rainfall**: 50 mm/hr for 1-hour storm (Romanian standard)
3. **Runoff volume**: 36 m² x 0.050 m = 1.8 m³
4. **Soakaway size**: 1.8 m³ (use 2.0 m³ with safety factor) = crate soakaway 1.0 x 1.0 x 2.0 m
5. **Rain garden alternative**: 36 m² x 10% = 3.6 m² rain garden at 200 mm depth = 0.72 m³ detention + infiltration

## ModulCA Application

Screw pile foundations give ModulCA homes a distinct SUDS advantage:

- **Permeable ground preserved**: Unlike slab or strip foundations, screw piles occupy minimal ground area (each pile ~150 mm diameter). The soil beneath the building remains permeable, naturally infiltrating rainfall that passes through the raised floor zone.
- **Raised floor void**: The 400-800 mm void beneath modules allows rainwater to reach soil underneath, effectively making the building footprint semi-permeable.
- **Rain chain integration**: Downpipes from module roofs can feed directly into rain gardens or soakaways positioned at the 3 m grid points between pile locations.
- **Green roof compatibility**: Extensive green roofs (sedum, 60-100 mm substrate) add only 80-120 kg/m² saturated load, well within the structural capacity of modular roof panels designed for snow loads (75-150 kg/m² in Romanian climate zones).
- **Permeable driveway**: Screw pile approach extends to carport structures, keeping the entire site permeable except for the minimal pile footprints.
