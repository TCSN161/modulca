# European Planting & Hardiness Zones

## Overview
Plant hardiness zones indicate the minimum winter temperatures a region experiences, guiding species selection for landscaping. For modular homes on screw pile foundations, tree and shrub selection must also consider root systems that could interfere with piles, as well as species suitable for green walls and facades that enhance building performance.

## Trees for Shade Near Modular Homes

Tree selection near screw pile foundations must balance shade benefits against root damage risk. Large trees with aggressive root systems can exert lateral forces on piles or cause soil moisture changes affecting pile capacity.

### Safe Planting Distances from Screw Piles

| Tree Category | Mature Height | Root Spread | Min Distance from Piles | Examples |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|------------------------|----------|
| Small ornamental | <6 m | 2-4 m | 2.00 m | Japanese maple, serviceberry, crabapple |
| Small-medium | 6-10 m | 4-6 m | 3.00 m | Birch, rowan, hornbeam |
| Medium | 10-15 m | 6-10 m | 5.00 m | Field maple, cherry, pear |
| Large | 15-25 m | 10-15 m | 8.00 m+ | Oak, beech, lime (linden) |
| Very large / aggressive roots | >25 m | 15-25 m | 12.00 m+ | Poplar, willow, plane tree |

**Rule of thumb**: Minimum planting distance from screw piles = mature tree height x 0.5, with an absolute minimum of 5 m for any tree exceeding 10 m mature height.

**Trees to AVOID near screw piles**: Populus (poplar), Salix (willow), Robinia (black locust), Ailanthus (tree of heaven) -- all have aggressive, far-reaching root systems.

## Seasonal Planting Calendar for Romania

| Month | Activity | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------|
| January-February | Plan garden layout, order seeds | Indoor seed starting (tomatoes, peppers) |

| July-August | Maintain, harvest, deadhead flowers | Water deeply in drought periods |

## Green Wall and Facade Options

Green walls reduce building surface temperature by 5-15 C in summer and provide insulation in winter.

| System | Type | Suitable Zones | Weight (kg/m²) | Irrigation | Cost (EUR/m²) | Maintenance |
|--------|------|---------------|-----------------|------------|---------------|-------------|
| Climbing plants on wire/trellis | Indirect | 5b-9b | 5-15 | Natural rainfall | 15-40 | Prune 1-2x/year |
| Self-clinging climbers | Direct | 6a-9b | 5-20 | Natural rainfall | 5-15 | Monitor for damage |
| Modular panel (felt pocket) | Living wall | 7a-9b | 30-80 (saturated) | Automated drip | 200-500 | Weekly check, seasonal replant |
| Modular tray system | Living wall | 6a-9b | 40-100 (saturated) | Automated drip | 300-700 | Monthly check |

**Warning for modular timber cladding**: Avoid self-clinging climbers (ivy, Virginia creeper) directly on timber or rendered surfaces as they can trap moisture and damage finishes. Use a trellis mounted 50-100 mm off the wall with stainless steel standoffs.

## ModulCA Application

Planting zone knowledge directly impacts ModulCA site design:

- **Species selection by market**: The AI consultant should recommend plants matching the project's hardiness zone. A ModulCA home near Cluj (zone 6b) needs different species than one near Constanta (zone 8a) or Rotterdam (zone 8a-8b).
- **Root protection zone**: Maintain a minimum 5 m clear zone between any tree over 10 m mature height and the nearest screw pile. The 3 m grid helps: a tree planted 2 grid spacings (6 m) from the building edge provides shade without root risk.
- **Green facade integration**: Mount trellis systems on the module exterior using the panel joint lines as fixing points. The 3 m module width accommodates standard trellis panels (typically 0.60-1.80 m wide) in repeating patterns.
- **Seasonal planning**: In Romania, schedule site landscaping for October-November (after module installation) to take advantage of autumn planting conditions for trees and hedges. This allows one full growing season before the first summer.
- **Microclimate creation**: L-shape and U-shape module layouts create sheltered courtyards that can be 1-2 hardiness zones warmer than the exposed site, enabling planting of slightly tender species (e.g., zone 8 plants in a zone 7 area).
- **Green roof compatibility**: Sedum species (Sedum album, S. acre, S. spurium) are hardy to zone 3-4 and thrive across all ModulCA markets without irrigation once established.
