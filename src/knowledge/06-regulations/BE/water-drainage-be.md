# Water Management & Drainage (Belgium)

## Overview

Water management in Belgium is a regional competence, with Flanders, Wallonia, and Brussels each having distinct regulations. Belgium's flat, densely built landscape and high water table make rainwater management, sewage treatment, and flood prevention critical topics. For new construction, all three regions require separation of rainwater and wastewater, rainwater harvesting, and controlled discharge. Flanders has the most prescriptive requirements.

## Regional Authorities

| Region | Water Authority | Sewage Authority | Key Legislation |
|--------|----------------|------------------|-----------------|
| Flanders | VMM (Vlaamse Milieumaatschappij) | Aquafin + municipal rioolbeheerder | Vlarem II, GSV Hemelwater |
| Wallonia | SPW (Service public de Wallonie) | SPGE (Société Publique de Gestion de l'Eau) | Code de l'eau |
| Brussels | Bruxelles Environnement | VIVAQUA / SBGE | Ordonnance relative à l'eau |

## Flanders: Hemelwaterverordening (Rainwater Regulation)

The Gewestelijke Stedenbouwkundige Verordening inzake Hemelwater (GSV Hemelwater, updated 2023) is the most detailed rainwater regulation in Belgium:

### Rainwater Tank (Hemelwaterput)

| Roof Area | Minimum Tank Volume | Mandatory? |
|-----------|--------------------|-|
| < 40 m² | Not required | No |
| 40-80 m² | 5,000 litres | Yes (new build) |
| 80-120 m² | 7,500 litres | Yes (new build) |
| 120-200 m² | 10,000 litres | Yes (new build) |
| > 200 m² | 10,000 L + 50 L per additional m² | Yes (new build) |

### Mandatory Rainwater Reuse

Collected rainwater must be connected to:

- Toilet flushing
- Washing machine
- Outdoor taps
- Cleaning (optional)

A dual plumbing system (drinkwater / hemelwater) is required in new builds.

### Infiltration Requirement

| Condition | Requirement |
|-----------|------------|
| Permeable soil | Infiltration facility (infiltratievoorziening) required |
| Minimum volume | 33 litres per m² of impermeable surface |
| Design | Infiltration crate, wadi, permeable paving, or rain garden |
| Overflow | To buffering, then to drainage/sewer |

### Buffering Requirement

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Buffer volume | 45 litres per m² of impermeable surface |
| Discharge rate | Max 20 litres/second/hectare (varies by municipality) |
| Purpose | Delay peak runoff to prevent flooding |

### Discharge Hierarchy (Prioriteit Afvoer)

Flanders mandates this priority order for excess rainwater:

1. **Reuse** (via hemelwaterput)
2. **Infiltration** into the ground
3. **Buffering** with delayed discharge
4. **Surface water** discharge to watercourse
5. **Storm sewer** (last resort)
6. **Combined sewer** (absolutely last resort)

## Wallonia: Code de l'eau

Wallonia's water management is governed by the Code de l'eau (Water Code):

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Rainwater tank | Recommended but not always mandatory; required in many municipalities via local rules |
| Separation | New builds must separate rainwater and wastewater |
| Infiltration | Required where soil permits |
| Autonomous treatment | In zones d'assainissement autonome (no public sewer), individual treatment plants required |
| Treatment standard | D-class (nitrate/phosphate removal) for autonomous systems |

### Assainissement Zones (Wallonia)

| Zone Type | Description | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|-------------|
| Zone d'assainissement collectif | Public sewer available | Connect to public sewer |
| Zone d'assainissement autonome | No public sewer planned | Individual treatment plant (micro-station) |
| Zone d'assainissement transitoire | Public sewer planned but not yet built | Temporary autonomous treatment |

## Brussels: Urban Water Management

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Rainwater tank | Mandatory for new builds (min 33 L per m² roof) |
| Green roofs | Encouraged through premiums; may be required in PPAS zones |
| Separation | Mandatory for new builds |
| Infiltration | Required where feasible; often limited by urban soil conditions |
| Flood prevention | Strict requirements in flood-prone zones along Senne/Zenne valley |

## Sewage Connection

### General Requirements (All Regions)

| Element | Requirement |
|---------|------------|
| Connection to public sewer | Mandatory where available |
| Separate system | Rainwater and wastewater in separate pipes |
| Inspection | Camera inspection (keuring) of private sewer before connection (Flanders) |
| Backflow prevention | Recommended for basements and low-lying connections |
| Fat trap | Required for commercial kitchens; recommended for residential |

### Connection Costs

| Item | Typical Cost |
|------|-------------|
| Sewer connection (aansluiting) | EUR 500-2,000 |
| Private sewer inspection (keuring) | EUR 200-500 |
| Rainwater tank (installed) | EUR 2,000-5,000 |
| Infiltration crate system | EUR 1,500-4,000 |
| Micro-station (autonomous) | EUR 5,000-12,000 |

## Watertoets (Water Assessment)

In Flanders, every building permit application is subject to a watertoets (water assessment):

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Purpose | Evaluate impact of the project on the water system |
| Who assesses | VMM, polders & wateringen, province, or municipality depending on location |
| Flood zones | Projects in signal areas or flood-prone zones may be refused or require mitigation |
| Binding advice | In delineated flood zones, the watertoets advice can be binding |
| Online tool | waterinfo.be — check flood risk and signal areas |

## Modular Construction Water Considerations

| Aspect | Solution |
|--------|----------|
| Rainwater collection | Design roof drainage to feed underground tank; plan tank location before module placement |
| Dual plumbing | Install rainwater supply lines in factory (Flanders requirement) |
| Infiltration | Design infiltration system under parking or garden areas |
| Module placement | Ensure crane access does not damage underground water infrastructure |
| Foundation drainage | In high water table areas (common in Flanders), drainage around foundation is essential |

## ModulCA Application

- **Dual plumbing standard**: Include rainwater supply for toilets and washing machines in all Belgian modules
- **Tank sizing tool**: Calculate hemelwaterput volume based on roof area in the configurator
- **Infiltration planning**: Site layout tool should show infiltration zone requirements
- **Watertoets check**: Integrate waterinfo.be flood zone check into site feasibility assessment
- **Factory-installed pipes**: Rainwater and wastewater pipes pre-installed in modules reduce on-site work
