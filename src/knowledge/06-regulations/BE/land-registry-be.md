# Land Registry & Zoning (Belgium)

## Overview

Before building in Belgium, you must verify that the plot allows construction and understand what type of building is permitted. Belgium has a complex layered system of spatial plans, zoning designations, and building regulations that vary by region. The Kadaster (Dutch) / Cadastre (French) records property boundaries and ownership, while regional zoning plans determine permitted land use.

## The Kadaster / Cadastre System

The Belgian cadastral system is managed federally by the SPF Finances / FOD Financiën:

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Managing authority | Administration Générale de la Documentation Patrimoniale (AGDP) |
| Function | Property registration, boundaries, ownership, fiscal valuation |
| Cadastral plan | Publicly available; shows plot boundaries, numbers, and buildings |
| Kadastraal inkomen (KI) | Fiscal rental value assigned to each property — basis for property tax |
| Access | CadGIS viewer (online), municipal offices, notary records |

The cadastral plan shows plot boundaries but does NOT indicate building rights — this comes from zoning.

## Zoning Framework by Region

### Flanders

| Plan Level | Name | Authority | Function |
|-----------|------|-----------|----------|
| Regional | Gewestplan (1970s-80s) | Flemish Government | Base zoning across all of Flanders |
| Provincial | Provinciaal Ruimtelijk Structuurplan (PRS) | Province | Strategic spatial vision |
| Municipal | Gemeentelijk Ruimtelijk Structuurplan (GRS) | Municipality | Local spatial vision |
| Regulatory | Ruimtelijk Uitvoeringsplan (RUP) | Any level | Legally binding; overrides gewestplan |
| Prescriptive | Stedenbouwkundige verordening | Municipality | Building regulations (heights, setbacks) |

### Wallonia

| Plan Level | Name | Authority | Function |
|-----------|------|-----------|----------|
| Regional | Plan de secteur (1970s-80s) | Walloon Government | Base zoning across Wallonia |
| Municipal | Schéma de Développement Communal (SDC) | Municipality | Strategic spatial vision |
| Regulatory | Plan Communal d'Aménagement (PCA) | Municipality | Detailed local zoning |
| Prescriptive | Guide communal d'urbanisme | Municipality | Design guidelines |

### Brussels

| Plan Level | Name | Authority | Function |
|-----------|------|-----------|----------|
| Regional | PRAS (Plan Régional d'Affectation du Sol) | Brussels Region | Base zoning |
| Municipal | PPAS (Plan Particulier d'Affectation du Sol) | Municipality | Detailed local zoning |
| Regulatory | RRU (Règlement Régional d'Urbanisme) | Brussels Region | Building regulations |

## Gewestplan / Plan de Secteur Zones

The base zoning plans from the 1970s-80s remain the foundation. Key zone types:

| Zone Colour | Dutch (Flanders) | French (Wallonia) | Building Allowed? |
|-------------|-------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| Red | Woongebied | Zone d'habitat | Yes — residential |
| Red hatched | Woongebied met landelijk karakter | Zone d'habitat à caractère rural | Yes — lower density |
| Orange | Woonuitbreidingsgebied | Zone d'aménagement communal concerté | Conditional — needs social need proof |
| Purple | Industriegebied | Zone d'activité économique industrielle | Industrial only |
| Green | Natuurgebied | Zone naturelle | No construction |
| Yellow | Agrarisch gebied | Zone agricole | Agricultural buildings only |
| Blue | Recreatiegebied | Zone de loisirs | Recreational buildings |

## How to Check Building Rights

Step-by-step process to verify if a plot can be built on:

| Step | Action | Source |
|------|--------|--------|
| 1 | Check cadastral plan for plot boundaries | CadGIS.be / notary |
| 2 | Verify zone on gewestplan/plan de secteur | Geopunt.be (Flanders) / Géoportail (Wallonia) / MyBruGIS (Brussels) |
| 3 | Check for RUP/PCA/PPAS overlays | Municipal urban planning department |
| 4 | Request stedenbouwkundig uittreksel / extrait urbanistique | Municipality (official document confirming zoning) |
| 5 | Check for protection orders | Heritage, nature, landscape |
| 6 | Verify rooilijn (building line) and bouwvoorschriften | Municipal building regulations |
| 7 | Pre-consultation with municipality | Recommended before purchase |

## Stedenbouwkundig Uittreksel / Extrait Urbanistique

This is the essential document that summarizes a plot's legal status:

| Information Included | Detail |
|---------------------|--------|
| Zoning designation | Current zone on gewestplan/plan de secteur |
| Applicable RUP/PCA | Any detailed plans that override base zoning |
| Building regulations | Height, setback, density rules |
| Existing permits | Previous building or environmental permits |
| Pre-emption rights | Whether the government has first-purchase rights |
| Heritage status | Protected buildings or landscapes |
| Environmental constraints | Flood zones, contaminated soil records |

Cost: typically EUR 50-150, issued within 30 days. Mandatory for any property sale.

## Building Regulations (Typical Municipal)

While varying by municipality, common building prescriptions include:

| Parameter | Typical Range |
|-----------|---------------|
| Front setback (voorbouwlijn) | 0-6 m from road |
| Side setback | 0 m (halfopen) or 3+ m (open) |
| Max building height | 6-12 m (2-3 floors residential) |
| Max building depth | 12-15 m from front facade |
| Garden ratio | 30-50% of plot must remain unbuilt |
| Parking | 1-2 spaces per dwelling |

## Building Typologies

Belgium has specific plot typologies that affect modular design:

| Type | Dutch | French | Description |
|------|-------|--------|-------------|
| Open | Open bebouwing | Construction ouverte | Detached, setbacks on all sides |
| Half-open | Halfopen bebouwing | Construction semi-ouverte | Attached on one side |
| Closed | Gesloten bebouwing | Construction fermée | Row housing, attached both sides |

## Property Purchase Process

| Step | Action | Duration |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Agree on price (compromis / onderhandse overeenkomst) | — |
| 2 | Notary prepares deed | 4 months max |
| 3 | Notary checks cadastre, zoning, soil, liens | During preparation |
| 4 | Sign authentic deed (authentieke akte) | At notary |
| 5 | Register with Kadaster | Immediate |
| 6 | Pay registration tax (6% Flanders, 12.5% Wallonia/Brussels) | At deed signing |

## ModulCA Application

- **Pre-purchase check**: Always verify zoning before committing to a plot — use the online viewers (Geopunt, Géoportail, MyBruGIS) for initial screening
- **Plot size**: Belgian plots are often narrow (5-8 m wide in closed/half-open); modular designs should accommodate 5.5 m and 7 m width modules
- **Building line**: Strict front setback rules mean the module placement is constrained — verify rooilijn early
- **Woonuitbreidingsgebied**: These reserve zones require proof of social need; avoid for private projects
- **Registration tax**: Factor into total project cost — significantly varies between regions
