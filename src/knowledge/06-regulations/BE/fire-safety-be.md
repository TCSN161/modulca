# Fire Safety Norms (Belgium)

## Overview

Belgian fire safety is regulated at the federal level through the Royal Decree of 7 July 1994 (Arrêté Royal / Koninklijk Besluit), which sets minimum requirements for new buildings. The decree classifies buildings by height and occupancy and defines fire resistance, compartmentation, evacuation, and detection requirements. Additional requirements may apply at the regional or municipal level, and the fire brigade (brandweer / service d'incendie) issues binding advice during the permit process.

## Building Classification

The AR 7/7/1994 classifies buildings into three height categories:

| Category | Height | Designation | Typical Use |
|----------|--------|-------------|-------------|
| Low-rise | h < 10 m | Laag / Bas | Single-family, small apartments |
| Medium-rise | 10 m <= h < 25 m | Middelhoog / Moyen | Apartment blocks |
| High-rise | h >= 25 m | Hoog / Élevé | Towers, large residential |

Most modular residential projects fall under low-rise, which has the least demanding requirements but still mandates fire resistance, detection, and evacuation provisions.

## Fire Resistance Requirements

Fire resistance is expressed in minutes (Rf or REI notation under Eurocodes):

| Element | Low-Rise (<10m) | Medium-Rise (10-25m) | High-Rise (>=25m) |
|---------|-----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Load-bearing structure | R 30 | R 60 | R 120 |
| Floor between units | REI 30 | REI 60 | REI 120 |
| Compartment walls | EI 30 | EI 60 | EI 120 |
| Doors in compartment walls | EI1 30 | EI1 30 | EI1 60 |
| Facade cladding | Class B-s3,d1 | Class B-s2,d0 | Class A2-s2,d0 |

## Compartmentation

Buildings must be divided into fire compartments:

- **Maximum compartment area**: 2,500 m² (general rule)
- **Each dwelling unit** forms its own compartment in multi-family buildings
- **Corridors and stairwells** must be separate compartments
- **Technical shafts** (HVAC, plumbing) must be fire-stopped at each floor

For modular construction, compartmentation is often achieved at the module boundary. Joints between modules must be sealed with fire-rated intumescent strips or mineral wool to prevent fire and smoke spread.

## Escape Routes

| Requirement | Low-Rise | Medium-Rise | High-Rise |
|-------------|----------|-------------|-----------|
| Minimum exits per compartment | 1 | 2 | 2 |
| Max distance to exit | 30 m (dead-end) / 45 m (two directions) | 30 m / 45 m | 30 m / 45 m |
| Stair width (min) | 0.80 m | 1.00 m | 1.20 m |
| Corridor width (min) | 1.00 m | 1.20 m | 1.20 m |
| Emergency lighting | Required | Required | Required |

## Detection and Alarm

- **Smoke detectors**: Mandatory in all dwellings across Belgium (Flanders: Decree of 1 June 2012; Wallonia: Decree of 2003; Brussels: Ordnance of 2004)
- **Type**: Optical smoke detectors compliant with NBN EN 14604
- **Placement**: At least one per floor, in hallways and near bedrooms
- **Multi-family buildings**: Central detection system with alarm transmission for medium-rise and high-rise

## Timber and Modular-Specific Considerations

Timber-frame and modular buildings face additional scrutiny:

- **Exposed timber** is generally not permitted in escape routes or common areas for medium- and high-rise
- **CLT and glulam** can achieve R 60 through charring calculations per NBN EN 1995-1-2
- **Protective cladding**: One or two layers of fire-rated gypsum board (Type F, 12.5-15 mm) are standard to protect timber frames
- **Facade materials**: Must meet reaction-to-fire classification; timber cladding typically requires fire breaks every two floors and non-combustible cavity barriers
- **Module-to-module joints**: Critical fire path — intumescent seals and mineral wool packing are required to maintain compartment integrity

## Regional Additions

| Region | Additional Requirement |
|--------|----------------------|
| Flanders | Brandpreventiedossier required with permit application; brandweeradvies (fire brigade advice) is binding |
| Wallonia | Service Régional d'Incendie reviews plans; rapport de prévention incendie required |
| Brussels | SIAMU (fire service) issues binding advice; stricter facade requirements in urban areas |

## Sprinkler and Suppression Systems

Sprinklers are not mandatory for low-rise residential in Belgium but may be required by the fire brigade for:

- Buildings with limited fire brigade access
- Mixed-use buildings (residential above commercial)
- High-rise buildings (generally mandatory)
- Underground parking garages

Voluntary installation of residential sprinklers (NBN EN 16925) can offset other requirements and enable more flexible design.

## Documentation for Permit

The fire safety dossier submitted with the building permit must include:

1. Fire safety concept note
2. Floor plans with compartment boundaries and escape routes
3. Fire resistance specifications for all structural elements
4. Detection and alarm system layout
5. Material reaction-to-fire classifications
6. Access plan for fire brigade vehicles

## ModulCA Application

- **Factory advantage**: Fire-rated assemblies can be quality-controlled in the factory, ensuring consistent fire resistance
- **Module joints**: Detailed fire-stopping at module connections must be designed and documented
- **CLT charring**: ModulCA's timber systems can demonstrate R 30 or R 60 through EN 1995-1-2 charring calculations
- **Smoke detectors**: Pre-installed in factory, reducing on-site installation errors
- **Compact designs**: Short escape distances in typical modular layouts easily meet the 30 m dead-end rule
