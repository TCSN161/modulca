# Building Permit Process (Belgium)

## Overview
Belgium's building regulations are regionalized — Flanders, Wallonia, and Brussels each have their own permit system, energy requirements, and procedures. This complexity requires understanding which region applies. For modular homes, Flanders is the most active market due to Dutch-language overlap with Netherlands and progressive building culture.

## Regional Permit Systems

| Region | Permit Name | Law | Language |
|--------|------------|-----|----------|
| Flanders | Omgevingsvergunning | Vlaamse Codex RO | Dutch |
| Wallonia | Permis d'urbanisme | CoDT | French |
| Brussels | Stedenbouwkundige vergunning / Permis d'urbanisme | CoBAT/COBAT | Dutch/French |

## Flanders Process (Most Relevant for ModulCA)

| Step | Action | Who | Duration |
|------|--------|-----|----------|
| 1 | Check gewestplan/RUP (zoning plan) | Owner/Architect | 1 week |
| 2 | Pre-consultation with municipality | Architect → Gemeente | Optional, 2-4 weeks |
| 3 | Architectural design | Architect (mandatory) | 4-8 weeks |
| 4 | Submit omgevingsvergunning | Architect → Omgevingsloket | Online portal |
| 5 | Public inquiry (openbaar onderzoek) | Municipality | 30 days |
| 6 | Advisory opinions | Brandweer, water, etc. | During inquiry |
| 7 | Decision | College van Burgemeester en Schepenen | 60-105 days |
| 8 | Appeal period | Third parties | 30 days |
| 9 | Construction | Builder | — |
| 10 | As-built attestation | Architect → Kadaster | After completion |

## Key Requirements

| Requirement | Flanders | Wallonia | Brussels |
|-------------|----------|----------|----------|
| Architect mandatory | Yes (always for new build) | Yes | Yes |
| Energy report (EPB/PEB) | EPB-verslaggever required | PEB-responsable required | PEB-responsable required |
| Soil investigation | Mandatory (grondverzet) | Varies | Varies |
| Water assessment | Watertoets (like NL) | Permis d'environnement | — |
| Safety coordinator | Mandatory during construction | Mandatory | Mandatory |

## Key Differences

| Aspect | Belgium | Netherlands | Germany | France |
|--------|---------|-------------|---------|--------|
| Regional complexity | 3 separate systems | 1 national system | 16 state codes | 1 national system |
| Architect | Always mandatory | Not always | Always (Bauvorlage.) | > 150m² |
| Permit timeline | 60-105 days | 8-26 weeks | 4-12 weeks | 2-3 months |
| Energy system | EPB/PEB (regional) | BENG (national) | GEG (national) | RE2020 (national) |
| Public inquiry | 30 days (Flanders) | Not standard | Varies | Varies |

## ModulCA Application
- **Flanders first**: Dutch-speaking, similar regulations to NL, progressive building culture — natural expansion from NL market
- **Architect always required**: Must partner with Belgian-registered architect for every project
- **EPB compliance**: ModulCA energy performance meets/exceeds Belgian EPB requirements
- **Watertoets**: Similar to Dutch system — ModulCA's rain management strategy applies
- **Small plot advantage**: Belgian plots are often small — compact modular homes fit well
