# Modular & Compact Housing (Belgium)

## Overview
Belgium, especially Flanders, faces housing affordability challenges similar to the Netherlands. Compact and modular housing is gaining policy support, though regulations still lag behind the Netherlands' flexwonen framework. Key developments include the "klein wonen" (small living) movement and growing acceptance of prefab/modular construction.

## Belgian Housing Context

| Factor | Flanders | Wallonia | Notes |
|--------|----------|----------|-------|
| Average plot size | 300-600m² | 500-1000m² | Flanders: smaller, denser |
| Average home price | €300,000-400,000 | €200,000-300,000 | Flanders significantly more expensive |
| New build rate | ~25,000/year | ~10,000/year | Flanders: higher demand |
| Modular market share | ~5-8% (growing) | ~2-3% | Flanders leads |
| Timber construction share | ~12% (residential) | ~5% | Growing in both regions |

## Klein Wonen (Compact Living) — Flanders

| Category | Definition | Status |
|----------|-----------|--------|
| Tiny house (on wheels) | Relocatable, < 30m² | Legal grey area — depends on municipality |
| Tiny house (on plot) | Fixed, < 50m² | Requires permit, EPB compliance |
| Compact home | 50-100m² | Standard regulations apply |
| ADU (bijgebouw) | Accessory dwelling unit | Possible with permit, max 40m² in some cases |
| Container housing | Repurposed containers | Must meet EPB — often challenging |

## Regulations for Modular Homes

| Requirement | Standard Home | Modular Home | Notes |
|-------------|--------------|--------------|-------|
| Building permit | Required | Required (same process) | No simplified path yet |
| EPB compliance | Full | Full | No relaxation for modular |
| Architect | Mandatory | Mandatory | Must supervise assembly too |
| Foundation | Permanent typical | Screw piles accepted | Check with municipality |
| Fire safety | Standard | Standard | No modular-specific rules |
| Acoustics | NBN S 01-400-1 | Same requirements | Module joints need attention |

## Belgian Market Opportunities

| Opportunity | Description |
|-------------|-------------|
| Aging population | Need for accessible, ground-floor living — single-story modules |
| Verdichting (densification) | Policy to build on infill plots — compact modular homes fit |
| Renovation obligation | Flanders requires energy renovation at sale — modular extension alternative |
| Social housing shortage | 170,000 households on waiting list (Flanders) — modular can deliver fast |
| Kangoeroewonen | Multi-generational living — add modules for elderly parents |

## ModulCA Application
- **Flanders first**: Dutch-speaking, progressive, small plots — ideal for compact ModulCA homes
- **Kangoeroewonen**: Add 2-3 modules as self-contained granny flat — Belgian "kangaroo living" trend
- **Plot densification**: Small Belgian plots suit the compact 3×3m module system
- **EPB compliance**: Same energy performance as NL market — no design changes needed
- **Cross-border from NL**: Once established in Netherlands, Belgian Flanders is a short hop (same language, similar regulations)
