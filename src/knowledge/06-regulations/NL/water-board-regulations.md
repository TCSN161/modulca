# Water Board Regulations — Waterschap (Netherlands)

## Overview
The Netherlands has 21 regional water boards (waterschappen) — the oldest democratic institutions in the country, predating the national government. They manage water levels, water quality, flood defense, and wastewater treatment. Any construction project must consider water management.

## Why Water Boards Matter for Construction
With much of the Netherlands below sea level, water management is critical. Building a home affects:
- Surface water drainage
- Groundwater levels
- Flood risk
- Wastewater treatment

## Watertoets (Water Assessment)
For any building permit application, a watertoets is required:

| Aspect | What's Assessed | Typical Requirement |
|--------|----------------|-------------------|
| **Rainwater** | On-site retention capacity | Retain first 20-60mm on plot |
| **Groundwater** | Impact on water table | No negative impact on neighbors |
| **Surface water** | Discharge to ditches/canals | Permit may be needed |
| **Flooding** | Building level above flood risk | Floor level above MHW (max high water) |
| **Wastewater** | Connection to sewer/treatment | Municipal sewer or own treatment |

## Rainwater Management (Hemelwaterafvoer)

| Requirement | Value | Notes |
|-------------|-------|-------|
| On-site retention | 20-60 l/m² impervious area | Varies by waterschap |
| Discharge rate | ≤ 2 l/s per 1000m² | Maximum to public system |
| Green roof credit | Counts as retention | 25-40 l/m² depending on type |
| Rainwater harvesting | Encouraged | For garden irrigation |
| Permeable paving | Encouraged | Reduces runoff |

## Groundwater

| Issue | Consideration | Notes |
|-------|--------------|-------|
| High water table | Common in western NL | 0.5-1.5m below surface |
| Basement construction | Difficult/expensive | Waterproof construction needed |
| Foundation impact | Piles don't affect water table | Screw piles: minimal disturbance |
| Drainage | May need drainage system | Check local conditions |

## Flood Risk
| Zone | Building Restriction | Notes |
|------|---------------------|-------|
| Buitendijks (outside dyke) | Floor level above MHW + margin | Flood-resistant construction |
| Binnendijks (inside dyke) | Normal construction | Protected by dykes |
| Waterkering (near dyke) | Restrictions within protection zone | No building near dyke foot |

## ModulCA Application
- **Screw pile foundations**: Minimal groundwater disturbance — positive for watertoets
- **Flat roofs**: Can incorporate green roof → counts as water retention
- **Modular rainwater system**: Rain barrel or infiltration crate per module
- **Elevated floor**: Module floor is naturally ~0.5m above ground on piles — flood resilience
- **Permeable surroundings**: Module placement leaves garden area for infiltration

## Waterschap Fees
All property owners in NL pay annual waterschap belasting (tax):
- Watersysteemheffing: €100-300/year (water management)
- Zuiveringsheffing: €150-250/year (wastewater treatment)
- Total: ~€250-550/year depending on region
