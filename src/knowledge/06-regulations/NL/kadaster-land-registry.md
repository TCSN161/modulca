# Kadaster — Land Registry (Netherlands)

## Overview
The Kadaster (Land Registry and Mapping Agency) maintains all property records in the Netherlands. Every plot of land and building is registered with boundaries, ownership, and encumbrances.

## What the Kadaster Records

| Record | Contents | Public Access |
|--------|----------|---------------|
| Ownership (eigendom) | Who owns the property | Yes (via kadaster.nl) |
| Boundaries (grenzen) | Plot boundaries and dimensions | Yes |
| Mortgage (hypotheek) | Registered mortgages | Yes |
| Easements (erfdienstbaarheid) | Rights of way, utilities | Yes |
| Building information | Type, use, construction year | Via BAG (Basisregistratie Adressen en Gebouwen) |
| WOZ value | Municipal tax valuation | Via municipality |

## Property Purchase Process

| Step | Action | Who | Cost |
|------|--------|-----|------|
| 1 | Find property | Buyer + makelaar (agent) | Agent fee |
| 2 | Due diligence | Check kadaster records | €25 per uittreksel |
| 3 | Koopovereenkomst (purchase agreement) | Buyer + seller + notaris | — |
| 4 | Bedenktijd (cooling-off period) | Buyer can withdraw | 3 days |
| 5 | Financing | Mortgage approval | Bank costs |
| 6 | Levering (transfer deed) | Notaris | Notaris fee ~€1,500 |
| 7 | Kadaster registration | Notaris submits | €78-152 |
| 8 | Overdrachtsbelasting (transfer tax) | Buyer pays | 2% (dwelling) or 10.4% (other) |

## BAG — Building Registration
All buildings in the Netherlands are registered in the BAG (Basisregistratie Adressen en Gebouwen):

| Record | When Registered | Who Registers |
|--------|----------------|---------------|
| New building | After completion (gereedmelding) | Municipality |
| Address | When building permit issued | Municipality |
| Building use | When registered | Municipality |
| Floor area | When registered | Municipality |
| Construction year | When registered | Automatically |

## Key Differences from Romania

| Aspect | Netherlands | Romania |
|--------|-------------|---------|
| System maturity | 100% digitized | Digitizing (systematic registration ongoing) |
| Public access | Full online access | Limited online |
| Registration speed | Days | 15-30 days |
| Cost | €78-152 | Varies |
| Required professional | Notaris (notary) | Notar public + topographer |
| Transparency | Very high | Improving |

## ModulCA Application
Registration of modular buildings follows the same process as traditional construction. The key steps are:
1. Obtain omgevingsvergunning → address created
2. Complete construction
3. Submit gereedmelding → BAG updated
4. Building appears in official records
