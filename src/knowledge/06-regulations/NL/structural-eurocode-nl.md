# Structural Standards — Eurocode National Annex (Netherlands)

## Overview
The Netherlands uses the Eurocodes with Dutch National Annexes (NB = Nationale Bijlage). Key differences from Romanian parameters include: no significant seismic risk, high wind loads (coastal), and unique foundation requirements due to soft soil conditions.

## Dutch National Annex Parameters

| Parameter | NL Value | RO Comparison | Notes |
|-----------|----------|---------------|-------|
| Seismic (ag) | 0.0–0.05g | 0.10–0.40g | NL: negligible seismic risk |
| Basic wind speed (vb) | 27 m/s | 25-30 m/s | NL: coastal exposure |
| Snow load (sk) | 0.7 kN/m² | 1.0-2.5 kN/m² | NL: minimal snow |
| Frost depth | 0.80m | 0.80-1.10m | Similar |
| Consequence class | CC2 (residential) | CC2 | Same |

## Wind Loading (Dominant Action in NL)

| Terrain Category | Description | Factor |
|-----------------|-------------|--------|
| 0 | Sea, open coast | Highest exposure |
| I | Open country, few obstacles | Common inland |
| II | Scattered trees/buildings | Suburban |
| III | Urban, regular buildings | Built-up areas |

Wind is typically the governing lateral load in the Netherlands (not earthquake as in Romania).

## Foundation Requirements

The Netherlands has unique soil conditions — much of the country is below sea level with soft clay and peat soils. This significantly affects foundation design.

| Foundation Type | When Used | Depth | Notes |
|----------------|----------|-------|-------|
| **Heipalen** (driven piles) | Western NL, soft soil | 10-20m | To sand/gravel bearing layer |
| **Schroefpalen** (screw piles) | Some areas | 5-15m | Less vibration, modular-friendly |
| **Funderingsplaat** (raft/slab) | Good soil areas | 0.80m+ | Eastern NL, sand soils |
| **Strokenfundering** (strip) | Good soil, light buildings | 0.80m+ | Rural eastern NL |

### Soil Conditions by Region
| Region | Soil Type | Foundation Needed |
|--------|-----------|-------------------|
| Randstad (Amsterdam, Rotterdam, Den Haag) | Soft clay/peat | Deep piles (12-20m) |
| Utrecht, Gelderland | Mixed | Moderate piles or screw piles |
| Brabant, Limburg | Sand/gravel | Strip or pad foundations |
| Friesland, Groningen | Clay over sand | Piles (8-12m) |
| Flevoland | Reclaimed land | Piles |

## ModulCA Application
Key differences for NL market vs. Romania:
- **No seismic design needed** — simplifies connections significantly
- **Wind load is critical** — modules need adequate lateral bracing
- **Deep pile foundations likely** — in western NL, screw piles may need to go 10m+ vs. 1.5-3m in Romania
- **Foundation cost is higher** — budget €15,000-30,000 for piling in Randstad
- **KOMO certification** — may be expected for timber products entering NL market
