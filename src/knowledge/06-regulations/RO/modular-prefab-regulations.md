# Modular & Prefabricated Building Regulations (Romania)

## Overview

Romania does not have a separate regulatory framework for modular or prefabricated buildings. Instead, modular construction must comply with the same building codes as traditional construction, with additional requirements for factory-produced elements. This is actually an advantage — modular homes have the same legal status as any permanent building.

## Legal Framework

### Primary Legislation

| Law | Scope | Key Requirement |
|-----|-------|----------------|
| Legea 50/1991 | Building permits | All buildings require autorizatie de construire |
| Legea 10/1995 | Construction quality | Quality assurance system applies to modular |
| HG 766/1997 | Quality regulations | Detailed implementation rules |
| Legea 372/2005 | Energy performance | Energy certificate required |
| HG 273/1994 | Construction reception | Formal handover process |

### Key Principle

**A modular building on a permanent foundation is legally identical to a traditional building.** It receives the same:
- Building permit (autorizatie de construire)
- Property registration (intabulare)
- Mortgage eligibility
- Insurance coverage
- Resale value recognition

## Permit Process for Modular Buildings

### Step 1: Urban Certificate (Certificat de Urbanism)

Identical to traditional construction:
- Apply at local primarie (town hall)
- Provides building parameters: footprint limits, height, setbacks
- Validity: 12-24 months
- **Timeline**: 15-30 days

### Step 2: Technical Documentation

Required project documentation:
1. **Architecture project** (proiect de arhitectura) — by authorized architect
2. **Structural project** (proiect de rezistenta) — by authorized structural engineer
3. **MEP project** — electrical, plumbing, HVAC
4. **Geotechnical study** (studiu geotehnic)
5. **Factory documentation** — module specifications, material certificates, quality plan

**Modular-specific addition**: The structural project must include:
- Module-to-module connection details
- Module-to-foundation interface design
- Transport and lifting analysis
- Factory quality control plan reference

### Step 3: Building Permit (Autorizatie de Construire)

Issued by local authority based on:
- Complete technical documentation
- Design verification reports (from authorized verifier)
- Utility connection agreements
- Fire safety approval (ISU aviz)
- Environmental approval (if applicable)

**Timeline**: 30 days from complete submission

### Step 4: Construction Phase

Even though modules are factory-built, on-site requirements include:
- **Site supervisor** (diriginte de santier) — mandatory for all construction
- **ISC notification** — inform State Inspectorate of construction start
- **Phase inspections**: Foundation, module placement, connections, finishing
- **Quality control documents** at each phase

### Step 5: Reception (Receptie)

Formal handover (receptie la terminarea lucrarilor):
- Reception committee: investor, designer, builder, ISC representative
- Verify compliance with approved project
- Issue reception protocol (proces-verbal de receptie)
- **Final reception** (receptie finala) after guarantee period (5 years)

## Agrement Tehnic (Technical Approval)

### When Required

An Agrement Tehnic from MDLPA may be required for:
- **Novel construction systems** not covered by existing standards
- **New materials** without harmonized European standards
- **Innovative connection methods**

### When NOT Required

Standard modular construction using:
- Steel frames per SR EN 1090 / Eurocode 3 → **No agrement needed**
- Standard insulation materials with CE marking → **No agrement needed**
- Conventional foundations → **No agrement needed**

Most modular homes in Romania do NOT require Agrement Tehnic because they use standard materials and methods, just assembled differently.

## Factory Requirements

### For Romanian Factories

- **ISC authorization** for construction product manufacturing
- **SR EN 1090-1 certification** for steel structural elements (CE marking)
- **Quality management system** (ISO 9001 recommended)
- **Factory Production Control** (FPC) per EN 1090-1

### For Imported Modules

Modules manufactured abroad and assembled in Romania must have:
- **CE marking** on structural elements
- **Declaration of Performance** (DoP) per EU Construction Products Regulation
- **Romanian-language documentation**
- Compliance verification by authorized Romanian verifier

## Common Misconceptions

### "Modular homes are temporary/mobile"

**FALSE.** A modular home on permanent foundations is a permanent building. Romanian law makes no distinction between how a building is assembled — only that it meets building codes.

### "You can't get a mortgage for modular"

**FALSE.** Banks finance modular homes the same as traditional — the building permit and property registration are identical. See our Financing article for details.

### "Modular homes depreciate faster"

**FALSE.** With proper foundations and standard materials, modular homes have the same lifespan (50+ years) and appreciation potential as traditional construction.

### "No building permit needed for small modular homes"

**PARTIALLY TRUE.** Structures under 50m2 footprint on existing property MAY qualify for simplified permit process (notificare), but most residential buildings still require full permits.

## Insurance and Warranty

### Mandatory Insurance

- **PAD** (Polita de Asigurare a Locuintelor): Mandatory home insurance covering earthquake, flood, landslide — same cost as traditional
- **Construction insurance**: Required during build phase (usually included by builder)

### Warranty Periods (Legea 10/1995)

| Component | Warranty Period |
|-----------|----------------|
| Structural elements | 10 years |
| Waterproofing | 5 years |
| MEP installations | 3 years |
| Finishes | 2 years |
| General construction | 5 years |
