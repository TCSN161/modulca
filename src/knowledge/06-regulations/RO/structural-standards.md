# Structural Standards for Modular Buildings (Romania)

## Overview

Modular steel-frame construction in Romania must comply with a layered system of European and national standards. The Eurocodes form the base, with national annexes (NA) adapting safety factors, snow loads, and seismic requirements to Romanian conditions.

## Key Standards

### CR 6-2013 — Steel Structure Design Code

The primary Romanian code for steel structures, used alongside Eurocode 3 (SR EN 1993). Specifies:

- **Steel grades**: S235, S275, S355 (most modular factories use S355 for higher strength-to-weight)
- **Connection design**: Bolted and welded joints must follow SR EN 1993-1-8
- **Corrosion protection**: Hot-dip galvanizing or equivalent for external steel elements
- **Minimum steel thickness**: 1.5mm for cold-formed sections in residential use

### P100-1/2013 — Seismic Design

Romania has significant seismic activity. Key requirements for modular buildings:

| Seismic Zone | Peak Ground Acceleration (ag) | Typical Locations |
|-------------|-------------------------------|-------------------|
| Zone 1 | 0.40g | Vrancea region, eastern Wallachia |
| Zone 2 | 0.30g | Bucharest, southern Moldavia |
| Zone 3 | 0.20g | Transylvania, Banat |
| Zone 4 | 0.15g | Western Romania, Maramures |

**Modular advantage**: Light steel-frame modules (typically 800-1200 kg/module for 3x3m) have significantly lower seismic mass than traditional masonry, reducing seismic forces by 40-60%.

### Snow and Wind Loads (CR 1-1-3 / CR 1-1-4)

- **Snow load zones**: 1.0 to 3.5 kN/m2 depending on altitude and region
- **Wind pressure**: 0.3 to 0.6 kN/m2 reference pressure
- Flat-roof modular buildings must account for snow drift accumulation at parapet edges

## Modular-Specific Considerations

### Transport Structural Integrity

Modules must maintain structural integrity during transport. Romanian road regulations limit:
- **Width**: 2.55m standard, up to 3.50m with special permit
- **Height**: 4.00m (module + trailer)
- **Length**: 16.50m for articulated trucks
- **Weight**: 40 tonnes maximum GVW

A 3x3m module fits within standard transport dimensions without special permits.

### Foundation Interface

The module-to-foundation connection must transfer:
- Vertical loads (gravity + snow)
- Horizontal loads (seismic + wind)
- Uplift forces (wind suction on lightweight roofs)

Common solutions in Romania:
1. **Screw piles** (most popular for modular) — minimal site disruption, reversible
2. **Strip foundations** — traditional, required on expansive clay soils
3. **Raft foundations** — for poor soil conditions or >2 story buildings

### Inter-Module Connections

Module-to-module structural connections must be:
- **Demountable** (for future reconfiguration)
- **Seismically qualified** per P100-1
- **Thermally broken** to prevent cold bridging
- **Documented** in the structural engineer's calculation note (nota de calcul)

## Required Documentation

For structural approval in Romania, you need:
1. **Structural calculation note** (nota de calcul) — by authorized structural engineer (MLPAT attestation)
2. **Steel quality certificates** (certificate de calitate) for all structural elements
3. **Welding inspection reports** (if factory-welded connections)
4. **Foundation geotechnical report** (studiu geotehnic) — mandatory for all buildings
