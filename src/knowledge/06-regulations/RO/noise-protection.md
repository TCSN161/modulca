# Noise Protection Standards for Modular Buildings (Romania)

## Overview

Romanian noise protection requirements for residential buildings are governed by C125-2013 (Normativ privind cerinte acustice ale cladirilor). Modular construction presents unique acoustic challenges due to lightweight wall assemblies, but properly designed steel-frame modules can exceed traditional masonry performance.

## Required Performance Levels

### Airborne Sound Insulation (DnT,w)

| Partition Type | Minimum DnT,w | Recommended for Comfort |
|---------------|---------------|------------------------|
| Between apartments | 52 dB | 56+ dB |
| Between rooms (same dwelling) | 42 dB | 45+ dB |
| Bedroom from corridor | 48 dB | 52+ dB |
| From exterior (quiet area) | 30 dB | 35+ dB |
| From exterior (noisy road) | 35-40 dB | 42+ dB |

### Impact Sound Insulation (L'nT,w)

| Floor Type | Maximum L'nT,w | Recommended |
|-----------|---------------|-------------|
| Between apartments | 58 dB | 52 dB or less |
| Bedroom above living room | 53 dB | 48 dB or less |

## Modular Wall Acoustic Performance

### ModulCA Standard Wall Buildup (20cm)

The standard 20cm exterior wall achieves:
- **Rw = 48-52 dB** (airborne sound reduction index)
- Layers: 15mm cladding + 25mm air gap + 80mm mineral wool + 60mm steel frame + 15mm interior board

### ModulCA Enhanced Wall (30cm)

The enhanced 30cm wall with additional insulation:
- **Rw = 55-60 dB** (significantly exceeds requirements)
- Layers: 20mm cladding + 25mm air gap + 120mm mineral wool + 80mm SIP + 5mm vapour barrier + 15mm interior + 35mm additional insulation

### Interior Shared Walls (15cm)

Between modules within the same dwelling:
- **Rw = 38-42 dB** (meets intra-dwelling requirements)
- Layers: 12.5mm gypsum + 80mm steel frame + 40mm mineral wool + 12.5mm gypsum

## Key Acoustic Strategies for Modular Buildings

### 1. Mass-Spring-Mass Principle

Lightweight steel-frame walls achieve good sound insulation through the mass-spring-mass principle:
- Two rigid layers (gypsum boards) separated by an elastic layer (mineral wool)
- The air cavity and mineral wool act as the "spring"
- Adding a second layer of gypsum board (+3-6 dB improvement)

### 2. Avoiding Flanking Transmission

The biggest acoustic risk in modular construction is **flanking paths** — sound traveling through the structure rather than through the wall:

- **Module joints**: Use resilient gaskets (EPDM or neoprene) at all module-to-module connections
- **Floor-wall junctions**: Decouple floor from wall structure using resilient channels
- **Service penetrations**: Seal all pipe and cable penetrations with acoustic sealant
- **Steel frame**: Use resilient clips or hat channels to decouple gypsum from steel studs

### 3. Floor Impact Noise

For upper-story modules:
- **Floating floor**: 18mm OSB on 25mm impact foam over structural deck
- **Suspended ceiling**: 12.5mm gypsum on resilient channels (below the upper module)
- Combined improvement: 15-25 dB reduction in impact noise

## Testing and Certification

### Required Tests

For residential buildings in Romania:
1. **In-situ measurement** of DnT,w between dwellings (per SR EN ISO 16283-1)
2. **Impact sound measurement** for multi-story (per SR EN ISO 16283-2)
3. **Facade sound insulation** in noisy environments (per SR EN ISO 16283-3)

### Factory Testing Advantage

Modular factories can test wall assemblies in laboratory conditions (per SR EN ISO 10140), providing reliable Rw values. This is more predictable than site-built construction where quality varies.

| Issue | Solution | Cost Impact |
|-------|----------|-------------|
