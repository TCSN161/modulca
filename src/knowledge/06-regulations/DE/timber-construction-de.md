# Timber Construction Regulations (Germany)

## Overview
Germany has rapidly liberalized timber construction rules. Historically restricted to low-rise (Gebäudeklasse 1-3), most states now allow timber up to Gebäudeklasse 4-5 (up to 13m building height / 7 stories). Germany is Europe's largest timber construction market and a major CLT producer (with Austria).

## Gebäudeklassen (Building Classes) and Timber

| Class | Height | Floors | Timber Allowed | Fire Requirements |
|-------|--------|--------|---------------|-------------------|
| GK 1 | Free-standing, ≤ 7m | 1-2 | Fully allowed | Basic |
| GK 2 | Free-standing, ≤ 7m | 1-2 | Fully allowed | Basic |
| GK 3 | ≤ 7m, multiple units | 1-2 | Fully allowed | REI 30-60 |
| GK 4 | ≤ 13m | 3-5 | Allowed (most states) | REI 60-90, encapsulation |
| GK 5 | > 13m | 5+ | Allowed in some states (BaWü, Hamburg, Berlin) | REI 90+, sprinklers |

## Fire Protection for Timber (Muster-Holzbaurichtlinie)

| Concept | Description | Application |
|---------|-------------|-------------|
| Encapsulation (Kapselung) | Timber covered with non-combustible boards | GK 4-5: K₂60 required |
| K₂60 | 60 min. protection before timber ignites | 2× 18mm gypsum board |
| Exposed timber (sichtbar) | Timber visible without cladding | GK 1-3 generally allowed |
| Brandwand | Fire wall between units | Non-combustible, or timber with K₂90 |
| Sprinkler trade-off | Sprinklers allow reduced requirements | Some states allow exposed timber in GK 4 with sprinklers |

## German Timber Construction Market

| Statistic | Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------|-------|
| Market share (new residential) | ~20-22% (2024) | Growing steadily |
| Timber-frame homes/year | ~20,000 | Single-family mainly |
| CLT production (DE+AT) | ~2.5 million m³/year | Europe's largest production region |
| Prefab timber share | ~15% of all new homes | Higher in southern Germany |
| Average timber home cost | €2,000-3,000/m² | Turnkey, regional variation |

## Key Differences from Other Markets

| Aspect | Germany | Netherlands | Romania |
|--------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Timber building culture | Very strong (especially south) | Growing, modular focus | Traditional (Maramureș), modern: emerging |
| Max height (timber) | Up to 7 stories (some states) | No specific limit (meets BBL) | 2-3 stories typical |
| Fire approach | Encapsulation (Kapselung) | BBL fire requirements | P118 fire code |
| Prefab acceptance | Very high | High (flexwonen) | Growing |
| Cost vs. masonry | Competitive to slightly higher | Competitive | Often lower than masonry |
