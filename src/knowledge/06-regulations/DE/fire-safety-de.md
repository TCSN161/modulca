# Fire Safety Regulations (Germany)

## Overview
German fire safety (Brandschutz) is governed primarily through the Musterbauordnung (MBO) and its state-level equivalents (Landesbauordnungen). The MBO dedicates §§26-32 to fire protection, covering structural fire resistance, building materials, escape routes, and fire compartments. For timber and modular buildings, additional guidance comes from the Muster-Holzbaurichtlinie (M-HBauRL) and the Muster-Richtlinie über brandschutztechnische Anforderungen (MLAR) for technical building systems.

## Fire Resistance Classes (Feuerwiderstand)

Germany uses both the traditional DIN 4102 classification and the European DIN EN 13501 system.

### DIN 4102 Fire Resistance Ratings

| Class | Duration | Typical Application |
|-------|----------|-------------------|
| F 30 | 30 minutes | Gebäudeklasse 1-2 (low-rise residential) |
| F 60 | 60 minutes | Gebäudeklasse 3 (multi-unit, ≤ 7m) |
| F 90 | 90 minutes | Gebäudeklasse 4-5 (multi-story) |
| F 120 | 120 minutes | Special structures, high-rise (Hochhaus) |

### European Classification (DIN EN 13501-2)

| Symbol | Meaning |
|--------|---------|
| R | Load-bearing capacity (Tragfähigkeit) |
| E | Integrity — no flame/gas passage (Raumabschluss) |
| I | Insulation — temperature limit on unexposed side |
| REI 60 | Load-bearing + integrity + insulation for 60 min |
| EI 30 | Non-load-bearing partition with 30 min protection |

## Building Material Classes (DIN 4102-1 / EN 13501-1)

| DIN 4102 | EN 13501 | Description | Examples |
|----------|----------|-------------|----------|
| A1 | A1 | Non-combustible, no organic content | Concrete, steel, stone |
| A2 | A2-s1,d0 | Non-combustible, limited organic | Gypsum fibreboard, mineral wool |
| B1 | B-s1,d0 | Flame-retardant (schwerentflammbar) | Treated timber cladding, fire-retardant panels |
| B2 | C/D/E | Normal combustibility (normalentflammbar) | Untreated wood, standard insulation |
| B3 | F | Easily combustible (leichtentflammbar) | Not permitted in buildings |

## Fire Requirements by Gebäudeklasse

| Gebäudeklasse | Load-bearing Walls | Floors/Ceilings | Compartment Walls | Stairway Walls |
|---------------|-------------------|------------------|--------------------|----------------|
| GK 1 | No requirement | No requirement | — | — |
| GK 2 | F 30 / REI 30 | F 30 / REI 30 | — | F 30 |
| GK 3 | F 30 / REI 30 | F 30 / REI 30 | F 30 | F 30 |
| GK 4 | F 60 / REI 60 | F 60 / REI 60 | F 60 (non-combustible) | F 90 (A) |
| GK 5 | F 90 / REI 90 | F 90 / REI 90 | F 90 (A) | F 90 (A) |

Note: "(A)" means non-combustible material required (Baustoffklasse A).

## Escape Routes (Rettungswege) — MBO §33-35

Every residential unit must have two independent escape routes (Rettungswege):

| Route | Requirements | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| First escape route | Via stairway (notwendiger Treppenraum) | Max. 35m from apartment door to building exit |
| Second escape route | Window accessible by fire service ladder OR second stairway | Rescue window: min. 0.90m × 1.20m, max. sill height 1.20m above floor |
| Corridor width | ≥ 1.20m (GK 3-5) | ≥ 1.00m acceptable in GK 1-2 |
| Stairway width | ≥ 1.00m (clear) | ≥ 1.20m for buildings with > 2 apartments per floor |
| Door swing | Escape doors swing outward | In direction of escape |

### Rescue Window (Rettungsfenster)
For buildings up to Gebäudeklasse 4, the second escape route is typically a window reachable by fire service ladder (Hubrettungsfahrzeug). Requirements:
- Minimum opening: 0.90m wide x 1.20m high
- Maximum sill height above floor: 1.20m
- Maximum height above ground: varies by state, typically ≤ 8m for standard ladder reach

## Fire Compartments (Brandabschnitte)

| Element | Requirement | Notes |
|---------|-------------|-------|
| Brandwand (fire wall) | REI 90 non-combustible or REI-M 90 | Required every 40m of building length |
| Wohnungstrennwand | F 30 to F 60 depending on GK | Between dwelling units |
| Decke (floor/ceiling) | REI per Gebäudeklasse | Must prevent fire spread between stories |
| Durchführungen (penetrations) | Fire-rated sealing required | Pipes, cables, ducts through fire-rated elements |
| Installationsschacht | F 30 to F 90 enclosure | Vertical service shafts |

## Fire Safety Concept (Brandschutzkonzept)

For GK 4-5 and special buildings, a formal fire safety concept prepared by a Brandschutzplaner (fire safety engineer) is required. This document covers:

- Structural fire protection measures
- Technical systems (smoke detectors, sprinklers if required)
- Organizational measures (escape plans, maintenance)
- Fire service access (Flächen für die Feuerwehr)

## Smoke Detectors (Rauchwarnmelder)

All 16 German states now mandate smoke detectors in residential buildings:
- Required in: bedrooms, children's rooms, corridors serving as escape routes
- Standard: DIN 14676 (installation) and DIN EN 14604 (device)
- Responsibility: owner installs, tenant maintains (varies by state)

## Special Considerations for Modular/Timber Buildings

| Topic | Requirement | Notes |
|-------|-------------|-------|
| CLT walls GK 1-3 | Exposed timber permitted | No encapsulation needed |
| CLT walls GK 4 | K260 encapsulation | 2x 18mm gypsum fibreboard over CLT |
| Module joints | Fire-rated sealing at all joints | Critical for modular: factory joints + site joints |
| Facade cladding | B1 minimum for GK 4-5 | Timber cladding with fire retardant or A2 cladding |
| Cavity barriers | Firestops in facade cavities | Prevent hidden fire spread (Hinterlüftung) |

## ModulCA Application
- **GK 1-2 simplicity**: Single-family ModulCA homes face minimal fire requirements — no encapsulation, no fire safety concept
- **Factory fire sealing**: Module-to-module fire-rated joints can be quality-controlled in the factory — consistent Brandschutz
- **CLT fire performance**: CLT chars predictably at ~0.7mm/min — inherent fire resistance simplifies REI calculations
- **Smoke detectors included**: ModulCA modules can be delivered with DIN 14676 smoke detectors pre-installed
- **Scalable to GK 4**: Multi-family modular buildings achievable with K260 encapsulation — pre-applied in factory
