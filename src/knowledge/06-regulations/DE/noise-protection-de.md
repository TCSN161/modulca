# Noise Protection — Schallschutz (Germany)

## Overview
Sound insulation (Schallschutz) in German buildings is governed by DIN 4109, which defines minimum requirements for airborne and impact sound insulation between dwelling units and from external noise. DIN 4109 is referenced in all state building codes and is mandatory. VDI 4100 provides voluntary higher comfort levels (Schallschutzstufen SSt I-III) that go beyond the legal minimum. For modular/timber buildings, achieving adequate sound insulation is a critical design challenge due to the lower mass of timber compared to concrete.

## Regulatory Framework

| Standard | Scope | Status |
|----------|-------|--------|
| DIN 4109-1:2018 | Minimum sound insulation requirements | Mandatory (referenced in LBOs) |
| DIN 4109-2:2018 | Verification methods (calculation) | Mandatory for proof |
| DIN 4109-31 to -36 | Input data for different construction types | Supporting data |
| VDI 4100:2012 | Enhanced comfort levels (SSt I-III) | Voluntary — market expectation |
| TA Lärm | External industrial/commercial noise protection | Mandatory for mixed-use areas |
| DIN EN ISO 717-1 | Rating of airborne sound insulation (Rw) | Test standard |
| DIN EN ISO 717-2 | Rating of impact sound insulation (Ln,w) | Test standard |

## Sound Insulation Concepts

| Concept | German Term | Symbol | Unit | Direction |
|---------|------------|--------|------|-----------|
| Airborne sound insulation | Luftschalldämmung | R'w | dB | Voices, music, TV through walls/floors |
| Impact sound level | Trittschallpegel | L'n,w | dB | Footsteps, dropped objects through floors |
| Facade sound insulation | Schalldämmung Außenbauteile | R'w,res | dB | Traffic, outside noise through facade |

Note: The apostrophe (') indicates field measurement values (including flanking transmission). Lower L'n,w = better impact sound insulation. Higher R'w = better airborne sound insulation.

## Minimum Requirements — DIN 4109-1 (Residential)

### Between Dwelling Units (Wohnungstrennbauteile)

| Element | R'w (Airborne) | L'n,w (Impact) |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Walls between dwellings | ≥ 53 dB | — |
| Floors between dwellings | ≥ 54 dB | ≤ 50 dB |
| Walls to stairways | ≥ 53 dB | — |
| Floors to stairways | ≥ 54 dB | ≤ 50 dB |
| Doors to stairways | ≥ 27 dB | — |

### Within a Dwelling (Wohnungsinterne Bauteile)

| Element | R'w (Airborne) | L'n,w (Impact) |
|---------|----------------|----------------|
| Walls between rooms | No requirement (DIN 4109) | — |
| Floors between stories (same unit) | No formal requirement | Recommended ≤ 53 dB |

### From Building Services (Haustechnische Anlagen)

| Source | Maximum Level in Protected Rooms | Notes |
|--------|--------------------------------|-------|
| Continuously operating systems (ventilation, heat pumps) | ≤ 30 dB(A) | LAF,max,n |
| Intermittent systems (elevator, water pipes) | ≤ 30 dB(A) | LAF,max,n |
| Toilet flushing (in neighboring unit) | ≤ 30 dB(A) | LAF,max,n |

## Enhanced Comfort Levels — VDI 4100

VDI 4100 defines three Schallschutzstufen (SSt) above DIN 4109 minimums:

### Airborne Sound (R'w between dwellings)

| Element | DIN 4109 Min. | SSt I | SSt II | SSt III |
|---------|--------------|-------|--------|---------|
| Walls between dwellings | 53 dB | 56 dB | 59 dB | ≥ 62 dB |
| Floors between dwellings | 54 dB | 56 dB | 59 dB | ≥ 62 dB |

### Impact Sound (L'n,w between dwellings)

| Element | DIN 4109 Min. | SSt I | SSt II | SSt III |
|---------|--------------|-------|--------|---------|
| Floors between dwellings | ≤ 50 dB | ≤ 46 dB | ≤ 39 dB | ≤ 34 dB |

### Building Services Noise

| Source | DIN 4109 Min. | SSt I | SSt II | SSt III |
|--------|--------------|-------|--------|---------|
| Continuous systems | ≤ 30 dB(A) | ≤ 28 dB(A) | ≤ 25 dB(A) | ≤ 22 dB(A) |
| Intermittent systems | ≤ 30 dB(A) | ≤ 28 dB(A) | ≤ 25 dB(A) | ≤ 22 dB(A) |

**Market expectation**: In premium German housing, VDI 4100 SSt II (or at least SSt I) is commonly expected. Failure to achieve this can lead to buyer complaints and legal disputes under BGB (civil code) construction defect law, even if DIN 4109 minimums are technically met.

## Facade Sound Insulation (External Noise)

Required facade insulation depends on the external noise level (Lärmpegelbereich):

| Lärmpegelbereich | External Level dB(A) | Required R'w,res of Facade |
|-----------------|---------------------|---------------------------|
| I | ≤ 55 | 30 dB |
| II | 56-60 | 30 dB |
| III | 61-65 | 35 dB |
| IV | 66-70 | 40 dB |
| V | 71-75 | 45 dB |
| VI | 76-80 | 50 dB |

External noise levels are determined from Lärmkarten (noise maps), which are publicly available for most German municipalities online.

## Timber and Modular Construction Challenges

Timber buildings inherently have less mass than concrete or masonry, making sound insulation more challenging.

### Typical Solutions for Timber/Modular

| Challenge | Solution | Notes |
|-----------|----------|-------|
| Low mass for airborne insulation | Double-leaf wall construction with decoupled layers | Avoid rigid connections between leaves |
| Impact sound through CLT floors | Floating screed (Estrich auf Trennlage) + resilient layer | 30-50mm screed on 20-30mm mineral wool |
| Flanking transmission | Elastic bearings at module joints (Elastomerlager) | Critical in modular: module-to-module transfer |
| Building services noise | Decoupled pipe runs, flexible connections | Anti-vibration mounts for heat pumps |
| Low-frequency noise | Heavy mass layers (sand fill, concrete topping) | Add mass to CLT floors for bass frequencies |

### Achievable Performance in Timber

| Assembly | Typical R'w | Typical L'n,w | Notes |
|----------|------------|---------------|-------|
| Single CLT wall (100mm) | 33-36 dB | — | Insufficient between dwellings |
| Double CLT wall + cavity | 55-62 dB | — | Meets DIN 4109 + VDI SSt I-II |
| CLT floor (140mm) bare | 36-38 dB / 72-78 dB | — | Insufficient for both |
| CLT floor + floating screed + ceiling | 56-62 dB / 38-48 dB | — | Meets DIN 4109, SSt I-II achievable |
| CLT floor + heavy fill + screed + ceiling | 60-65 dB / 32-40 dB | — | SSt II-III achievable |

## Verification Methods

| Method | Description | When Used |
|--------|-------------|-----------|
| DIN 4109-2 (calculation) | Analytical prediction from assembly data | Building permit application |
| DIN 4109-31 to -36 | Catalog of tested assemblies with known values | Design reference |
| DIN EN ISO 140 / 16283 | Field measurement (Baumessung) | Post-construction verification |
| Prognose-Gutachten | Expert acoustic assessment | Complex buildings, GK 4-5 |

## ModulCA Application
- **Assembly development**: ModulCA must develop and test standard wall/floor assemblies that reliably meet DIN 4109 + VDI SSt I
- **Floating screed in factory**: Factory-applied floating screed on CLT floors ensures consistent acoustic performance
- **Module joint detailing**: Elastic bearings (Elastomerlager) between modules prevent flanking noise — a key advantage of factory precision
- **Heavy floor option**: Offer an acoustic upgrade package with sand/gravel fill or thin concrete topping for SSt II-III
- **Heat pump isolation**: Pre-installed anti-vibration mounts for air-source heat pumps prevent structure-borne noise complaints
- **Acoustic testing**: Test standard ModulCA assemblies per DIN EN ISO 10140 and publish verified R'w / L'n,w values — credibility for German market
