# Water Supply and Drainage Regulations (Germany)

## Overview
Water supply and wastewater drainage in Germany are regulated at federal, state, and municipal levels. The Wasserhaushaltsgesetz (WHG — Federal Water Act) sets the framework, while municipalities govern local connections through their Abwassersatzung (wastewater bylaws). Germany increasingly promotes on-site rainwater management (Regenwasserbewirtschaftung) through split wastewater fees (Gesplittete Abwassergebühr), making sustainable drainage design financially relevant.

## Regulatory Framework

| Level | Legislation | Scope |
|-------|------------|-------|
| Federal | WHG (Wasserhaushaltsgesetz) | Water protection, wastewater principles |
| Federal | TrinkwV (Trinkwasserverordnung) | Drinking water quality |
| Federal | AbwV (Abwasserverordnung) | Wastewater discharge limits |
| State | Landeswassergesetz (LWG) | State-specific water regulations |
| Municipal | Abwassersatzung | Local connection rules, fees |
| Municipal | Entwässerungssatzung | Drainage requirements |

## Water Supply Connection (Trinkwasseranschluss)

### Connection Process

| Step | Action | Responsible | Typical Cost |
|------|--------|-------------|-------------|
| 1 | Apply for connection | Owner to Wasserversorger (water utility) | — |
| 2 | Connection assessment | Utility engineer | Free or small fee |
| 3 | Hausanschluss (house connection) | Licensed installer (Wasserversorger-approved) | 2,000-5,000 EUR |
| 4 | Water meter installation | Wasserversorger | Included in connection fee |
| 5 | Internal plumbing | Licensed SHK-Fachbetrieb | Part of construction cost |
| 6 | Pressure test + inspection | SHK-Fachbetrieb + utility | Before commissioning |

### Key Technical Requirements

| Parameter | Requirement | Standard |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Supply pressure | 2.0-6.0 bar (typically 3-4 bar) | DIN 1988 (TRWI) |
| Pipe material (house connection) | PE-HD, copper, or stainless steel | DIN 1988 / DIN 50930 |
| Backflow prevention | Required at building entry | DIN EN 1717 |
| Water meter location | Hausanschlussraum (utility room) | DIN 1988-200 |
| Hot water preparation | ≥ 60 degrees C at heater (Legionella prevention) | TrinkwV / DVGW W 551 |
| Circulation system | Required if pipe length > 3 liters volume to tap | DVGW W 551 |

## Wastewater Drainage (Entwässerung)

Germany distinguishes between Schmutzwasser (foul water/sewage) and Niederschlagswasser (rainwater/stormwater). Many municipalities require or incentivize separate handling.

### Drainage Systems

| System | German Term | Description |
|--------|------------|-------------|
| Combined system | Mischsystem | Foul water + rainwater in one pipe |
| Separate system | Trennsystem | Separate pipes for foul and rain water |
| On-site infiltration | Versickerung | Rainwater soaks into ground on-site |
| On-site retention | Rückhaltung | Rainwater stored and released slowly |

### Foul Water Drainage (Schmutzwasser)

| Parameter | Requirement | Standard |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Pipe material | PP, PE-HD, PVC, stoneware | DIN EN 12056 / DIN 1986-100 |
| Minimum gradient (ground-level) | 1:DN (1% for DN 100) | DIN 1986-100 |
| Minimum pipe diameter | DN 100 (single dwelling) | DIN 1986-100 |
| Inspection chamber (Revisionsschacht) | At property boundary + direction changes | DIN 1986-100 |
| Grundleitungsprüfung (drain test) | Mandatory before backfill | DIN 1610 / EN 1610 |
| Rückstausicherung (backflow protection) | Required for fixtures below street level | DIN EN 12056-4 |

### Rainwater Drainage (Niederschlagswasser)

| Option | Description | When Required |
|--------|-------------|--------------|
| Public sewer discharge | Rainwater to municipal storm drain | Where permitted by municipality |
| On-site infiltration (Versickerung) | Percolation via soakaway, swale, or trench | Increasingly required, especially new B-Plans |
| Retention + throttled discharge | Stored on-site, released at limited rate | Dense urban areas with capacity limits |
| Rainwater harvesting (Regenwassernutzung) | Collection for toilet flushing, garden use | Voluntary, reduces Niederschlagswassergebühr |

### Infiltration Design (DIN 4261 / DWA-A 138)

| Method | German Term | Typical Application |
|--------|------------|-------------------|
| Swale (Mulde) | Muldenversickerung | Surface depression in garden — cheapest |
| Trench (Rigole) | Rigolenversickerung | Underground gravel-filled trench |
| Swale-trench combination | Mulden-Rigolen-Element | Surface swale over underground storage |
| Soakaway (Schacht) | Schachtversickerung | Vertical shaft for small plots |
| Permeable paving | Durchlässige Befestigung | Driveways, parking — reduces sealed surface |

Design is based on local rainfall intensity (Regenspende) from KOSTRA-DWD-2020 dataset (statistical rainfall data for Germany).

## Split Wastewater Fee (Gesplittete Abwassergebühr)

Most German municipalities now charge separate fees for:

| Fee Component | Basis | Typical Cost |
|---------------|-------|-------------|
| Schmutzwassergebühr (foul water) | Freshwater consumption (m3) | 1.50-4.00 EUR/m3 |
| Niederschlagswassergebühr (rainwater) | Sealed surface area (m2) | 0.50-2.00 EUR/m2/year |

Reducing sealed surface area or managing rainwater on-site directly reduces the Niederschlagswassergebühr. This creates a financial incentive for:
- Green roofs (Dachbegrünung) — typically 50% fee reduction
- Permeable paving — up to 100% fee reduction for that area
- Rainwater harvesting — partial reduction in some municipalities
- On-site infiltration — up to 100% fee reduction

## Connection Fees (Erschließungsbeiträge)

| Fee Type | Typical Cost Range | Basis |
|----------|-------------------|-------|
| Kanalanschlussbeitrag (sewer connection) | 2,000-8,000 EUR | Plot size + building size |
| Wasseranschlussbeitrag (water connection) | 1,500-5,000 EUR | Plot size |
| Baukosten Hausanschluss (physical connection) | 3,000-8,000 EUR | Length of connection pipe |

These are one-time fees paid to the municipality when connecting a new building.

## Approval Process

| Step | Action | Authority |
|------|--------|-----------|
| 1 | Entwässerungsgesuch (drainage application) | Submit to municipality with Bauantrag |
| 2 | Entwässerungsplan (drainage plan) | Prepared by TGA-Planer (building services engineer) |
| 3 | Municipal approval | Tiefbauamt or Stadtentwässerung |
| 4 | Connection construction | Approved contractor |
| 5 | Dichtheitsprüfung (leak test) | Before backfill — DIN EN 1610 |
| 6 | As-built documentation | Bestandsplan submitted to municipality |

## ModulCA Application
- **Pre-plumbed modules**: ModulCA bathroom and kitchen modules arrive with internal plumbing complete — only site connections needed
- **Drainage plan integration**: ModulCA floor plans should include standardized connection points for municipal hook-up
- **Rainwater strategy**: Green roof modules or integrated retention tanks reduce Niederschlagswassergebühr — selling point
- **Backflow protection**: Factory-installed Rückstausicherung in basement/ground-floor modules prevents sewer backup damage
- **Quick connection**: Modular construction reduces open-trench time — less disruption, faster Dichtheitsprüfung
- **Split fee savings**: Communicate potential annual savings from reduced sealed surface to clients
