# Fire Safety Regulations (France)

## Overview
French fire safety for residential buildings (habitations) is governed by the Arrêté du 31 janvier 1986. Requirements depend on building family classification. Single-family homes (1ère famille) have modest requirements, while multi-family buildings have progressively stricter rules.

## Building Classification (Habitations)

| Family | Description | Max Height | Examples |
|--------|------------|------------|---------|
| 1ère famille | Individual houses (≤ 2 floors above ground) | R+1 | Single-family homes, ModulCA |
| 2ème famille | Individual houses > 2 floors, or collective ≤ 3 floors | R+3 | Small apartment buildings |
| 3ème famille A | Collective, ≤ 28m, with conditions | 28m | Medium apartment buildings |
| 3ème famille B | Collective, ≤ 28m, without conditions | 28m | Medium apartments |
| 4ème famille | Collective, 28-50m | 50m | Tall residential |

## Requirements for 1ère Famille (ModulCA Homes)

| Element | Requirement | Notes |
|---------|------------|-------|
| Structure | Stable au feu ½ h (R 30) | Minimum structural fire resistance |
| Floors | Coupe-feu ½ h (REI 30) | Between stories |
| Walls (load-bearing) | Stable au feu ½ h (R 30) | |
| Facade cladding | Euroclasse C-s3,d0 minimum | For timber cladding |
| Smoke detectors | DAAF mandatory (each floor) | Loi Morange (since 2015) |
| Escape | Direct exit to outside | Ground floor access |
| Electrical | NF C 15-100 compliance | French electrical standard |

## Timber Construction Fire Rules

| Timber Element | 1ère Famille | 2ème Famille | Notes |
|---------------|-------------|-------------|-------|
| Exposed CLT walls (interior) | Allowed | Allowed with conditions | D-s2,d0 minimum |
| Exposed CLT ceiling | Allowed | Encapsulation may be needed | Check local SDIS guidance |
| Timber cladding (exterior) | Euroclasse C-s3,d0 | B-s3,d0 for higher | Fire-treated or naturally resistant |
| Timber structure | R 30 achievable with CLT ≥ 80mm | R 60 with thicker CLT | Calculate charring rate |

## CLT Fire Performance

| CLT Thickness | Charring Time | Fire Resistance |
|---------------|--------------|-----------------|
| 80mm (3-layer) | ~50 min to lose integrity | R 30+ easily |
| 100mm (3-layer) | ~62 min | R 45-60 |
| 120mm (5-layer) | ~75 min | R 60+ |
| 160mm (5-layer) | ~100 min | R 90+ |

## Key Differences

| Aspect | France | Germany | Netherlands | Romania |
|--------|--------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Single-family requirement | R 30 (modest) | REI 30-60 (varies by GK) | Based on BBL | REI 30-60 |
| Smoke detectors | Mandatory (DAAF) | Mandatory (most states) | Mandatory | Mandatory |
| Timber facade | Euroclasse C | Depends on GK | Class B minimum | Fire-treated |
| Exposed timber interior | Allowed (1ère fam.) | GK 1-3: yes, GK 4+: encapsulated | Generally allowed | Limited |
| Sprinklers (residential) | Not required (1ère-2ème) | Not required (GK 1-3) | Not required (single) | Not required |

## ModulCA Application
- **1ère famille**: ModulCA single-family homes have the most relaxed French fire requirements — R 30 easily met by CLT
- **Exposed CLT**: Interior exposed CLT is permitted in 1ère famille — biophilic + fire-safe
- **DAAF pre-installed**: Factory-install smoke detectors in each module — French compliance built-in
- **Timber cladding**: Fire-treated larch or thermowood achieves Euroclasse C-s3,d0 — compliant
- **Minimal encapsulation**: Unlike Germany GK 4+, French 1ère famille homes rarely need gypsum encapsulation
