# Energy Standards — RE2020 (France)

## Overview
France's RE2020 (Réglementation Environnementale 2020), effective January 2022, is Europe's most ambitious building regulation. It goes beyond energy performance to also regulate **embodied carbon** — the first national regulation to do so. This makes timber and bio-sourced construction inherently advantageous.

## RE2020 Three Pillars

| Pillar | Indicator | Target | What It Measures |
|--------|-----------|--------|------------------|
| Energy sobriety | Bbio (bioclimatic need) | ≤ 63 points (house) | Heating + cooling + lighting need (before systems) |
| Energy performance | Cep (primary energy) | ≤ 50 kWh/m²·year | Total primary energy consumption |
| Carbon performance | Ic construction | ≤ 640 kgCO₂e/m² | Embodied carbon of materials |
| | Ic énergie | Decreasing thresholds | Operational carbon (fossil fuel use) |

## Key Requirements (New Single-Family Home)

| Parameter | RE2020 Value | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| Bbio max | 63 points (climate zone H1) | Bioclimatic design quality |
| Cep max | 50 kWh/m²·year | Non-renewable primary energy |
| Cep,nr max | 50 kWh/m²·year | Non-renewable portion |
| DH (summer comfort) | ≤ 1250 °C·h | Degree-hours of discomfort |
| Ic construction | ≤ 640 kgCO₂e/m² (2022-2024) | Tightening to ≤ 530 by 2028 |
| Ic énergie | Progressively decreasing | Pushes toward non-fossil heating |

## RE2020 Carbon Innovation: Ic Construction

This is what makes RE2020 unique globally:

| Material System | Typical Ic (kgCO₂e/m²) | RE2020 Limit | Status |
|----------------|------------------------|-------------|--------|
| Concrete/masonry house | 500-700 | 640 (→530) | Tight, may fail post-2028 |
| Steel frame house | 450-650 | 640 (→530) | Challenging |
| Timber frame house | 150-350 | 640 (→530) | **Easy compliance** |
| CLT house | **-50 to +200** | 640 (→530) | **Massively below limit** |
| ModulCA (CLT+timber) | **~100-250** | 640 (→530) | **Excellent margin** |

## Heating Under RE2020

| System | Ic énergie Impact | Status |
|--------|------------------|--------|
| Heat pump | Very low carbon | Preferred |
| Wood pellet/biomass | Low carbon | Compliant |
| District heating (renewable) | Low carbon | Compliant |
| Gas boiler | High carbon | Effectively banned by DH threshold |
| Electric resistance | Moderate | Allowed but inefficient |

## Comparison with Other Countries

| Parameter | France (RE2020) | Germany (GEG) | Netherlands (BENG) | Romania |
|-----------|----------------|--------------|-------------------|---------|
| Energy target | Cep ≤ 50 | ≤ 55 kWh/m²y | BENG2 ≤ 50 | ≤ 100 kWh/m²y |
| Embodied carbon | **Yes (Ic)** | No (voluntary) | MPG (voluntary) | No |
| Summer comfort | DH threshold | No specific | TOjuli ≤ 1.20 | Ti ≤ 27°C |
| Timber advantage | **Massive** (carbon) | Good (KfW) | Good (circular) | Moderate |
| Gas ban | De facto (carbon) | Explicit (65%) | Explicit (2018) | No |
