# Modular & Prefab Construction Rules (France)

## Overview
France has a well-established framework for evaluating and certifying non-traditional construction methods. Modular and offsite construction (construction hors-site) must comply with the same regulations as site-built construction, but the approval pathway for innovative systems involves additional technical assessments through the CSTB (Centre Scientifique et Technique du Bâtiment). Understanding DTU standards, Avis Technique (ATec), and ATEx procedures is essential for modular builders entering the French market.

## Technical Approval Framework

| Certification | Issuer | Purpose | Duration |
|--------------|--------|---------|----------|
| DTU (Document Technique Unifié) | AFNOR/NF | Standard construction methods (techniques courantes) | Permanent (updated periodically) |
| Avis Technique (ATec) | CSTB | Non-standard products/systems evaluation | 2-7 years (renewable) |
| ATEx (Appréciation Technique d'Expérimentation) | CSTB | Experimental systems, limited projects | 2 years or specific project count |
| DTA (Document Technique d'Application) | CSTB | European product with French application rules | Aligned with ETA |
| ETA (European Technical Assessment) | Notified body | European-level product assessment | 5 years |
| Certification NF | AFNOR | Voluntary quality mark for products | Annual renewal |

## Techniques Courantes vs. Non Courantes

French insurance law makes a critical distinction:

| Category | Definition | Insurance Impact |
|----------|-----------|-----------------|
| Technique courante | Covered by a DTU or ATec with favorable opinion | Standard insurance rates |
| Technique non courante | No DTU or ATec coverage | Higher premiums or refusal; requires specific evaluation |

This distinction is crucial for modular construction because:
- Insurers (assureurs décennaux) may refuse or increase premiums for non-standard techniques
- The dommage-ouvrage policy (mandatory) depends on the technique being insurable
- ATec or ATEx provides the "ticket" to standard insurance

## Avis Technique (ATec) Process

| Step | Action | Duration |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Manufacturer submits dossier technique to CSTB | Preparation: 1-3 months |
| 2 | CSTB assigns to Groupe Spécialisé (GS) | 1-2 months |
| 3 | Testing program (fire, structural, durability, etc.) | 3-12 months |
| 4 | GS evaluation meeting | Scheduled quarterly |
| 5 | ATec published (favorable / favorable with reservations / unfavorable) | Total: 12-24 months |
| 6 | Renewal before expiry | Every 2-7 years |

### Costs (Indicative)

| Item | Cost Range |
|------|-----------|
| ATec application and evaluation | 15,000-50,000 EUR |
| Testing (fire, structural, acoustic) | 20,000-80,000 EUR |
| Total for new system | 40,000-130,000 EUR |
| ATec renewal | 10,000-30,000 EUR |

## ATEx (Appréciation Technique d'Expérimentation)

For systems too new for full ATec — faster but limited:

| Aspect | Detail |
|--------|--------|
| Purpose | Evaluate innovative systems for limited use before full ATec |
| Types | Type A (specific case, one project) / Type B (small series, up to ~20 projects) |
| Duration | 2 years maximum |
| Cost | 5,000-20,000 EUR (faster than ATec) |
| Insurance | Generally accepted by insurers for covered projects |
| Strategy | Use ATEx to build track record, then apply for full ATec |

## Insurance Requirements for Modular

| Insurance | Who | Mandatory? | Notes |
|-----------|-----|-----------|-------|
| Responsabilité décennale | Builder/manufacturer | Yes (10-year liability) | Covers structural + waterproofing defects |
| Dommage-ouvrage | Building owner | Yes | Pre-finances repairs without proving fault |
| RC Pro | Builder/architect | Yes | Professional liability |
| Garantie de parfait achèvement | Builder | Yes (1 year) | All defects reported in first year |
| Garantie biennale | Builder | Yes (2 years) | Dissociable equipment (doors, heating) |

Key: Insurers will check that the construction system is either DTU-compliant or covered by ATec/ATEx before issuing décennale policies.

## Transport of Modules

Transporting large modular units on French roads requires:

| Dimension | Standard Transport | Convoi Exceptionnel (Special Transport) |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------------------------|
| Width | ≤ 2.55m | > 2.55m (up to 5m with permits) |
| Height | ≤ 4.00m | > 4.00m (up to 4.50m typical) |
| Length (semi-trailer) | ≤ 16.50m | > 16.50m (up to 25m with escort) |
| Weight (total) | ≤ 44t (5 axles) | > 44t (requires specific permit) |

### Convoi Exceptionnel Categories

| Category | Max Dimensions | Authorization |
|----------|---------------|---------------|
| 1ère catégorie | Width ≤ 3.00m, L ≤ 20m, Weight ≤ 48t | Permanent permit (renewable) |
| 2ème catégorie | Width ≤ 4.00m, L ≤ 25m, Weight ≤ 72t | Route-specific permit (prefect) |
| 3ème catégorie | Beyond 2ème limits | Case-by-case study |

### Transport Requirements

| Requirement | Detail |
|-------------|--------|
| Permit | Arrêté préfectoral for 2ème/3ème catégorie |
| Escort vehicle | 1 for 1ère cat., 1-2 for 2ème, police for 3ème |
| Travel hours | Typically sunrise to sunset, restricted on holidays |
| Route study | Mandatory for 2ème/3ème catégorie (bridges, tunnels, turns) |
| Insurance | Specific transport insurance required |

## ModulCA Application
- **ATEx strategy**: Start with ATEx Type B for first French projects (up to 20 builds), then pursue full ATec
- **DTU 31.2 alignment**: Module wall and floor assemblies follow DTU 31.2 principles where possible to maintain technique courante status
- **Insurance pathway**: ATEx provides insurability — critical for securing dommage-ouvrage and décennale policies
- **Module width 2.55m**: Standard ModulCA modules designed at ≤ 2.55m width for normal road transport — no convoi exceptionnel needed
- **Factory NF certification**: Target NF Maison à Ossature Bois certification for French market credibility
- **CSTB testing**: Budget 40,000-80,000 EUR for initial French market entry (ATEx + testing)
