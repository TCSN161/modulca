# Building Permit Process (France)

## Overview
France has a centralized building permit system (permis de construire) managed by municipalities but governed by national law. The process is relatively straightforward for single-family homes. All construction must comply with the local Plan Local d'Urbanisme (PLU) and national building standards.

## Permit Types

| Type | Applies To | Duration |
|------|-----------|----------|
| Déclaration préalable (DP) | Small works: 5-20m² extension, fence, facade change | 1 month review |
| Permis de construire (PC) | New buildings > 20m², major works | 2-3 months review |
| Permis d'aménager | Land subdivision, major earthworks | 3 months review |

## Permit Process Steps

| Step | Action | Who | Duration |
|------|--------|-----|----------|
| 1 | Consult PLU (local urban plan) | Owner/Architect | 1 week |
| 2 | Certificat d'urbanisme (optional) | Mairie (town hall) | 1-2 months |
| 3 | Architectural design | Architect (mandatory > 150m²) | 4-8 weeks |
| 4 | Submit permis de construire | Owner → Mairie | — |
| 5 | Review + consultation | Mairie + ABF (if heritage zone) | 2-3 months |
| 6 | Permit granted or refused | Mairie | — |
| 7 | Affichage (display permit on site) | Owner | 2 months for third-party appeal |
| 8 | DAACT (completion declaration) | Owner → Mairie | Within 90 days of completion |

## Key Professionals

| Professional | French Title | When Required |
|-------------|-------------|---------------|
| Architect | Architecte DPLG/DE | Mandatory for > 150m² floor area |
| Bureau d'études structure | Structural engineer | Structural calculations |
| Bureau d'études thermique | Energy consultant | RE2020 attestation |
| Contrôleur technique | Technical controller | Buildings > 2 floors or ERP |
| Géomètre-expert | Land surveyor | Boundary definition |

## Costs

| Item | Cost Range | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| Permit fee | Free | No municipal fee for permit |
| Taxe d'aménagement | €800-1,000/m² × rate (1-5%) | Development tax, varies by commune |
| Architect | 8-12% of construction cost | If mandatory |
| RE2020 attestation | €500-1,500 | Energy compliance certificate |
| Insurance (Dommage-ouvrage) | 1-3% of construction cost | Mandatory 10-year building insurance |

## French Unique: Dommage-Ouvrage Insurance
France requires **dommage-ouvrage** insurance — a 10-year building defects insurance policy that covers structural and waterproofing issues. This is mandatory before construction starts and applies to the building, not the builder.

## Key Differences

| Aspect | France | Germany | Netherlands | Romania |
|--------|--------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Architect mandatory | > 150m² | Always (Bauvorlageberechtigter) | Not always | Always |
| Permit fee | Free | €500-3,000 | €500-5,000 | €100-500 |
| 10-year insurance | Mandatory (dommage-ouvrage) | No | No | No |
| Review time | 2-3 months | 4-12 weeks | 8-26 weeks | 3-6 months |
| Heritage zones | ABF review (strict) | Denkmalschutz | Monument commission | Protected zones |

## ModulCA Application
- **Under 150m²**: Most ModulCA homes under 150m² — architect not legally required (but recommended)
- **Fast permit**: 2-month review for standard PC — faster than most EU countries
- **Dommage-ouvrage**: Factory-built modules need to be insured — but controlled manufacturing reduces defect risk
- **PLU compliance**: Module layout must match local PLU rules (height, COS/CES, setbacks)
- **RE2020 ready**: ModulCA's energy performance meets or exceeds RE2020 thresholds
