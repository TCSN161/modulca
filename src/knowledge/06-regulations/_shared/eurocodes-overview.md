# Eurocodes Overview

## Overview
The Eurocodes are the European standards for structural design, adopted by all EU member states with National Annexes for local parameters.

## Eurocode Family

| Code | Title | Relevance to Modular |
|------|-------|---------------------|
| EN 1990 | Basis of structural design | Design life, safety factors |
| EN 1991 | Actions on structures | Loads: dead, live, wind, snow |
| EN 1992 | Concrete structures | Foundation design |
| EN 1993 | Steel structures | Steel frame modules |
| EN 1994 | Composite structures | Steel-concrete floors |
| EN 1995 | Timber structures | CLT, glulam, timber frame |
| EN 1996 | Masonry structures | Rarely used in modular |
| EN 1997 | Geotechnical design | Foundation design |
| EN 1998 | Seismic design | Earthquake resistance |
| EN 1999 | Aluminium structures | Specialist applications |

## Key Concept: National Annexes
Each EU country publishes National Annexes that set locally-applicable parameters (e.g., snow load zones, wind speeds, seismic accelerations). The Eurocode framework is universal, but the numbers change per country.

## ModulCA Application
ModulCA modules are designed to EN 1995 (timber) or EN 1993 (steel), with foundations per EN 1997. Each target market requires checking the relevant National Annex parameters. The modular knowledge base includes country-specific Eurocode parameters under each country's regulations folder.
