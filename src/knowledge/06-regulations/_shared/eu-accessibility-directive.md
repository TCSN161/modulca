# EU Accessibility Directive & EN 17210

## Overview
The European Accessibility Act (EAA) and EN 17210 establish accessibility requirements across the EU. While the EAA focuses on products and services, EN 17210 covers the built environment.

## EN 17210 — Accessibility of the Built Environment

| Element | Requirement | Notes |
|---------|------------|-------|
| Entrance door width | ≥ 850mm clear | ≥ 900mm recommended |
| Corridor width | ≥ 1200mm | For wheelchair passage |
| Turning space | 1500mm ∅ | At direction changes |
| Ramp gradient | ≤ 1:20 preferred | Max 1:12 for short ramps |
| Ramp width | ≥ 1200mm | With handrails both sides |
| Step riser | 150–170mm | Uniform throughout |
| Handrail height | 900–1000mm | Both sides of stairs |
| Lift minimum | 1100 × 1400mm | Car internal dimensions |
| Sanitary facilities | 1 accessible per floor | With grab bars, turning space |
| Signage | Visual + tactile | Contrast ratio ≥ 0.6 |

## Key Principles
1. **Equitable use** — same means of access for all users
2. **Flexibility** — accommodate range of abilities
3. **Simple and intuitive** — easy to understand
4. **Perceptible information** — multi-sensory communication
5. **Tolerance for error** — minimize hazards
6. **Low physical effort** — comfortable use
7. **Size and space** — adequate for approach and use

## National Transposition
Each EU country implements accessibility through national building codes:
- **Romania**: NP 051/2012 — Accessibility norms
- **Netherlands**: Bouwbesluit 2012, Chapter 4 — Accessibility
- **Germany**: DIN 18040-1/2 — Barrier-free buildings
