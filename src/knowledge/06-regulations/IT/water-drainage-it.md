# Water Supply & Drainage — Italy

## Overview

Italian plumbing and drainage must comply with **DM 37/2008** (general installations law), national UNI standards, and European EN norms. Installation must be performed by a **qualified installer** (impresa abilitata) who issues a **Dichiarazione di Conformità** (conformity declaration).

## Water Supply (UNI 9182)

### Minimum Flow Rates
| Fixture | Flow (l/s) |
|---------|-----------|
| Washbasin | 0.10 |
| Bidet | 0.10 |
| Shower | 0.15 |
| Bathtub | 0.20 |
| Kitchen sink | 0.20 |
| WC (cistern) | 0.10 |
| Washing machine | 0.20 |

### Pressure
- **Minimum at fixture**: 50 kPa (0.5 bar)
- **Recommended**: 150–300 kPa
- **Maximum**: 500 kPa — pressure reducer required above this

### Hot Water
- **Minimum temperature**: 45°C at outlet
- **Maximum storage**: 60°C (legionella prevention)
- Solar thermal required for new builds (per regional energy laws)
- Heat pump increasingly common (superbonus incentives)

## Drainage (UNI EN 12056)

### Pipe Sizing
| Connection | Minimum Ø |
|-----------|-----------|
| Washbasin | 32mm |
| Bidet | 32mm |
| Shower | 40mm |
| WC | 100mm |
| Kitchen sink | 40mm |
| Vertical stack | 100mm |
| Horizontal collector | 110mm |

### System Types
- **System I**: Single-stack system (most efficient, UNI EN 12056-2)
- **System III**: Separate stacks for grey/black water
- Ventilation: Each stack needs vent to roof or AAV (Aerator)

## Municipal Connection
- Application to local water utility (ATO — Ambito Territoriale Ottimale)
- Connection fee varies by municipality (€500–2,000)
- Separate rainwater and sewage connections typically required
- Septic tank + sub-irrigation allowed for rural properties without sewer
