# Fire Safety Regulations — Italy

## Overview

Italian fire safety is overseen by the **Vigili del Fuoco** (VVF — Fire Brigade Corps) under the Ministry of Interior. The main framework is the **Codice di Prevenzione Incendi** (DM 3 agosto 2015), a performance-based fire safety code that replaced the old prescriptive rules.

## Key Requirements for Residential Buildings

### Fire Resistance
| Element | Requirement (height ≤12m) | Requirement (12–24m) |
|---------|--------------------------|---------------------|
| Load-bearing structure | R 30 | R 60 |
| Compartment walls | REI 30 | REI 60 |
| Floors | REI 30 | REI 60 |
| External walls | Class A2-s1,d0 (above 12m) | Class A2-s1,d0 |

### Evacuation
- **Maximum floor area per compartment**: 500m² (single staircase)
- **Maximum travel distance**: 30m to exit
- **Staircase width**: Minimum 1.05m (up to 2 floors, ≤10 persons)
- **Emergency lighting**: Required on all escape routes

### SCIA (Segnalazione Certificata di Inizio Attività)
- Buildings >400m² or >2 floors need fire prevention assessment (SCIA antincendio)
- Filed with local VVF command
- Professional fire engineer (professionista antincendio) must sign

## Timber Construction Fire Safety

The 2015 Fire Code explicitly addresses timber:
- **CLT charring rate**: 0.65mm/min (standard design value)
- **Encapsulation**: Option to protect with gypsum boards (2×12.5mm = 30 min)
- **Exposed CLT**: Allowed if overall fire resistance achieved through oversizing
- **Connections**: Steel connections must be recessed and covered

- ModulCA CLT modules designed with 30mm sacrificial char layer
- All inter-module connections include fire stops (intumescent strips)
- For buildings >400m²: SCIA filing included in ModulCA's permit package
