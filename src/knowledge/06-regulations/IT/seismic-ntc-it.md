# Seismic Design — NTC 2018 (Italy)

## Overview
Italy has the highest seismic risk in Western Europe. The NTC 2018 (Norme Tecniche per le Costruzioni) is Italy's comprehensive structural code, heavily based on Eurocode 8 but with Italian-specific seismic hazard maps and requirements. Unlike most EU countries, Italy requires separate seismic authorization (autorizzazione sismica) from the Genio Civile for construction in seismic zones — a process that can add months to the permit timeline.

## Seismic Zones

| Zone | ag (PGA on rock) | Risk Level | Typical Areas |
|------|-----------------|------------|---------------|
| Zone 1 | > 0.25g | Very high | Calabria, eastern Sicily, Friuli, Irpinia, L'Aquila |
| Zone 2 | 0.15-0.25g | High | Central Apennines, Basilicata, parts of Puglia, Marche |
| Zone 3 | 0.05-0.15g | Moderate | Most of northern Italy, Rome, Naples, western coast |
| Zone 4 | < 0.05g | Low | Sardinia, parts of Piedmont and Lombardy |

**Key fact:** ~60% of Italy is in Zone 1-2 (high-very high risk), ~95% has some seismic hazard.

## Design Parameters (NTC 2018)

| Parameter | Value Range | Notes |
|-----------|-------------|-------|
| ag (reference PGA) | 0.05-0.35g | Site-specific from hazard map |
| Return period (SLV) | 475 years | Life-safety limit state |
| Importance factor (CU) | 1.0 (residential) — 1.5 (strategic) | Usage-dependent |
| Soil amplification (S) | 1.0-1.80 | Depends on soil category (A-E) |
| Behaviour factor (q) | 1.5-4.0 (timber) | Ductility class dependent |
| Seismic weight | G1 + G2 + ψ2 × Qk | Quasi-permanent combination |
| Ductility classes | CD"A" (low), CD"B" (high) | Timber typically CD"B" |

## Comparison with Other Seismic Countries

| Parameter | Italy (NTC 2018) | Romania (P100) | Spain (EC8+NA) | Greece |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------|----------------|--------|
| Max PGA | 0.35g | 0.40g | 0.16g | 0.36g |
| Territory coverage | ~95% has some risk | ~100% | ~30% | ~100% |
| Seismic zones | 4 (+ microzonation) | 4 | Continuous map | 3 |
| Separate seismic permit | **Yes (Genio Civile)** | No (integrated) | No (integrated) | Yes |
| Dominant hazard type | Shallow crustal | Deep (Vrancea) + crustal | Moderate, shallow | Subduction + crustal |
| Timber behaviour factor | Up to q=4.0 (CD"B") | Up to q=3.0 | Up to q=2.0 | Up to q=3.0 |
| Code basis | EC8-based (NTC) | P100 (→ EC8) | EC8 + NA | EC8 + NA |
| Seismic incentives | **Sismabonus (up to 85%)** | None specific | None specific | None specific |

## Timber Construction in Italian Seismic Zones

| Advantage | Explanation |
|-----------|-------------|
| Low mass | Timber modules: 3-5 tonnes vs. masonry 15-20 tonnes → proportionally lower seismic forces |
| Ductile connections | Bolted timber connections dissipate energy — high behaviour factor (q up to 4.0) |
| No brittle failure | Unlike unreinforced masonry, timber doesn't collapse catastrophically |
| Historical evidence | L'Aquila 2009, Emilia 2012 — timber buildings performed well |
| Fast reconstruction | Modular timber allows rapid post-earthquake rebuilding |

## Seismic Authorization Process

| Step | Action | Duration |
|------|--------|----------|
| 1 | Structural design per NTC 2018 | Part of project |
| 2 | Submit to Genio Civile (regional office) | — |
| 3 | Technical review (Zone 1-2: full review) | 2-6 months |
| 4 | Authorization granted (or corrections requested) | — |
| 5 | Construction with structural testing at milestones | During construction |
| 6 | Final structural collaudo (testing certificate) | At completion |

**Note:** In Zone 3-4, some regions allow simplified notification (denuncia) instead of full authorization, but structural review still occurs.

## Sismabonus — Seismic Upgrade Incentive

| Improvement | Tax Deduction |
|-------------|--------------|
| 1 risk class improvement | 70% (single unit) / 75% (condominium) |
| 2+ risk class improvement | 80% (single unit) / 85% (condominium) |
| Maximum deduction base | €96,000 per unit |
| Deduction period | 5 years |

## ModulCA Application
- **Seismic performance**: CLT timber modules have excellent seismic behaviour — light, ductile, documented performance in Italian earthquakes
- **Sismabonus eligible**: Replacing masonry with timber modular can improve seismic class by 2+ levels → 80-85% tax deduction
- **Genio Civile advantage**: Factory-certified structural calculations with standardized connections speed up seismic authorization
- **L'Aquila model**: Post-earthquake reconstruction used prefab extensively — modular timber is the next evolution
- **Southern Italy**: Calabria and Sicily (Zone 1) have oldest housing stock + highest risk — replacement market potential
- **Dual incentive**: Sismabonus + Ecobonus can be combined — seismic upgrade + energy upgrade in one modular intervention
