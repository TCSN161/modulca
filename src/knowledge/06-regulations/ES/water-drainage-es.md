# Water Supply & Drainage — Spain

## Overview

Water and drainage in Spain are regulated by **CTE DB-HS** (Salubridad — Health), specifically:
- **HS 4**: Water supply (suministro de agua)
- **HS 5**: Wastewater evacuation (evacuación de aguas residuales)

Municipal water companies handle connections and may impose additional requirements.

## Water Supply (DB-HS 4)

### Minimum Flow Rates
| Fixture | Cold (l/s) | Hot (l/s) |
|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Washbasin | 0.10 | 0.065 |
| Shower | 0.20 | 0.10 |
| Bathtub | 0.30 | 0.20 |
| Kitchen sink | 0.20 | 0.10 |
| WC cistern | 0.10 | — |
| Washing machine | 0.20 | 0.15 |
| Dishwasher | 0.15 | 0.10 |

### Pressure Requirements
- **Minimum at fixture**: 100 kPa (taps) / 150 kPa (thermostatic valves)
- **Maximum**: 500 kPa at any point
- Pressure reducer required if mains >500 kPa

### Hot Water (ACS — Agua Caliente Sanitaria)
- CTE DB-HE 4 mandates **solar thermal** contribution for hot water
- Solar fraction: 30–70% depending on climate zone and consumption
- Climate zones: I (north) to V (Canary Islands/south)

## Drainage (DB-HS 5)

### Pipe Sizing (Minimum Diameters)
| Connection | Minimum Ø |
|-----------|-----------|
| Washbasin | 32mm |
| Shower | 40mm |
| WC | 100mm |
| Kitchen sink | 40mm |
| Main collector | 110mm (horizontal) |

### Rainwater
- Separate system required in most municipalities
- Sustainable drainage (SUDS) increasingly encouraged
- Rainwater harvesting allowed for garden irrigation (not potable)
