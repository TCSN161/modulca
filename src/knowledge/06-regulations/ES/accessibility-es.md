# Accessibility Standards — Spain

## Overview

Spanish accessibility is regulated by **CTE DB-SUA** (Documento Básico — Seguridad de Utilización y Accesibilidad), specifically **SUA 9**. Additional requirements come from autonomous community legislation, which can be stricter.

## Key Requirements

### Accessible Routes (SUA 9)
| Element | Minimum Dimension |
|---------|------------------|
| Accessible corridor width | 1.20m |
| Accessible door clear opening | 0.80m (0.90m in public buildings) |
| Level change threshold | ≤20mm (beveled if >5mm) |
| Ramp gradient | ≤10% (≤3m) / ≤8% (3–6m) / ≤6% (6–9m) |
| Wheelchair turning radius | 1.50m diameter |

### Accessible Bathroom
- Minimum 1.50m turning circle
- Grab bars at WC (horizontal at 700–750mm)
- Shower: level-access, minimum 800×1200mm
- Washbasin: knee clearance below, height 800–850mm

### Single-Family Homes
For private residential (vivienda unifamiliar):
- Accessible route from street to dwelling entrance required
- Interior rooms on ground floor must be accessible
- Doors: 0.80m minimum clear width
- At least one ground-floor bathroom must be accessible or adaptable

## Regional Variations

Each autonomous community may have additional requirements:
- **Catalonia**: Decree 135/1995 (stricter than national code)
- **Basque Country**: Decree 68/2000
- **Andalusia**: Decree 293/2009
