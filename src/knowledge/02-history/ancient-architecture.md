# Ancient Architecture (3000 BC – 500 AD)

## Overview
Ancient architecture laid the foundations of Western building culture. From Egyptian pyramids to Greek temples to Roman engineering, the principles of proportion, structure, and civic purpose that emerged in this era still influence architecture today.

## Key Civilizations

| Civilization | Period | Key Contribution | Iconic Building |
|-------------|--------|-----------------|-----------------|
| Egyptian | 3000-30 BC | Monumental stone construction | Pyramids of Giza |
| Mesopotamian | 3500-500 BC | Brick, arch, ziggurat | Ziggurat of Ur |
| Greek | 800-30 BC | Proportional orders, temple form | Parthenon |
| Roman | 500 BC-476 AD | Arch, vault, dome, concrete | Pantheon, Colosseum |
| Persian | 550-330 BC | Grand palace architecture | Persepolis |

## Greek Orders of Architecture

| Order | Capital | Proportions | Character |
|-------|---------|-------------|-----------|
| Doric | Plain, cushion-like | Column height = 6× diameter | Strength, masculinity |
| Ionic | Scroll volutes | Column height = 8× diameter | Grace, femininity |
| Corinthian | Acanthus leaves | Column height = 10× diameter | Richness, decoration |

## Roman Engineering Innovations

| Innovation | Description | Legacy |
|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Concrete (opus caementicium) | Volcanic ash + lime morite | Enabled domes, vaults |
| Arch | Semi-circular, load-bearing | Aqueducts, bridges |
| Vault | Extended arch into 3D | Covered large spans |
| Dome | Rotated arch | Pantheon: 43.3m span (unreinforced) |
| Hypocaust | Underfloor heating system | Ancestor of radiant floor heating |
| Aqueduct | Gravity-fed water supply | Urban water infrastructure |

## Vitruvius — First Architectural Theory
Marcus Vitruvius Pollio's "De Architectura" (~30 BC) defined architecture's three principles:
- **Firmitas** (Strength) — structural soundness
- **Utilitas** (Utility) — functional fitness
- **Venustas** (Beauty) — aesthetic delight

These remain the foundation of architectural thinking.
