# Art Deco (1920 – 1940)

## Overview
Art Deco emerged after World War I as a celebration of modernity, technology, and glamour. It combined geometric patterns, bold colors, and luxurious materials with machine-age streamlining. Unlike the organic curves of Art Nouveau, Art Deco was angular, symmetrical, and deliberately modern.

## Key Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Geometric patterns | Zigzags, chevrons, sunbursts, stepped forms |
| Symmetry | Strong central axis, balanced composition |
| Luxury materials | Chrome, brass, marble, exotic woods |
| Bold color | Gold, silver, black, jewel tones |
| Streamlined forms | Smooth curves, horizontal lines |
| Machine aesthetic | Celebration of technology and speed |

## Art Deco in Romania (Bucharest)
Bucharest has one of Europe's richest interwar architectural heritages:

| Building | Architect | Year |
|----------|-----------|------|
| Telephone Palace | Walter Froy, Edmond van Saanen Algi | 1934 |
| ARO Building | Horia Creangă | 1931 |
| Scala Cinema | Rudolf Fränkel | 1935 |
| Many residential villas | Various | 1920s-1930s |
| Boulevard apartments | Modernist-Deco hybrid | 1930s |

Bucharest was called "Little Paris" partly due to its rich interwar Art Deco and Modernist architecture.

## Design Elements Relevant Today

| Element | Art Deco Expression | Modern Adaptation |
|---------|--------------------|-------------------|
| Geometric patterns | Carved stone, metalwork | CNC-cut timber screens, laser-cut panels |
| Material contrast | Marble + chrome | Wood + steel, warm + cool |
| Statement entrance | Grand portal, canopy | Dramatic covered entrance module |
| Stepped forms | Zigguarat-like massing | Stepped module arrangements on sloped sites |
| Lighting | Decorative sconces, indirect | Feature lighting, LED accent |
