# Life Cycle Assessment & Embodied Carbon

## Overview
A building's environmental impact has two parts: operational carbon (heating, cooling, electricity use) and embodied carbon (materials, manufacturing, transport, construction, demolition). As buildings become more energy-efficient, embodied carbon becomes the dominant share — up to 50-70% of lifetime emissions for nZEB buildings.

## Life Cycle Stages (EN 15978)

| Stage | Code | What It Covers |
|-------|------|----------------|
| Product | A1-A3 | Raw materials, transport to factory, manufacturing |
| Construction | A4-A5 | Transport to site, assembly |
| Use | B1-B7 | Maintenance, repair, replacement, energy, water |
| End of life | C1-C4 | Demolition, transport, waste processing, disposal |
| Beyond life | D | Reuse, recycling, energy recovery benefits |

## Embodied Carbon by Material

| Material | kgCO₂e/kg | kgCO₂e/m³ | Notes |
|----------|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Concrete (C30) | 0.10-0.15 | 240-360 | High volume, moderate intensity |
| Steel (structural) | 1.5-2.5 | 12,000-20,000 | High intensity, recyclable |
| Brick | 0.20-0.25 | 350-500 | Traditional, moderate |
| CLT (cross-laminated timber) | -0.7 to -1.0 | -350 to -500 | **Carbon negative** (stores CO₂) |
| Glulam | -0.7 to -1.0 | -350 to -500 | Carbon negative |
| Timber stud | -0.7 to -1.0 | -350 to -500 | Carbon negative |
| Mineral wool insulation | 1.0-1.5 | 30-50 | Low embodied per function |
| EPS insulation | 3.0-4.0 | 60-80 | Petroleum-based |
| Gypsum board | 0.12-0.18 | 100-150 | Low |
| Glass (double) | 1.2-1.5 | 3,000-3,800 | Per m² of glazing unit |

## Whole-Building Benchmarks

| Building Type | kgCO₂e/m² (A1-A3) | Notes |
|---------------|-------------------|-------|
| Conventional concrete/masonry | 400-600 | Standard Romanian/Dutch house |
| Steel frame | 350-550 | Commercial, some residential |
| Timber frame + SIP | 150-250 | Modular timber construction |
| CLT structure | **-50 to +150** | Carbon storage often exceeds emissions |
| Passive House (any material) | Add 50-100 | Extra insulation and glazing |

## ModulCA Carbon Advantage

| Component | Conventional House | ModulCA Module | Savings |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------|---------|
| Structure | 200-300 kgCO₂e/m² | -100 to +50 kgCO₂e/m² | 70-130% |
| Insulation | 30-50 kgCO₂e/m² | 25-40 kgCO₂e/m² | 20-30% |
| Factory waste | 10-15% material waste | 3-5% (factory precision) | 60-70% less waste |
| Transport | Multiple trips | 1-2 trips (complete modules) | 50-70% fewer trips |
| End of life (Stage D) | Demolition → landfill | Demountable → reuse | Circular credit |

## ModulCA Application
- **CLT = carbon sink**: Each m³ of CLT stores ~700-900 kgCO₂ — a ModulCA home can be carbon-negative in embodied terms
- **Factory efficiency**: CNC cutting minimizes waste, off-cuts used for smaller components
- **Demountable = Stage D credit**: Bolted connections mean modules can be disassembled and reused — massive end-of-life benefit
- **EPD availability**: CLT and timber products have Environmental Product Declarations — can prove carbon performance to clients
- **Future-proof**: EU taxonomy and upcoming regulations will reward low-embodied-carbon buildings
