# Solar Orientation & Site Planning

## Overview
Solar orientation is the single most impactful passive design decision. A well-oriented building can reduce heating demand by 25-40% and improve natural lighting significantly — at zero additional cost. In both Romania and the Netherlands, south-facing living spaces are the foundation of energy-efficient design.

## Optimal Room Orientation

| Room | Best Orientation | Reason |
|------|-----------------|--------|
| Living room | South / South-East | Maximum winter sun, daylight all day |
| Kitchen | East / South-East | Morning sun, warm start to the day |
| Master bedroom | East | Morning light, wake naturally |
| Children's bedroom | East / South-East | Bright mornings, cooler evenings |
| Home office | North / North-East | Even light, no screen glare |
| Bathroom | North / Interior | No solar requirement, privacy |
| Garage/utility | North / West | Buffer against cold winds |
| Terrace/garden | South / South-West | Afternoon sun, evening warmth |

## Solar Geometry

| Parameter | Romania (45°N) | Netherlands (52°N) |
|-----------|---------------|-------------------|
| Summer sun altitude (June 21) | ~68° | ~61° |
| Winter sun altitude (Dec 21) | ~22° | ~15° |
| Summer sunrise direction | NE (~60° azimuth) | NNE (~50° azimuth) |
| Winter sunrise direction | SE (~120° azimuth) | SE (~130° azimuth) |
| Daylight hours (summer) | ~15.5h | ~16.5h |
| Daylight hours (winter) | ~8.5h | ~7.5h |
| Heating season | Oct-Apr (~180 days) | Oct-Apr (~200 days) |

## Passive Solar Design Rules

| Rule | Guideline | Notes |
|------|-----------|-------|
| South glazing | 40-60% of south wall | With overhang for summer shading |
| North glazing | 10-20% of north wall | Minimize heat loss |
| East/West glazing | 20-30% | External shading recommended |
| Overhang depth | H × tan(summer altitude) | Blocks June sun, admits December sun |
| Thermal mass | Expose floor/walls to sun | Concrete, stone, or CLT absorbs heat |
| Buffer zones | Utility, garage on north | Reduce heat loss through north wall |

## Overhang Sizing

| Location | Summer Block Angle | Winter Admit Angle | Overhang for 2.5m window |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|-------------------------|
| Romania (45°N) | 68° | 22° | ~0.60m overhang |
| Netherlands (52°N) | 61° | 15° | ~0.55m overhang |

## ModulCA Application
- **Module rotation**: Orient the longest facade of L/T shapes to the south
- **Living modules south**: Place living/kitchen modules on south side of layout
- **Bedroom modules east**: Morning sun orientation for sleeping modules
- **Utility north**: Bathroom and storage modules on north — thermal buffer
- **Roof overhang**: 0.50-0.60m factory-built overhang on south modules
- **Screw pile flexibility**: Piles allow any building rotation — optimize for solar access
