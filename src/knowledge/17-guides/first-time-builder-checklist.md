# First-Time Home Builder Checklist: Everything You Need

Building your first home is one of the biggest financial and emotional commitments you will ever make. This checklist covers every step from the first idea to moving in, with practical guidance tailored to Romania's market in 2026. Follow it in order and you will avoid the mistakes that cost first-time builders thousands of euros and months of delays.

---

## Phase 1: Planning and Research (Month 1–2)

Before spending any money, invest time in planning.

### Define Your Needs

- [ ] List your non-negotiable requirements (bedrooms, bathrooms, office, garage)
- [ ] List your nice-to-have features (terrace, garden size, storage)
- [ ] Determine your target useful area (sqm) — typical Romanian family homes: 80–150 sqm
- [ ] Decide on number of floors: ground only (P), ground + 1 (P+1), or with attic (P+M)
- [ ] Consider future needs: will your family grow? Do you plan to work from home?
- [ ] Think about accessibility: single-story is easier long-term

### Set Your Budget

This is the most critical planning step. Be honest and conservative.

| Budget Category | Percentage of Total | Notes |
|----------------|-------------------|-------|
| Land purchase | 20–35% | Varies enormously by location |
| Construction (turnkey) | 45–55% | Structure, finishes, installations |
| Permitting and design | 5–8% | Architect, engineer, permits, avize |
| Utilities connection | 3–6% | Electricity, water, gas, sewer |
| Landscaping and exterior | 3–5% | Fence, driveway, garden |
| Furniture and appliances | 5–10% | Kitchen, bathroom, living room |
| Contingency reserve | 10–15% | Unexpected costs (always have this) |

### Budget Examples for Romania 2026

| Project Type | Land (periurban) | Construction | Total Budget |
|-------------|-----------------|-------------|-------------|
| Modest 80 sqm (P) | 15,000 EUR | 50,000 EUR | 80,000 EUR |
| Standard 120 sqm (P+1) | 20,000 EUR | 80,000 EUR | 120,000 EUR |
| Comfortable 160 sqm (P+1) | 25,000 EUR | 110,000 EUR | 170,000 EUR |

**Golden rule:** Your contingency reserve (10–15% of total budget) is not optional. Every build encounters unexpected costs. If you cannot afford the contingency, reduce your scope until you can.

### Explore Financing

- [ ] Calculate how much you can borrow: banks typically lend up to 75–80% of property value
- [ ] Check your eligibility for **Noua Casa** (government-backed mortgage): up to 70,000 EUR for new construction, 5% down payment
- [ ] Compare bank offers: BRD, BCR, Banca Transilvania, ING, Raiffeisen are the main players
- [ ] Factor in interest rates: fixed-rate for first 5 years is recommended (IRCC-linked loans are risky)
- [ ] Calculate your monthly payment comfort: do not exceed 35% of net household income
- [ ] Consider a **credit pentru constructie** (construction loan) that converts to mortgage upon completion
- [ ] Get a pre-approval from your bank before buying land

### Research Construction Methods

- [ ] Understand the difference between modular, traditional masonry, and timber frame
- [ ] Visit completed homes built with each method if possible
- [ ] Attend a construction expo (Construct Expo Bucharest, BIFE-SIM) to see products and meet suppliers
- [ ] Join online communities: Romanian home builder forums, Facebook groups
- [ ] Read comparison guides (see our Modular vs Traditional guide)

## Phase 2: Land Acquisition (Month 2–4)

- [ ] Define your target area: commute distance, school districts, neighborhood character
- [ ] Search listings: Imobiliare.ro, OLX, Storia, local real estate agents
- [ ] Shortlist 3–5 plots for in-person visits
- [ ] For each shortlisted plot:
  - [ ] Visit at different times of day and in different weather
  - [ ] Talk to neighbors about the area
  - [ ] Check mobile phone signal and internet availability
  - [ ] Verify road conditions in rain/winter
  - [ ] Request a Certificat de Urbanism (CU) to check buildability
- [ ] Commission an **extras de Carte Funciara** for your preferred plot
- [ ] Have a lawyer review the ownership documents
- [ ] Negotiate the price (periurban land is negotiable; 10–20% below asking is common)
- [ ] Sign the **antecontract** (preliminary contract) with a deposit (typically 10%)
- [ ] Complete the purchase at the notar (notary) and register in Cartea Funciara

### Land Purchase Costs Beyond the Price

| Item | Cost |
|------|------|
| Notary fee | 0.5–1% of price |
| Carte Funciara registration | 50–200 EUR |
| Real estate agent (if used) | 2–3% of price |
| Lawyer review | 200–500 EUR |
| Topographic survey | 200–500 EUR |
| Geotechnical study | 300–800 EUR |

## Phase 3: Design and Permitting (Month 3–7)

### Hire Your Team

- [ ] **Architect**: registered with OAR, experienced with your construction type. Fee: 2,000–6,000 EUR
- [ ] **Structural engineer**: MLPAT-attested. Fee: 1,500–3,000 EUR
- [ ] **Installations engineer**: electrical, plumbing, HVAC design. Fee: 500–1,500 EUR
- [ ] Verify all credentials and ask for references from previous clients

### Design Process

- [ ] Brief your architect with your requirements, budget, and site conditions
- [ ] Review the **studiu de fezabilitate** (feasibility study) based on CU constraints
- [ ] Approve the concept design (plan layout, elevations)
- [ ] Review and approve the detailed design (PAC/DTAC project)
- [ ] Ensure the project includes:
  - [ ] Energy performance calculation (C107 compliance)
  - [ ] Structural calculations (P100 seismic compliance)
  - [ ] Fire safety scenario
  - [ ] All installation plans

### Permitting Process

- [ ] Obtain CU if not already done during land selection
- [ ] Apply for all required avize simultaneously (see Building Permit guide for full list)
- [ ] Track avize status weekly — follow up actively with each institution
- [ ] Pay ISC (0.5%) and CSC (0.5%) contributions
- [ ] Submit AC application with complete documentation
- [ ] Receive AC and post the site information board (panou de identificare)

## Phase 4: Pre-Construction Preparation (Month 6–8)

### Choose Your Builder

- [ ] Get quotes from at least 3 builders or modular manufacturers
- [ ] For each quote, verify:
  - [ ] What is included and what is excluded
  - [ ] Payment schedule (never pay more than 30% upfront)
  - [ ] Timeline with specific milestones
  - [ ] Warranty terms (minimum 2 years for finishes, 10 years structural by law)
  - [ ] Penalty clauses for delays
- [ ] Check the builder's previous work: visit completed homes, talk to past clients
- [ ] Verify the builder has valid insurance and legal standing (check at Registrul Comertului)
- [ ] Sign a detailed construction contract reviewed by your lawyer

### Hire a Diriginte de Santier (Site Supervisor)

This is legally required and critically important.

- [ ] Hire a diriginte with MLPAT attestation for your construction category
- [ ] The diriginte must be independent from the builder (do not accept the builder's recommendation)
- [ ] Typical fee: 1,000–2,500 EUR for a residential project
- [ ] The diriginte will verify quality at every milestone and sign off on the Cartea Tehnica

### Prepare the Site

- [ ] Clear the land (vegetation, old structures)
- [ ] Arrange temporary utilities (electricity, water) for the construction phase
- [ ] Ensure site access is adequate for delivery vehicles
- [ ] Arrange waste disposal for construction debris

## Phase 5: Construction (Month 8–14 for Traditional, Month 8–11 for Modular)

### Foundation

- [ ] Diriginte verifies excavation depth and soil conditions match geotechnical study
- [ ] Rebar placement inspected before concrete pour
- [ ] Concrete quality tested (grade minimum C20/25 for residential)
- [ ] Curing period respected (minimum 28 days for full strength)
- [ ] Waterproofing membrane applied where required
- [ ] Foundation inspection signed off by diriginte

### Structure

**For modular construction:**
- [ ] Confirm delivery date and site readiness
- [ ] Crane positioned and tested
- [ ] Modules delivered and placed on foundation
- [ ] Module connections sealed and insulated
- [ ] Structure inspection by diriginte

**For traditional construction:**
- [ ] Walls erected according to project plans
- [ ] Reinforced concrete elements (stalpi, centuri, plansee) poured at specified intervals
- [ ] Roof structure installed
- [ ] Roof covering completed (house is "closed" — protected from weather)

### Installations

- [ ] Electrical wiring roughed in and inspected
- [ ] Plumbing roughed in and pressure tested
- [ ] HVAC system installed (heat pump, radiators/floor heating, ventilation)
- [ ] All installations photographed before being covered by finishes

### Interior Finishes

- [ ] Plastering and drywall
- [ ] Flooring (tiles, parquet, etc.)
- [ ] Bathroom fixtures and tiling
- [ ] Kitchen installation
- [ ] Interior doors
- [ ] Painting

### Exterior

- [ ] Thermal insulation system (ETICS/thermosystem)
- [ ] Exterior cladding or render
- [ ] Windows and exterior doors installed and sealed
- [ ] Drainage and rainwater management

## Phase 6: Inspections and Milestones

### Mandatory Inspections (Romania)

- [ ] **Foundation inspection** — before backfilling
- [ ] **Structure inspection** — before applying finishes
- [ ] **Installations pressure tests** — before closing walls
- [ ] **ISC inspection** — at structure completion (for projects over a certain value)
- [ ] **Final inspection (receptie la terminarea lucrarilor)** — when construction is complete

### Your Own Inspection Checklist

At each site visit, check:

- [ ] Work matches the approved plans
- [ ] Materials match specifications (check labels, delivery notes)
- [ ] No visible defects (cracks, misalignments, gaps)
- [ ] Diriginte has signed off on completed phases
- [ ] Builder is on schedule — address delays immediately

## Phase 7: Completion and Move-In (Month 12–16)

### Receptie (Final Acceptance)

- [ ] Builder notifies you the work is complete
- [ ] Schedule receptie with:
  - [ ] You (the owner / investitor)
  - [ ] Builder representative
  - [ ] Diriginte de santier
  - [ ] Project architect
  - [ ] ISC representative (if required)
- [ ] Walk through the entire house and note all defects (liste de remedieri)
- [ ] Builder fixes all defects within agreed timeframe
- [ ] Sign the Proces Verbal de Receptie la Terminarea Lucrarilor

### Administrative Steps After Completion

- [ ] Obtain the **Proces Verbal de Receptie** (official acceptance document)
- [ ] Register the building at **OCPI** (Oficiul de Cadastru si Publicitate Imobiliara)
- [ ] Update the **Carte Funciara** to include the building
- [ ] Obtain the **cadastral number** for the building
- [ ] Connect permanent utilities (replace temporary connections)
- [ ] Get a **certificat energetic** (energy performance certificate) — required for registration
- [ ] Set up utility contracts in your name (electricity, water, gas, internet)
- [ ] Arrange **home insurance** (required if you have a mortgage, recommended regardless)
- [ ] Notify the local Primarie of completion (for tax assessment purposes)

### Warranty Period

- [ ] Track warranty expiration dates:
  - Finishes and installations: minimum 2 years
  - Structural: minimum 10 years
  - Waterproofing: typically 5 years
- [ ] Document any defects that appear and notify the builder in writing within warranty period
- [ ] Schedule a **receptie finala** at the end of the warranty period (legally required)

## Top 10 Mistakes First-Time Builders Make

1. **No contingency budget.** Every project has surprises. Budget 10–15% extra.
2. **Paying too much upfront.** Never pay more than 30% before seeing progress. Tie payments to milestones.
3. **Skipping the geotechnical study.** A 500 EUR study can prevent a 15,000 EUR foundation problem.
4. **Not hiring an independent diriginte.** The builder's recommended diriginte may not protect your interests.
5. **Choosing the cheapest quote.** The cheapest builder often becomes the most expensive through delays, rework, and hidden extras.
6. **No written contract.** Everything must be in writing: scope, price, timeline, penalties, warranty.
7. **Ignoring the CU.** Building without checking zoning rules leads to demolition orders and fines.
8. **Not visiting the site regularly.** Check progress at least weekly. Problems caught early are cheap to fix.
9. **Changing the design during construction.** Every change during construction costs 3–5x more than during the design phase.
10. **Forgetting about utility connection timelines.** Apply for utility connections early — some take 6+ months.
