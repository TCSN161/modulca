# How to Get a Building Permit in Romania (Step-by-Step)

Building a home in Romania requires navigating a well-defined but multi-step permitting process. This guide walks you through every stage, from the initial urbanism certificate to the final construction permit, with realistic timelines, costs, and practical advice for 2026.

## Overview of the Process

The Romanian building permit process has four major phases:

1. **CU** (Certificat de Urbanism) — the urbanism certificate
2. **Project Documentation** — hiring an architect and preparing the technical project
3. **Avize and Acorduri** — obtaining all required approvals from utilities and authorities
4. **AC** (Autorizatie de Construire) — the actual construction permit

Total timeline: **4 to 8 months** depending on locality and project complexity.

---

## Phase 1: Certificat de Urbanism (CU)

The CU is not a permit to build. It is an informational document that tells you what you are allowed to build on your plot and what approvals you will need.

### Required Documents for CU

- [ ] Cerere tip (standard application form from the local Primarie)
- [ ] Copie act de proprietate (deed, contract, or extract from Cartea Funciara)
- [ ] Extras de Carte Funciara recent (not older than 30 days)
- [ ] Plan cadastral / topographic at 1:500 or 1:1000 scale
- [ ] Plan de incadrare in zona at 1:5000 or 1:10000 scale
- [ ] Document de plata taxa CU

### Where to Submit

Submit to the local Primarie (city hall) of the administrative unit where the land is located. In larger cities, this may be the Directia de Urbanism.

### Timeline and Costs

| Item | Details |
|------|---------|
| Processing time | 30 calendar days (legal maximum) |
| Typical actual time | 15–30 days |
| Fee | Varies by locality, typically **5–15 EUR** for residential |
| Validity | 12 months (can be extended once for another 12 months) |

### What the CU Tells You

The CU will specify:

- **POT** (Procent de Ocupare a Terenului) — maximum ground coverage, often 35–40% in residential zones
- **CUT** (Coeficient de Utilizare a Terenului) — maximum total built area ratio
- **Height regime** — maximum height or number of stories allowed
- **Retrageri** — mandatory setbacks from property boundaries and road
- **List of avize** — all approvals you must obtain before applying for AC
- **Utilities** — which utility connections are available or required

### Tips for Phase 1

- Always request the CU **before** buying land if possible. It reveals restrictions that may make your project impossible.
- Check if the area has a **PUZ** (Plan Urbanistic Zonal) or only a **PUG** (Plan Urbanistic General). PUZ areas have more specific rules.
- If the CU conditions are too restrictive, you can commission a PUZ to change local zoning, but this adds 6–12 months and costs 3,000–10,000 EUR.

## Phase 2: Project Documentation (DTAC / PAC)

Once you have the CU, you need a licensed architect to prepare the technical project.

### Types of Projects

- **PAC** (Proiect pentru Autorizatia de Construire) — the standard project for obtaining the AC
- **DTAC** (Documentatie Tehnica pentru Autorizatia de Construire) — sometimes used interchangeably with PAC in practice

The project must include:

- [ ] Architectural plans (plans, sections, elevations)
- [ ] Structural project (signed by a structural engineer with MLPAT attestation)
- [ ] Installations projects (electrical, plumbing, HVAC)
- [ ] Thermal performance study (in compliance with C107)
- [ ] Fire safety scenario (for buildings over certain thresholds)
- [ ] Site plan showing the building placement on the plot
- [ ] Memoriu tehnic (technical memorandum)

### Choosing an Architect

- Verify the architect is registered with **OAR** (Ordinul Arhitectilor din Romania)
- For residential projects, look for specialists in the housing type you want
- Typical fees for a residential project: **2,000–6,000 EUR** depending on complexity
- Structural engineering: **1,500–3,000 EUR** additional

## Phase 3: Avize and Acorduri (Approvals)

The CU will list all the avize you need. Common ones include:

### Utility Approvals

- [ ] **Aviz alimentare cu apa si canalizare** — water and sewage (e.g., Apa Nova, Compania de Apa)
- [ ] **Aviz alimentare cu energie electrica** — electricity (Distributie Energie / E-Distributie)
- [ ] **Aviz alimentare cu gaze naturale** — natural gas (Distrigaz / Delgaz)
- [ ] **Aviz telecomunicatii** — telecom (if applicable)

### Authority Approvals

- [ ] **Aviz protectia mediului** — environmental approval (Agentia de Protectia Mediului)
- [ ] **Aviz ISU** — fire safety (Inspectoratul pentru Situatii de Urgenta) — for larger projects
- [ ] **Aviz Directia de Sanatate Publica** — public health (if applicable)
- [ ] **Aviz Directia Judeteana de Cultura** — heritage protection (if in protected zone)
- [ ] **Aviz drumuri** — road authority approval for access
- [ ] **Acord vecini** — neighbor consent (if building within certain distances)

### Timeline and Costs for Avize

| Aviz | Typical Time | Typical Cost |
|------|-------------|-------------|
| Water/sewage | 15–30 days | 50–200 EUR |
| Electricity | 15–30 days | 50–300 EUR |
| Gas | 15–30 days | 50–200 EUR |
| Environmental | 15–45 days | 100–500 EUR |
| Fire safety | 15–30 days | 100–300 EUR |
| Road access | 15–30 days | 50–150 EUR |

**Total avize cost estimate: 500–1,500 EUR**

### Tips for Phase 3

- Start all avize applications **simultaneously**, not sequentially. This is the biggest time-saver.
- Some localities allow you to submit avize requests through a single-window system (ghiseu unic). Ask your Primarie.
- Keep certified copies of everything. You will need originals for the AC application.
- Environmental approval can be the slowest. Apply for it first.

## Phase 4: Autorizatia de Construire (AC)

This is the actual building permit.

### Required Documents for AC

- [ ] Cerere tip for AC (standard form)
- [ ] CU (original + copy)
- [ ] Act de proprietate (ownership document)
- [ ] Extras CF recent (not older than 30 days at submission)
- [ ] Complete project documentation (PAC/DTAC) — 2 copies
- [ ] All avize and acorduri listed in the CU
- [ ] Deviz general (cost estimate for the project)
- [ ] Document de plata taxa AC
- [ ] Dovada de plata a contributiei la Inspectoratul de Stat in Constructii (ISC) — 0.5% of project value
- [ ] Dovada de plata a contributiei la Casa Sociala a Constructorilor (CSC) — 0.5% of project value

### AC Fees

| Fee | Amount |
|-----|--------|
| AC tax | 1% of declared construction value |
| ISC contribution | 0.5% of declared construction value |
| CSC contribution | 0.5% of declared construction value |
| **Total mandatory fees** | **2% of declared construction value** |

For a typical modular home valued at 80,000 EUR, expect approximately **1,600 EUR** in fees.

### Timeline

- Legal processing time: **30 calendar days**
- Typical actual time: **20–45 days**
- AC validity: **12 months** from issuance (construction must begin within this period)
- Can be extended once for another 12 months

### After Receiving the AC

Before starting construction:

- [ ] Post the **panou de identificare** (site information board) visibly on the plot
- [ ] Notify ISC (Inspectoratul de Stat in Constructii) of the start date
- [ ] Hire a **diriginte de santier** (site supervisor) with appropriate attestation
- [ ] Prepare the **Cartea Tehnica a Constructiei** (building technical book)

## Common Pitfalls to Avoid

1. **Buying land without checking the CU first.** Some plots have severe restrictions (protected zones, flood risk, no road access) that make building impossible or extremely expensive.

2. **Underestimating the avize phase.** Budget 2–3 months just for approvals. Missing a single aviz means the AC application is rejected.

3. **Not hiring a licensed architect early.** An experienced architect familiar with local Primarie procedures can navigate the process far more efficiently.

4. **Forgetting ISC and CSC payments.** These are separate from the AC tax and must be paid before submission.

5. **Letting the CU expire.** If your CU expires before you apply for AC, you must start over. Track deadlines carefully.

6. **Incorrect topographic survey.** An outdated or inaccurate cadastral plan causes delays. Always use a recent, authorized survey.

7. **Ignoring neighbor setback rules.** Building too close to property lines without proper consent or variances leads to legal disputes and potential demolition orders.

| Phase | Duration |
|-------|----------|
| CU | 2–4 weeks |
| Project preparation | 4–8 weeks |
| Avize collection | 4–12 weeks |
| AC processing | 3–6 weeks |
