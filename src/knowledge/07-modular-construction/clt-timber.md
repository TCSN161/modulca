# CLT (Cross-Laminated Timber) Deep Dive

## Overview
Cross-Laminated Timber (CLT) is an engineered wood product made from layers of timber boards glued at 90° angles. It combines the strength of cross-lamination with the sustainability of wood, making it ideal for modular construction.

## Technical Properties

| Property | Value | Notes |
|----------|-------|-------|
| Density | 400–500 kg/m³ | Spruce-based CLT |
| Bending strength (f_m) | 24 N/mm² | Parallel to grain, outer layer |
| Compressive strength | 21 N/mm² | Perpendicular to panel |
| Shear strength | 3.5 N/mm² | Rolling shear |
| Modulus of elasticity | 12,000 N/mm² | Parallel to grain |
| Thermal conductivity | 0.13 W/mK | Good insulation for structure |
| Fire charring rate | 0.65 mm/min | Predictable fire behaviour |
| Moisture content | 12 ± 2% | Factory conditioned |

## Panel Specifications

| Application | Thickness | Layers | Notes |
|-------------|-----------|--------|-------|
| Walls (non-bearing) | 80–100mm | 3 layers | Interior partitions |
| Walls (load-bearing) | 100–140mm | 5 layers | Exterior walls |
| Floors | 140–200mm | 5-7 layers | Residential spans |
| Roof | 120–160mm | 5 layers | With insulation above |
| Max panel size | 3.5 × 16m | — | Transport dependent |

## Sustainability Advantages

| Factor | Value | Notes |
|--------|-------|-------|
| Carbon storage | ~750 kg CO₂/m³ | Sequestered in wood |

| Renewable material | Yes | FSC/PEFC certified sources |
| End of life | Reusable / recyclable / energy recovery | Circular economy |
| Manufacturing energy | Low | Compared to steel/concrete |

## Fire Performance
CLT chars predictably at 0.65 mm/min, forming a protective charcoal layer. This means:
- 100mm CLT wall: ~75 minutes before structural failure
- With 12.5mm gypsum board: REI 60 easily achieved
- With 2×12.5mm gypsum: REI 90+ achievable
- Large cross-sections don't collapse suddenly (unlike steel)

## ModulCA Application
CLT is the preferred structural material for ModulCA modules:
- Factory CNC cutting ensures ±1mm precision
- Pre-finished surfaces can be left exposed as interior feature
- Carbon-negative modules contribute to nZEB and sustainability certifications
- 3×3m module uses approximately 2m³ of CLT (wall + floor panels)
- Estimated carbon storage per module: ~1,500 kg CO₂
