# Scandinavian Design

## Overview
Scandinavian design combines functionalism, simplicity, and warmth. Born from the harsh Nordic climate and democratic social values, it prioritizes everyday usability, natural materials, and bringing light into dark winters. "Hygge" (Danish: cozy contentment) and "lagom" (Swedish: just the right amount) capture its philosophy.

## Key Characteristics

| Feature | Description |
|---------|-------------|
| Warm minimalism | Clean but not cold — wood, textiles, warmth |
| Natural light obsession | Large windows, white surfaces to reflect light |
| Wood everywhere | Pine, birch, oak — on floors, walls, furniture |
| Functional beauty | Beautiful because it works well |
| White + wood palette | White walls, blonde wood, pops of muted color |
| Cozy textiles | Wool, sheepskin, linen — tactile comfort |
| Connection to nature | Views, materials, outdoor access |

## Scandinavian Interior Palette

| Element | Typical Choice | Notes |
|---------|---------------|-------|
| Walls | White or light grey | Maximize light reflection |
| Floor | Light oak or birch | Wide planks, matte finish |
| Ceiling | White or exposed timber | Keep it bright |
| Furniture | Blonde wood + natural fabrics | Classic mid-century pieces |
| Textiles | Wool throws, linen curtains | Layered for hygge |
| Lighting | Pendant lamps (PH, Muuto) | Warm, designed fixtures |
| Accent color | Muted blue, sage green, dusty pink | Never loud |
| Plants | Large statement plants | Monstera, fiddle leaf fig |

| Reason | Explanation |
|--------|-------------|

| Compact space expertise | Nordics excel at small, functional apartments |
| Climate similarity | Romania and NL have similar cold/dark winters |
| Democratic values | Good design for everyone — ModulCA's mission |
| Sustainability alignment | Natural materials, energy efficiency, longevity |
