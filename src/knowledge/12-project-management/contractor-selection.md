# Selecting Contractors for Modular Home Projects

## Overview

Modular construction requires fewer on-site trades than traditional building, but the contractors involved must have specific expertise in prefab assembly, precision foundation work, and modular MEP connections. Selecting the right team is critical because on-site assembly windows are compressed to days rather than months, leaving little room for error.

## Key Roles in a Modular Home Project

| Role | Responsibility | When Needed | Typical Duration |
|------|---------------|-------------|-----------------|
| General contractor / project manager | Overall coordination, scheduling, permits | Entire project | 4-8 months |
| Foundation specialist | Ground survey, excavation, foundation install | Site prep phase | 1-3 weeks |
| Crane operator | Module lifting and positioning | Assembly day(s) | 1-3 days |
| MEP installer (plumber + electrician + HVAC) | Utility hookup, inter-module connections | Post-assembly | 1-2 weeks |
| Interior finisher | Flooring, painting, kitchen/bathroom fit-out | Finishing phase | 2-4 weeks |
| Landscaper / exterior works | Driveway, terrace, garden, fencing | After assembly | 1-2 weeks |

## Contractor Qualification Checklist

Before engaging any contractor, verify:

- [ ] Valid business registration and trade license
- [ ] Professional liability insurance (minimum coverage varies by country)
- [ ] Relevant experience with prefab/modular projects (at least 3 completed)
- [ ] References from previous modular home clients
- [ ] Familiarity with the specific module system (connection types, tolerances)
- [ ] Adequate equipment (or confirmed rental agreements for crane, tools)
- [ ] Written warranty terms (minimum 2 years workmanship, 10 years structural)
- [ ] Clear payment schedule tied to milestones, not calendar dates
- [ ] Subcontractor management plan (if using sub-trades)

## Insurance Requirements by Market

| Requirement | Romania | Netherlands | EU General |
|-------------|---------|-------------|------------|
| Professional liability | Required (OAR for architects) | Beroepsaansprakelijkheid required | Varies by country |
| Construction all-risk (CAR) | Recommended, not mandatory | Commonly required by lenders | Standard for large projects |
| Decennial liability (10-year) | Not mandatory but emerging | Covered under Kwaliteitsborging | Required in France (Dommage-Ouvrage) |
| Worker compensation | CAS (social insurance) contributions mandatory | UWV workplace insurance mandatory | EU Framework Directive 89/391 |
| Minimum coverage amount | ~EUR 50,000 typical | EUR 500,000-1,000,000 typical | Varies widely |

## Red Flags to Watch For

- **No written contract** -- always insist on a formal agreement before any work begins
- **Full payment upfront** -- standard practice is 10-20% deposit, milestones, 5-10% retention
- **No insurance documentation** -- request certificates, not just verbal claims
- **Unrealistically low quotes** -- below-market pricing often signals corner-cutting or hidden extras
- **No modular experience** -- traditional builders may underestimate precision requirements
- **Resistance to inspections** -- quality contractors welcome third-party checks
- **Vague timeline** -- demand a phased schedule with specific milestone dates
- **No warranty in writing** -- verbal promises are unenforceable

## Cost Structure Breakdown

Understanding how contractor pricing works helps evaluate quotes:

| Component | % of Total | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| Materials | 40-50% | Modules, foundation materials, finishing supplies |
| Labor | 30-40% | On-site crew, crane, MEP specialists |
| Overhead | 10-15% | Insurance, equipment, transport, site facilities |
| Profit margin | 5-10% | Standard contractor margin |

**Tip:** Request itemized quotes separating materials, labor, and overhead. Lump-sum quotes make it harder to compare contractors or negotiate.

## Payment Schedule Best Practice

| Milestone | Payment % | Trigger |
|-----------|----------|---------|
| Contract signing | 10% | Signed agreement |
| Foundation complete | 15% | Foundation inspection passed |
| Modules delivered to site | 25% | Delivery confirmed |
| Assembly + weathertight | 20% | Structural inspection passed |
| MEP + finishing complete | 20% | Final inspection passed |
| Handover + snagging | 10% | Defects list resolved |

Retain 5-10% for 30-90 days after handover to cover any snagging items discovered after move-in.
