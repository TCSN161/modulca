# Site Preparation for Modular Home Installation

## Overview

Site preparation for modular homes is simpler and faster than for traditional construction, but demands careful planning around delivery logistics and crane access. Because the on-site assembly window is measured in days, all groundwork must be 100% complete before modules arrive. A poorly prepared site can delay assembly, damage modules, or increase costs significantly.

## Phase 1: Ground Survey and Soil Testing

### Topographic Survey
- Commission a licensed surveyor to produce a site plan with contour lines, boundaries, and existing features
- Identify: slope gradient, drainage direction, existing trees, overhead power lines, neighboring structures
- Establish building setback lines per local zoning (typically 3-5m from property boundary in Romania, varies in NL)

### Geotechnical Investigation
- Minimum requirement: 2-3 boreholes or CPT (Cone Penetration Test) probes to 3-6m depth
- Determines: soil bearing capacity, groundwater level, frost depth, settlement risk
- Romania: frost depth 0.8-1.1m depending on climate zone (NP 112/2014)
- Netherlands: high groundwater and soft soils (clay/peat) are common; pile foundations often required
- Cost: EUR 500-1,500 for a typical residential plot
- Duration: 1-2 days fieldwork + 1 week for laboratory report

### Key Soil Parameters

| Parameter | Acceptable Range | Risk if Outside Range |
|-----------|-----------------|----------------------|
| Bearing capacity | >= 100 kPa (for strip/slab) | Differential settlement, cracking |
| Groundwater level | >= 1.0m below foundation | Waterproofing issues, buoyancy risk |
| Frost depth | Foundation must be below local frost line | Frost heave damage |
| Organic content | < 5% | Excessive settlement over time |
| Slope | < 15% preferred | Retaining walls needed, increased cost |

## Phase 2: Access Route Planning

Module delivery requires careful route planning from factory to site.

### Delivery Vehicle Requirements
- Standard module transport: flatbed truck (13.6m trailer length)
- ModulCA 3x3m modules: 3.0m wide x 3.0m long x 3.2m tall on truck
- Total vehicle length: ~16.5m, width: ~3.2m (with mirrors), height: ~4.5m loaded
- Gross vehicle weight: 20-30 tonnes depending on module count

### Site Access Requirements

| Requirement | Minimum Specification | Ideal |
|-------------|----------------------|-------|
| Access road width | 3.5m clear | 4.5m+ |
| Turning radius | 12m inner radius | 15m+ |
| Overhead clearance | 4.5m minimum | 5.0m+ |
| Ground bearing | 10 tonnes/axle | Hardstanding or gravel |
| Gradient | Max 12% (1:8) | Max 8% (1:12) |

**Common obstacles:** low bridges, narrow village roads, overhead power lines, tree branches, parked cars. Conduct a route survey 2-4 weeks before delivery and arrange any temporary measures (branch trimming, traffic management, utility line lifting).

## Phase 3: Crane Positioning

### Mobile Crane Requirements
- Typical crane size: 50-100 tonne capacity mobile crane
- Required reach: 15-25m depending on house footprint and access position
- Module weight: 2-5 tonnes per 3x3m module (depending on specification)
- Crane pad area: minimum 8m x 8m level hardstanding
- Ground bearing for crane outriggers: minimum 100 kPa (may need steel plates or timber mats)

### Crane Setup Checklist
- [ ] Confirm crane position allows reach to all module placement locations
- [ ] Verify no overhead power lines within crane swing radius (minimum 6m clearance)
- [ ] Prepare hardstanding or mats for outrigger pads
- [ ] Arrange municipal permit for crane on public road (if needed)
- [ ] Confirm lifting plan signed by crane operator and site supervisor

## Phase 4: Foundation Installation

ModulCA modules are compatible with several foundation types. Selection depends on soil conditions, budget, and timeline.

| Foundation Type | Duration | Cost (EUR/m2) | Best For | Soil Requirement |
|----------------|----------|---------------|----------|-----------------|
| Screw piles | 1-2 days | 30-60 | Fast install, removable, low impact | Most soils except rock |
| Strip foundation | 2-3 weeks | 50-80 | Traditional, widely understood | Bearing capacity >= 100 kPa |
| Concrete slab (raft) | 1-2 weeks | 70-100 | Poor soils, high loads, basements | Any (distributes load) |
| Driven piles + beams | 1-2 weeks | 80-150 | Soft soils (NL peat/clay) | Required when bearing layer is deep |

### Foundation Tolerances
- Level tolerance: max 5mm deviation across any 3m span
- Position tolerance: max 10mm from design coordinates
- These are critical -- modules are factory-built to exact dimensions and cannot accommodate large deviations

## Phase 5: Utility Connections

Arrange utility connections early, as lead times with utility companies can be 4-8 weeks.

| Utility | Typical Hookup Distance | Cost Range (Romania) | Cost Range (Netherlands) | Notes |
|---------|------------------------|---------------------|-------------------------|-------|
| Electricity | Plot boundary to meter box | EUR 200-800 | EUR 500-1,500 | 3-phase recommended for heat pumps |
| Water | Street main to plot | EUR 300-1,000 | EUR 800-2,000 | Include isolation valve at boundary |
| Sewage | Plot to street sewer | EUR 500-2,000 | EUR 1,000-3,000 | Gravity or pumped, check invert levels |
| Gas | Street main to meter | EUR 300-800 | Increasingly unavailable (aardgasvrij) | NL phasing out gas in new builds |
| Telecom/fiber | Street to building | EUR 100-300 | Often included by provider | Check ISP coverage before purchase |

**Off-grid note:** For rural plots without sewer access, a septic system or bio-treatment plant may be required (EUR 3,000-8,000 in Romania).

## Phase 6: Temporary Site Facilities

During the assembly period (typically 1-2 weeks), the site needs:

- Temporary electricity supply (generator or temporary grid connection, min 32A)
- Portable toilet for construction crew
- Secure storage container for tools and materials
- Site fencing / hoarding if near public areas
- Skip or waste container for packaging and construction waste
- Parking for 4-6 vehicles (crew + delivery)

## ModulCA Application

ModulCA provides a site preparation checklist integrated into the project timeline. After the homeowner inputs their plot address, the platform generates a preliminary site assessment highlighting likely soil conditions (based on geological maps), access constraints (based on road network data), and recommended foundation type. The delivery logistics team plans the transport route and crane position as part of the standard project management workflow. All site readiness criteria must be confirmed before modules are dispatched from the factory.
