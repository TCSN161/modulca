# Financing Modular Construction in Romania

## Overview

Modular homes in Romania can be financed through several channels. While the banking sector is still adapting to prefabricated construction, financing options have expanded significantly since 2023.

## Financing Options

### 1. Bank Mortgage (Credit Ipotecar)

**Standard mortgages** from Romanian banks can be used for modular construction:

| Bank | Product | LTV | Rate (2025) | Notes |
|------|---------|-----|-------------|-------|
| BCR | Credit Ipotecar | 85% | IRCC + 2-3% | Requires building permit |
| BRD | Credit Imobiliar | 80% | IRCC + 2.5% | Accepts modular if permanent foundation |
| Banca Transilvania | BT Locuinta | 85% | IRCC + 2% | Case-by-case for modular |
| ING | Credit Ipotecar | 80% | IRCC + 2.2% | Requires certified builder |

**Key requirement**: Banks require the building to be on a permanent foundation (not mobile/temporary). ModulCA's screw pile and strip foundation options qualify.

### 2. Noua Casa (formerly Prima Casa)

The government-backed mortgage guarantee program:
- **Guarantee**: Up to 50% of loan value (max €70,000)
- **Down payment**: As low as 5%
- **Interest cap**: IRCC + max 2%
- **Eligibility**: First-time homebuyers, Romanian residents
- **Modular eligible**: Yes, if built on permanent foundation with building permit

### 3. Construction Loan (Credit pentru Constructii)

For building on your own land:
- **Disbursement**: In stages (foundation, structure, finishing)
- **Term**: 12-24 months construction + conversion to mortgage
- **Advantage for modular**: Faster construction means fewer disbursement stages
- **Documents needed**: Building permit, construction contract, site plan

### 4. Developer Pre-Financing

Some modular construction companies offer:
- **30/40/30 payment schedule**: 30% at order, 40% at delivery, 30% at completion
- **Factory financing**: Company covers factory costs, customer pays on delivery
- **Lease-to-own**: Monthly payments over 3-5 years (emerging model)

## Cost Comparison: Modular vs. Traditional

| Metric | Traditional | Modular | Savings |
|--------|------------|---------|---------|
| Construction time | 12-18 months | 3-6 months | 50-70% |
| Interest during construction | €3,000-8,000 | €800-2,000 | 60-75% |
| Cost overruns (average) | 15-25% | 3-8% | Predictable |
| Total financing cost | Higher | Lower | 10-20% |

## Insurance Requirements

Banks require:
1. **Construction insurance** (asigurare constructii) — during build phase
2. **Home insurance** (asigurare locuinta) — PAD mandatory, PADI recommended
3. **Structural warranty** — minimum 10 years from builder

Modular construction qualifies for standard home insurance. PAD (mandatory earthquake/flood insurance) costs the same as traditional buildings.

## Tips for Modular Home Buyers

1. **Get the building permit first** — banks won't lend without it
2. **Choose a certified builder** — banks prefer ISC-registered contractors
3. **Permanent foundation** — critical for mortgage eligibility
4. **Detailed cost breakdown** — banks want itemized quotes, not lump sums
5. **Energy certificate** — new builds require energy performance certificate
