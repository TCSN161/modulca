# Construction Timeline for Modular Homes

## Overview

Modular construction compresses total project time by 40-60% compared to traditional building. The key advantage is parallelization: factory production runs concurrently with site preparation and foundation work. A typical ModulCA home (6-12 modules, 54-108 m2) reaches move-in readiness in 4-8 months versus 12-18 months for an equivalent traditional build.

## Traditional vs Modular Timeline Comparison

| Phase | Traditional Build | Modular Build | Time Saved |
|-------|-------------------|---------------|------------|
| Design + engineering | 4-12 weeks | 2-4 weeks | 50-70% |
| Permitting | 2-6 months | 2-6 months | 0% (same process) |
| Site preparation | 2-4 weeks | 1-2 weeks | 50% |
| Structure + envelope | 3-6 months | 4-8 weeks (factory) | 60-80% |
| MEP rough-in | 4-8 weeks | Included in factory | 100% on-site |
| Interior finishing | 4-8 weeks | 2-4 weeks | 50% |
| Final inspections | 2-4 weeks | 1-2 weeks | 50% |
| **Total** | **12-18 months** | **4-8 months** | **40-60%** |

## Detailed Phase Breakdown (Gantt-Style)

| Phase | Duration | Predecessors | Notes |
|-------|----------|-------------|-------|
| 1. Design + configuration | 2-4 weeks | -- | ModulCA configurator accelerates this |
| 2. Engineering + documentation | 2-4 weeks | Phase 1 | Structural calcs, MEP design |
| 3. Permit application | 2-6 months | Phase 2 | Country-dependent (see below) |
| 4. Factory production | 4-8 weeks | Phase 3 start* | *Can overlap with permitting in some markets |
| 5. Site preparation + foundation | 1-2 weeks | Phase 3 complete | Runs parallel to Phase 4 |
| 6. Module delivery | 1-2 days | Phase 4 + 5 | Flatbed truck, route planning required |
| 7. On-site assembly | 2-5 days | Phase 6 | Crane + 4-6 person crew |
| 8. Module connections + sealing | 3-5 days | Phase 7 | Structural, weather, fire barriers |
| 9. MEP connections + testing | 1-2 weeks | Phase 8 | Water, electrical, HVAC hookup |
| 10. Interior finishing | 2-4 weeks | Phase 9 | Floors, paint, fixtures, kitchen |
| 11. External works | 1-2 weeks | Phase 7 | Landscaping, driveway, fencing |
| 12. Final inspection + handover | 1-2 weeks | Phase 10 + 11 | Snagging, certificates, keys |

**Critical path:** Permitting is almost always the longest single phase and the primary bottleneck.

## Permit Timeline by Country

| Country | Typical Permit Duration | Authority | Notes |
|---------|------------------------|-----------|-------|
| Romania | 2-4 months | Local municipality + ISC | CU (Certificat de Urbanism) + AC (Autorizatie de Construire) |
| Netherlands | 3-6 months | Municipality (Omgevingsloket) | Omgevingsvergunning; new Omgevingswet since 2024 |
| Germany | 3-6 months | Bauamt (local building office) | Baugenehmigung; varies greatly by Bundesland |
| France | 2-4 months | Mairie (municipality) | Permis de construire; 2 months standard, 3 if near monuments |
| Belgium | 2-6 months | Region-dependent | Stedenbouwkundige vergunning (Flanders) or Permis d'urbanisme (Wallonia) |
| Spain | 3-8 months | Ayuntamiento (municipality) | Licencia de obra mayor; highly variable by region |
| Italy | 3-6 months | Comune (municipality) | Permesso di costruire; Silenzio-assenso after 90 days in theory |

## Key Schedule Risks

- **Permit delays:** Allow 50% buffer on estimated permit timelines
- **Weather:** Foundation work and on-site assembly are weather-sensitive; plan assembly for dry periods
- **Transport access:** Module delivery requires route surveys 2-4 weeks in advance
- **Utility connections:** Coordination with utility companies can add 2-6 weeks if not started early
- **Factory capacity:** Peak season (March-September) may extend production lead times
