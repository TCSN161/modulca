# Light in Architecture

## Overview
"Architecture is the masterly, correct and magnificent play of masses brought together in light." — Le Corbusier. Light is not merely a practical requirement — it is the primary material of architecture, defining space, mood, and experience.

## Types of Architectural Light

### Natural Light
| Type | Character | How to Achieve |
|------|-----------|----------------|
| **Top light** | Even, dramatic, sacred | Skylights, clerestory, lanterns |
| **Side light** | Directional, creates depth | Standard windows |
| **Reflected light** | Soft, diffuse | Light walls, light shelves, courtyards |
| **Filtered light** | Atmospheric, dappled | Screens, perforated panels, trees |

### Light Qualities

| Quality | Warm Tone (2700K) | Cool Tone (5000K) |
|---------|-------------------|-------------------|
| Feeling | Cozy, intimate | Alert, productive |
| Best for | Bedrooms, living rooms | Offices, kitchens |
| Time of day | Evening | Daytime |
| Direction | Low, indirect | Overhead, direct |

## Light and Space

| Technique | Effect | Example |
|-----------|--------|---------|
| Bright ceiling, dark walls | Room feels taller | White ceiling, colored walls |
| Bright walls, dark floor | Room feels wider | Light paint, dark timber floor |
| Light at end of corridor | Draws movement | Window or lamp at terminus |
| Wash of light on texture | Reveals material quality | Light grazing stone/timber wall |
| Shadow as element | Creates depth and drama | Deep reveals, overhangs |

## Masters of Light
- **Louis Kahn**: "A room is not a room without natural light"
- **Tadao Ando**: Concrete and light as primary materials (Church of the Light)
- **Peter Zumthor**: Thermal baths at Vals — light through slots in stone
- **Alvar Aalto**: Skylights and indirect light in libraries and churches
- **Kengo Kuma**: Filtered light through timber screens

## ModulCA Application
- Each module should have at least one window (1/8 floor area for natural light)
- Corner modules can have windows on two walls — maximizing daylight
- Interior modules (surrounded by others) need skylights or borrowed light
- Module flat roofs accommodate rooflights for top-lit spaces
- Courtyard configurations create light wells for central modules
