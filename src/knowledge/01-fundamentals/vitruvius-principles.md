# Vitruvius — The Foundations of Architecture

## Overview
Marcus Vitruvius Pollio (c. 80-15 BC) wrote **De Architectura** (Ten Books on Architecture), the oldest surviving treatise on architecture. His work established principles that remain foundational to this day.

## The Three Vitruvian Principles

Every good building must possess three qualities:

### 1. Firmitas (Structural Soundness)
- **Durability** — the building must stand up robustly and remain in good condition
- Materials must be appropriate for their purpose
- Structure must resist loads: gravity, wind, seismic, snow
- **ModulCA connection**: CLT and steel frame modules are engineered to EN 1995/1993 with 50-year design life

### 2. Utilitas (Utility / Function)
- **Usefulness** — the building must be suitable for the purposes it is used for
- Rooms should be arranged for convenient use
- Circulation must be logical and efficient
- Spaces must serve their occupants' needs
- **ModulCA connection**: The 3×3m module system enables functional room layouts based on Neufert standards

### 3. Venustas (Beauty / Delight)
- **Aesthetics** — the building must be visually pleasing
- Proportions should be harmonious
- Ornament should be appropriate, not excessive
- The building should delight those who experience it
- **ModulCA connection**: Style customization through cladding, color, and form — beauty is not sacrificed for modularity

## The Ten Books

| Book | Subject | Key Topics |
|------|---------|------------|
| I | The education of the architect | Breadth of knowledge required |
| II | Building materials | Brick, sand, lime, stone, timber |
| III | Temples (proportions) | Ionic order, symmetry, proportion |
| IV | Temples (continued) | Doric, Corinthian orders |
| V | Public buildings | Forums, basilicas, theatres, baths |
| VI | Domestic buildings | Climate adaptation, room planning |
| VII | Interior finishes | Stucco, painting, colors |
| VIII | Water | Aqueducts, wells, cisterns |
| IX | Astronomy & sundials | Timekeeping, orientation |
| X | Machines | Construction equipment, military engines |

## The Vitruvian Man
Vitruvius described the human body as the model of natural proportion:
- A man's height equals his arm span (square)
- Navel is the center of a circle drawn around the body
- Leonardo da Vinci's famous drawing (c. 1490) illustrates these proportions
- This connects to Le Corbusier's Modulor — human proportion as the basis of architectural scale

## Why It Matters Today
Vitruvius established that architecture is not just building — it requires knowledge of:
- Engineering (firmitas)
- Social science (utilitas)
- Art (venustas)
- Plus: history, philosophy, music, medicine, law, astronomy

This holistic view is the foundation of architectural education worldwide.
